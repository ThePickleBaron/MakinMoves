## DISCOVERY CALL FRAMEWORK (For Jason/COO on Zoom)

**Duration:** 15 minutes
**Goal:** Understand their situation + determine if they're a good fit

### CALL OUTLINE

**MINUTE 0-2: Warmup**
"Thanks for booking. How's your day going? Busy week?"
[Small talk, build rapport]

**MINUTE 2-5: Situation**
"Tell me about [Company]. What do you do?"
[Listen. Take notes on: industry, stage, team size, main product]

**MINUTE 5-10: Problem**
"You mentioned content is a bottleneck. What does that look like for you right now?"
[Listen for: how many articles/month, who writes, what's broken, why]

**MINUTE 10-13: Vision**
"Imagine in 3 months, what would content success look like? What would change?"
[Listen for: inbound leads, traffic, brand positioning, team bandwidth]

**MINUTE 13-14: Fit Check**
"Here's what I'm thinking: [Your recommendation based on call]

Does this sound like something that could work for you?"
[Gauge: yes, maybe, no]

**MINUTE 14-15: Next Steps**
If YES: "Great. Let's get you started. [Book retainer process]"
If MAYBE: "No pressure. Think about it. I'll send you a recap email with a proposal."
If NO: "No worries. I'll refer you to someone who might be better fit."

## LANDING PAGE ANALYTICS (Track These)

**Daily Metrics:**
- Pageviews
- Signup rate (% who fill intake form)
- Conversion to audit booking (% who book Calendly)
- Cost per lead (if using ads)

**Weekly Metrics:**
- Cost per qualified lead (booked call)
- Cost per customer (booked retainer)

**Guardrail Rules:**
- If signup rate <10%: Landing page copy is weak, rewrite headline
- If booking rate <30% of signups: Intake form has friction, simplify
- If close rate <30% of calls: Audit call is weak, improve discovery questions
