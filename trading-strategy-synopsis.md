## CORE MACRO THESIS

Jason operates from a deeply held conviction that the 2026 Strait of Hormuz closure represents the beginning of a bifurcated global economic crisis. His framework:

**Oil-producing nations (US, Canada, Norway, Brazil)** will experience recession — painful but survivable due to domestic energy security, food production capacity, and fiscal resources to deploy stimulus. The US specifically benefits from shale self-sufficiency and agricultural output.

**Non-oil-producing nations (most of Europe, Japan, South Korea, India, Sub-Saharan Africa, Southeast Asia)** will experience depression — energy import costs consume national reserves, fertilizer shortages threaten food security within one growing season, currencies weaken against the dollar, and GDP contracts 10%+.

**China occupies a unique middle position** — largest oil importer but has strategic petroleum reserves, domestic production, a state-directed economy that can force-stimulus without legislative gridlock, and a manufacturing base the world still depends on. Jason expects China to enter recession but recover faster than Western democracies because the CCP will deploy liquidity to maintain social stability.

Jason explicitly stated he does not trust the market, the current administration, the Federal Reserve, or institutional guidance. He sees a global depression emerging from the oil and fertilizer impact of the Hormuz closure. His concern extends to famine in import-dependent nations — he notes that while the US has infrastructure and resources, that does not make markets flush. He questions where AI datacenter funding goes if credit contracts, whether private equity collapses, and whether real estate follows.

His conclusion: **metals, farm and heavy equipment, resources, and energy are the only tangible things that maintain or grow value if his scenario plays out.**

## TRADING STYLE & METHODOLOGY

**Quick partial profit-taking with defined exits.** Jason does not hold positions hoping they go up. He enters with a thesis, sets OCO orders (one-cancels-other: a stop loss and a limit sell), and lets the system execute. When he says he has "takes and OCO stops on everything," he means it literally — his order book at any given time has 20+ working orders across the portfolio.

**Scalping as a cash register.** His primary profit engine during this period was LITE (Lumentum Holdings). He bought 25 shares, then systematically sold in small batches at 2% gain increments. Over the course of a few weeks, he trimmed from 25 shares down to 4, realizing $2,769 in gains — more than the current market value of his remaining position. The remaining 4 shares are effectively free — pure house money. He described this explicitly: "I am taking from Lumentum starting at 2% gains with a single sale."

**Tiered OCO exits.** When Jason entered GNK (Genco Shipping, 195 shares at $23.32), he immediately set up three OCO tranches:
- 50 shares: stop $22.51 / target $25.50
- 45 shares: stop $22.51 / target $26.00
- 100 shares: stop $22.51 / target $28.00

All three tranches share the same stop ($22.51, about 5.5% below entry) but have staggered profit targets. The first two tranches, if filled, generate enough profit to cover the stop loss on the third — effectively making the 100-share swing trade risk-free. This is his standard approach for new positions.

**Momentum plays with tight leashes.** SATS (EchoStar) was entered purely on a golden cross signal with a 10% stop loss and a plan to hold no more than 1-2 weeks. FUN (Cedar Fair/Six Flags) was identified the same way — golden cross on 3/24, bouncing, with a plan to catch a peak before the recession thesis plays out and discretionary spending dies. Jason explicitly called these out as short-term momentum plays, distinct from his conviction holds.

**Conviction holds with active trading layers.** FJET (Starfighters Space) is held as a year-long bet on a defense contract, but Jason actively trades around the core position — selling spikes and buying dips to lower his effective cost basis while maintaining exposure for the catalyst event. He described this as: "I think there will be a big contract this year. So now that I'm seeing a normalized price I will probably sell spikes and buy dips all year with a small cache just sitting."

**Extended hours trading.** Jason trades extended hours specifically to avoid gap risk. He noted this as a deliberate strategy to catch moves that would otherwise jump his stops at market open.

## RISK MANAGEMENT PHILOSOPHY

Jason's approach to risk is layered and explicit:

1. **OCO stops on everything.** Every position in the individual account has a defined exit — both a stop loss and a take profit. No position is left without a plan.

2. **Gold as the floor.** Precious metals positions exist specifically to protect the down payment principal. If everything else drops, gold holds.

3. **"OK getting stuck at half."** Jason's position selection filter: he only buys things with fundamentals strong enough to justify holding through a 50% drawdown. This naturally filters out garbage and overvalued names.

4. **Tiered exits.** Rather than all-or-nothing, Jason takes profits in tranches. First tranche at a small gain (covers the stop loss on remaining shares), second tranche at a moderate gain, final tranche at the target. This means he can be wrong on timing but still profit.

5. **Extended hours trading.** Specifically used to avoid gap risk on overnight moves.

6. **Account separation.** Active trading and leverage in the individual account. Long-term compounding in the IRA. Growth stocks in the Roth (tax-free gains). Each account has a distinct purpose and risk tolerance.

7. **Margin awareness.** Jason acknowledges the margin is temporary and actively manages it down by trimming positions. He went from -$16.5K to -$9.3K in margin during a single session by trimming AP, IAUM, SLV, TFPM, and FN.
