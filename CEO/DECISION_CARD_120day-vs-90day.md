**For:** Board quick decision making
**Format:** 1-page reference during tomorrow's launch

---

## THE QUESTION

**Is Jun 30 (90 days) a realistic recovery target, or should we shift to Aug 31 (120 days)?**

---

## THE DATA

| Evidence | 90-Day Says | 120-Day Says | Winner |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Industry bootstrap timelines** | Compressed | Matches industry avg (4-5 mo) | 120-Day |
| **Slog phase duration** | 4 weeks, absorbs easily | 6-8 weeks for recovery founder | 120-Day |
| **Revenue lag** | Day 92 effort = Day 92 revenue | Revenue arrives 4-6 wks after effort | 120-Day |
| **Founder burnout risk** | 65% by Day 90 | 35% with 30-day recovery window | 120-Day |
| **Decision gate data** | 50% of signals available | 85-90% of signals available | 120-Day |
| **CFO financial model** | Assumes Jun 30 | Explicitly forecasts "late July-Aug" | 120-Day |
| **Execution risk analysis** | Calls out 60% failure rate | Mitigations require buffer time | 120-Day |

**Score: 120-Day wins 7-0 on data.**

---

## THE CHOICE

### Option A: KEEP 90-DAY TARGET (Jun 30)
**If board insists on original timeline:**

**Risks:**
- 65% founder burnout probability during Gate 2-3 (May 15-Jun 30)
- Quality collapse in Week 4-6 (May 1-15)
- Revenue doesn't actually arrive until Aug anyway (per CFO model)
- Decision gates made on incomplete market signals
- If P1/P2 underperforms, recovery pushed to Sep (90 days becomes 120+ anyway)

**Upsides:**
- Maintains psychological "Q2 close" narrative
- Forces urgency

**Verdict:** Hits psychological deadline, misses actual revenue deadline. Worse case: founder burns out AND timeline slips.

---

### Option B: RESET TO 120-DAY TARGET (Aug 31)  ← RECOMMENDED
**Shift primary target, keep Jun 30 as upside:**

**Benefits:**
- Founder sees revenue growth trajectory before breakdown
- 30-day slog-phase buffer prevents quality collapse
- Decision gates based on 85% of market data
- All 3 streams mature simultaneously (P3 affiliate finally generating revenue)
- Same revenue (CFO model already shows Aug 31), just planned instead of unplanned

**Risks:**
- Loses "90-day sprint" framing
- Requires messaging: "120-day sustainable recovery" (not sprint)
- Jun 30 misses by design (need to explain to stakeholders)

**Verdict:** Trades psychological deadline for actual revenue deadline + founder health.

---

## THE RECOMMENDATION

**✅ APPROVE OPTION B: 120-Day Target (Aug 31)**

**Reasoning:**
1. CFO already forecasted Aug 31 anyway
2. Execution risk analysis identified burnout as #2 risk (65%)
3. All 5 dimensions of investigation point to 120 days
4. Cost: $0 (timeline shift only)
5. Gain: Founder sustainability + execution quality

## IF SOMEONE QUESTIONS THE DECISION

**"Why extend to 120 days if we're pushing so hard for revenue?"**
- Because timeline shift doesn't delay revenue; it absorbs execution risk. CFO shows Aug 31 anyway.

**"Doesn't 120 days reduce founder motivation?"**
- No. 90-day target with 65% burnout probability kills motivation harder. 120 days with clear milestones maintains it.

**"Can't we just push harder for 90 days?"**
- Execution risk analysis shows 26 hrs/week required, Jason can do 20 hrs/week. Gap = burnout. No amount of "pushing" fixes math.

**"What about the $100K burn rate?"**
- Not changing. Revenue-focused streams. Burn is only $41/mo fixed costs anyway. 30 extra days adds <$30 cost.
