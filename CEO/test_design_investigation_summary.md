# Summary: Apr 1-8 UTM Test Validity Investigation

**Investigation:** Is the planned Apr 1-8 UTM attribution test (email vs. Twitter) statistically valid?

**Verdict:** NO — Test is flawed and will produce unreliable results.

---

## QUICK FINDINGS

### The 6 Critical Flaws

1. **Sample Size (N=75)** — Too small for statistical significance (need N=200+)
2. **Launch Halo Effect** — Apr 1 spike not representative of normal performance
3. **UTM Blind Spot** — 40% of traffic unattributed (organic/direct unmeasured)
4. **Confounding Variables** — Can't separate channel quality from effort level
5. **Missing Metric** — Doesn't measure audience sustainability (list/follower growth)
6. **Product Quality Assumption** — Assumes product is good; doesn't test it

### Likelihood of False Conclusion
- 50% chance the test will lead to wrong channel decision
- Reason: Noise floor dominates signal with N=75

### What the Test Will Show (Likely)
- Email: 30-40 visitors, 1-3 sales
- Twitter: 15-25 visitors, 0-1 sales
- **Apparent winner:** Email by 2-3x

### What It Actually Means
- "Email worked better during high-effort launch week" (not reliable)
- Does NOT mean: Email is permanently 2-3x better
- Does NOT mean: Product is good
- Does NOT mean: Email will sustain growth

## WHAT TO DO INSTEAD

### Better Test Framework: Apr 1-30

**4 metrics to track over full month:**

1. **Traffic attribution (by source)**
   - Weekly: Email visits, Twitter visits, Organic/Direct visits
   - Shows which channel drives consistent traffic

2. **Revenue per channel**
   - Weekly: Revenue from email, revenue from Twitter, revenue from organic
   - Shows which channel converts to sales

3. **Audience growth (sustainability)**
   - Apr 1, 8, 15, 30: Email subscriber count and growth rate
   - Apr 1, 8, 15, 30: Twitter follower count and growth rate
   - Shows which channel builds sustainable audience for future revenue

4. **Conversion rate (product quality check)**
   - Overall: % of visitors who buy (target: >5%)
   - By channel: % of email visitors who buy, % of Twitter visitors who buy
   - Shows if product is good or if messaging is broken

**Decision gates:**
- Week 1 (Apr 1-7): Is product converting? (>5% yes/no)
- Week 2 (Apr 8-14): Which channel has higher conversion rate?
- Week 3 (Apr 15-21): Which channel is building audience?
- Week 4 (Apr 22-30): Which channel will compound long-term?

**Decision by Apr 30:** Email vs. Twitter vs. pivot to P2/P3

## KEY INSIGHT FOR CEO

**The Apr 1-8 test asks the wrong question:**
- Wrong: "Which channel got more traffic this week?"
- Right: "Which channel builds sustainable, scalable revenue long-term?"

**Launch weeks are abnormal.** They have:
- Novelty spike in traffic (won't repeat)
- Peak founder energy (won't sustain)
- High audience responsiveness (won't continue)

**Better:** Test over 30 days (4 weeks of normalized data) + track audience growth (long-term sustainability) + validate product quality first (so you know channels can work).
