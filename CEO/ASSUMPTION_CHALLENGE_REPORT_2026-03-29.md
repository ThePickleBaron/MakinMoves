- Average content retainer: $1,000/month (CEO's range: $600-2K is appropriate)

- 64% of SEO agencies charge <$1,000/month retainers

- Senior SEO specialist: 2-3 articles/week (2K+ word, optimized)
- Mid-level copywriter: 3-4 articles/week
- Junior/AI-assisted: 5+ articles/week (lower quality)

**Customer load analysis:**
- 5 customers × average 1-2 pieces/month per customer = 5-10 pieces/month = 1.2-2.5 pieces/week

- **Add project management, revisions, invoicing:** 12-18 hours/week

**Reality:** One writer handling 5 customers is feasible BUT:
- Only if customers accept slow turnaround (10-15 day cycles, not 3-5 day)
- Only if revision requests are minimal (<2 rounds)
- Only if no custom research required
- **Burnout risk: HIGH** (55-65 hour-equivalent weeks)

**CEO's contingency:** Hire backup writer by Month 4 (+$1.5K-2K cost, compressing margin 60% → 45%)

**This is realistic but understates the problem.** Better to hire at Month 3 (before burnout) or cap customers at 3-4.

**If CEO assumes word-of-mouth:** CAC ≈ $50-100 per customer (effort-based)
**At $1,500 MRR customer lifetime value:** Payback in 4-6 months (acceptable)

**But:** Month 1-2 are pure acquisition cost with zero revenue. This explains why Month 3-4 is realistic first customer.

### Hidden Assumption: Scope Creep and Revision Cycles
