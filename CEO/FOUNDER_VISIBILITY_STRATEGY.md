## SECTION 4: DAILY CONTENT TEMPLATE (HOW TO POST QUICKLY)

### The 5-Minute Twitter Framework

**Goal:** Consistent posting without burnout. All posts fit one of these templates:

#### **1. Real-Time Update** (1 min to write)
```
[Metric] [Change] [Why]
Example: "Made $23 today (11 sales). Template price change drove conversion up 40%."
```

#### **2. Behind-the-Scenes** (2 min to write)
```
What I did: [Action]
Result: [Outcome]
Lesson: [Insight]
Example: "Built intake form for freelance clients (3 hours). Only 2 submissions. Learned: my CTAs aren't clear enough."
```

#### **3. Lesson / Advice** (2 min to write)
```
Mistake: [What everyone does]
Fix: [What I do instead]
Why: [Why it works]
Example: "Mistake: Pricing based on time. Fix: Pricing based on value. Why: Clients care about results, not hours."
```

#### **4. Question / Poll** (1 min to write)
```
Question: [Ask followers]
Context: [Why you're asking]
Example: "Should I charge $30 or $50 for the course template? The $50 option has more content."
```

#### **5. Retweet + Personal Take** (1 min to write)
```
Retweet [relevant post]
Add your comment: [Your angle]
Example: [Retweet about bootstrapping] "This. 90% of founders I meet are waiting for permission. Just ship."
```

### Batching Strategy (Save 5+ Hours/Week)

**Sunday evening (30 min):**
1. Plan week's themes
2. Write 5 core tweets (Monday–Friday)
3. Prepare 2-3 backup tweets (if needed)
4. Draft Substack outline

**Daily (5 min):**
1. Post 1 scheduled tweet (or live update if big news)
2. Spend 2 min replying to replies
3. Spend 2 min engaging with 2-3 followers

**Friday evening (30 min):**
1. Write + schedule Substack letter
2. Plan next week's themes

**Total time:** ~3.5 hours/week

## SECTION 10: COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

### ❌ Don't Do These

1. **Oversharing (too much personal life)**
   - ✓ Share: "Revenue update: $150 this week"
   - ✗ Share: "Sad day, my cat died, lost motivation"
   - Rule: Keep focus on business + recovery mission

2. **Ghosting followers**
   - ✓ Reply to DMs within 24 hours
   - ✓ Answer questions on your posts
   - ✗ Ignore engagement
   - Rule: Community = your best asset

3. **Inconsistent posting**
   - ✓ Post 1-3 times daily on Twitter
   - ✓ Weekly Substack email every Friday
   - ✗ Post 5 days, then disappear
   - Rule: Consistency beats perfection

4. **Fake metrics (lying about revenue/growth)**
   - ✓ "Made $47 this week"
   - ✗ "Made $1,000 this week" (when true is $200)
   - Rule: Credibility is everything. One lie kills it.

5. **Over-selling/hard pitching**
   - ✓ "We're running 4 revenue streams. Want to see how?"
   - ✗ "BUY NOW. Limited time offer. $100 VALUE FOR $29!!!"
   - Rule: Story > sales pitch

6. **Ignoring feedback**
   - ✓ "Product feedback: 3 people said X, changing it"
   - ✗ "No one gets my vision. Their loss."
   - Rule: Listen. Iterate. Be humble.

7. **Only posting links (no substance)**
   - ✓ Thread: "How I got first customer" + personal story
   - ✗ Post: "New template live. [link]" (no context)
   - Rule: Story > link
