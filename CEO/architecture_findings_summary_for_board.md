## Question Posed

## 6 Alternatives Investigated

### 1. Unified Platform (Kajabi/Teachable)
- **Pros:** Single email list, unified analytics, cross-sell
- **Cons:** $150–300/mo cost, 1–2 week delay, platform lock-in
- **Month 6 upside:** $1,320–2,600 (minimal vs. current)
- **Verdict:** Not worth the pivot. Revisit Month 4+.

### 2. Membership/Community
- **Pros:** Recurring revenue ($1,250/mo @ 50 members), community moat
- **Cons:** 5–10 hrs/week management, slow ramp (0 Month 1 → 50 Month 4), high execution risk
- **Month 6 upside:** $1,550–3,000 (if adoption works)
- **Verdict:** Better as Phase 2 (Month 4+). Too slow for initial launch.

### 3. Agency Model
- **Pros:** $3,000–5,000/mo by Month 6, recurring clients, high defensibility
- **Cons:** 8–12 week sales cycle = Month 3 first revenue, requires hiring, more active work
- **Month 6 upside:** $3,000–5,000 gross profit (best case)
- **Verdict:** STRONG Phase 2 option. Not optimal for initial launch (slow to revenue).

### 4. SaaS Tool
- **Pros:** Passive revenue, $10K achievable with 200 customers, defensible
- **Cons:** 2–4 week build time, no revenue until Month 2–3, product-market risk, ongoing tech debt
- **Month 6 upside:** $1,200–3,000 (if product-market fit works)
- **Verdict:** Too much upfront risk. Revisit Month 4+ with revenue to fund build.

### 5. Hyperspecialization (SaaS Niche)
- **Pros:** 2–3x higher conversion, premium pricing, easier positioning
- **Cons:** Smaller TAM, niche saturation risk, requires tight positioning
- **Month 6 upside:** $1,500–3,000 (if niche converts well)
- **Verdict:** EASY to test (Week 1, 2 hours). Low-risk experiment.

### 6. Email-First (Substack Building)
- **Pros:** Sponsor revenue, 10–15% conversion to warm list, owned audience
- **Cons:** $0 revenue Months 1–2, 2x/week writing, slow ramp to 1,000 subs
- **Month 6 upside:** $1,700–4,700 (if audience + sponsors acquired)
- **Verdict:** GOOD as Phase 1.5 parallel (not instead of 3-stream).
