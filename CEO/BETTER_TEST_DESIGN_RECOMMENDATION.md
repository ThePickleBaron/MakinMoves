# Better Test Design: What We Should Actually Measure

**Author:** CEO
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Purpose:** Move from invalid Apr 1-8 test to a reliable decision framework

---

## CORE INSIGHT

The Apr 1-8 test asks the wrong question:
- **Wrong question:** "Which channel got more traffic this week?"
- **Right question:** "Which channel builds sustainable, scalable revenue long-term?"

These are not the same thing.

---

## THE PROBLEM WITH Apr 1-8

Apr 1-8 is a **launch week snapshot**, not a performance baseline.

Launch weeks have abnormal:
- Traffic volume (novelty spike)
- Founder energy (peak motivation)
- Audience responsiveness (launch enthusiasm)

What you see Apr 1-8 is NOT what you'll see Apr 15-22 (normalized operations).

---

## WHAT WE SHOULD TEST INSTEAD

### Test 1: Product Quality Validation (Mar 30-Apr 1)

**Question:** "Does this product actually solve a real problem?"

**Method:**
- Interview 10-20 potential customers (your warm email list)
- Ask: "Would you buy this at $49/$99/$199?"
- Measure: What % say "yes, I'd buy"?

**Success gate:** >50% say yes → Green light to launch

**Timeline:** 2 days
**Cost:** 2 hours outreach
**Reliability:** 7/10 (good enough to launch with confidence)

**What you'll learn:**
- Is the problem real? (Do people care?)
- Is the price right? (Can they afford it?)
- Is the messaging clear? (Do they understand what they're buying?)

**What happens if <50% say yes:**
- Don't launch yet. Fix messaging or product. Then re-test.
- This saves you from launching a product nobody wants.

---

### Test 2: Channel Performance Baseline (Apr 1-30)

**Question:** "Which channel(s) will carry our revenue long-term?"

**Method:** Track 4 metrics over 30 days:

**Metric 1: Traffic attribution (by source)**
- Email UTM traffic (Apr 1-7, Apr 8-14, Apr 15-21, Apr 22-30)
- Twitter UTM traffic (same windows)
- Organic/Direct traffic (GA4, unattributed)
- **Chart:** Weekly trend (traffic per source)

**Metric 2: Revenue per channel**
- Revenue from email link clicks (calculate: % of revenue from email visitors)
- Revenue from Twitter link clicks (calculate: % of revenue from Twitter visitors)
- Revenue from organic/direct (calculate: remainder)
- **Chart:** Weekly cumulative revenue per channel

**Metric 3: Audience growth (sustainability)**
- Email subscribers on Apr 1, Apr 8, Apr 15, Apr 30 (growth trajectory)
- Twitter followers on Apr 1, Apr 8, Apr 15, Apr 30 (growth trajectory)
- **Chart:** Week-over-week subscriber/follower growth rate

**Metric 4: Conversion rate (product quality)**
- % of visitors who purchase (overall)
- % of email visitors who purchase (by channel)
- % of Twitter visitors who purchase (by channel)
- **Target:** >5% overall (if <3%, product messaging issue, not channel issue)

---

## DECISION FRAMEWORK: Apr 1-30 Results

### Week 1 (Apr 1-7): Launch Phase
**Check:** Is product converting? (Target: >5% conversion rate)
- ✅ Yes → Proceed to scale both channels
- ❌ No → Pause growth, fix product messaging, re-test

### Week 2 (Apr 8-14): Performance Baseline
**Check:** Which channel has higher conversion rate?
- Email: 8-12% conversion → Primary channel
- Twitter: 3-5% conversion → Secondary channel
- Both: 3-8% → Continue both, monitor audience growth

### Week 3 (Apr 15-21): Sustainability Check
**Check:** Which channel is building audience?
- Email list growing 5+/day → Email sustainable ✅
- Twitter followers growing 20+/day → Twitter sustainable ✅
- Neither growing → Channel strategy needs pivot (no audience retention)

### Week 4 (Apr 22-30): Long-Term Viability
**Check:** Which channel will compound revenue?
- Email: high conversion (8-12%), list growing → Prioritize email
- Twitter: lower conversion, big audience → Secondary but sustains
- Product: total revenue $100-500, clear trajectory → Viable to scale

## DECISION POINT: Apr 30

**Based on Apr 1-30 data, CEO makes final call:**

**Scenario A: Email wins (conversion > Twitter, list growing)**
- Decision: "Email is our primary revenue channel for P1"
- Action: Week 2 (May 1-7): Scale email list building + product copy iteration
- Budget: $500-1K on paid email list growth ads

**Scenario B: Twitter wins (followers growing, high engagement)**
- Decision: "Twitter is our primary reach channel for P1"
- Action: Week 2 (May 1-7): Scale Twitter audience + launch Twitter-native product (digital course?)
- Budget: $500-1K on Twitter ads/influencer partnerships

**Scenario C: Both channels underperform (conversion <3%, no audience growth)**
- Decision: "Product quality issue, not channel issue"
- Action: Week 2 (May 1-7): Pause growth, pivot to P2 or P3
- Budget: Reallocate to higher-potential revenue stream

## WHAT THIS TEST ANSWERS

✅ "Is the product actually good?" (conversion rate tells you)
✅ "Which channel has sustainable growth?" (list/follower growth tells you)
✅ "How much revenue will we make in Month 1?" (actual dollars tell you)
✅ "Should we scale email, Twitter, or both?" (metrics tell you)
✅ "Is P1 actually viable as a revenue stream?" (full month of data tells you)

---

## WHAT THIS TEST DOES NOT ANSWER

❌ "Which channel is 2x better?" (Need much larger sample, month-long baseline)
❌ "Which channel should we use forever?" (Depends on future audience growth, pricing changes, seasonal effects — not predictable from Apr 1-30)
❌ "What will revenue be in Month 6?" (Month 1 is not representative; compounding effects unknown)

## WHY THIS IS BETTER THAN Apr 1-8

| Dimension | Apr 1-8 Test | Apr 1-30 Test |
|---|---|---|
| Sample size | N=75 (too small) | N=500+ (valid) |
| Launch halo effect | Dominates results | Averaged out over 4 weeks |
| Effort variation | Confounds channels | Normalized over time |
| Audience sustainability | Not measured | Measured (list/follower growth) |
| Product quality check | Not tested | Measured (conversion rate) |
| Statistical confidence | 3/10 | 8/10 |
| Time cost | 1 week | 4 weeks (but continuous, not blocking) |

---

## WHAT CHANGES NOW (vs. Original Plan)

**Original plan:**
- Apr 1-8: Run UTM test
- Apr 9: Decide which channel
- Apr 10+: Scale winning channel

**New plan:**
- Mar 30-Apr 1: Validate product quality (optional, recommended)
- Apr 1-30: Run continuous channel performance test
- Apr 30: Decide which channel
- May 1+: Scale winning channel

**Change:** Decision moves from Apr 9 to Apr 30 (3 weeks later), but with much more reliable data.

**Trade-off:** 3 weeks of clarity vs. 3 weeks of maintaining both channels at balanced effort.

**Worth it?** Yes. Avoids costly channel decision mistakes.

---

## CONTINGENCY: If We Must Decide Apr 8

If board insists on Apr 8 decision (for some reason):

1. **Do not use Apr 1-8 data alone.** Wait until Apr 14.
2. **If forced to decide Apr 8:** Treat it as "directional only" (not final)
3. **Plan re-test:** Confirm Apr 1-8 decision with Apr 15-22 data
4. **Document:** "Apr 8 decision is preliminary; will confirm or reverse by Apr 22"

This protects against the N=75 sample size problem.
