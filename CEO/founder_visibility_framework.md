# Founder Visibility Framework (Jason Personal Brand)

**Strategy:** Build Jason as a thought leader in SaaS content, freelancing, and AI writing. This is the moat that defends all 3 revenue streams (P1, P2, P3).

**Execution timeline:** 8 weeks (Apr 1 - May 24)

---

## WHY FOUNDER VISIBILITY MATTERS

**Revenue stream dependence:**
- **P1 (Gumroad):** Products sell because Jason has audience (Twitter/Substack followers trust him)
- **P2 (Freelance Writing Services):** Clients hire Jason for content because he's a proven expert (not random freelancer)
- **P3 (Affiliate Sites):** His Substack + Twitter validation drives readers to affiliate sites (social proof)

**Without founder visibility:** All 3 streams are commodity (anyone could sell same products). Revenue ceiling = commodity prices ($1-5K/mo).

**With founder visibility:** Premium positioning, brand moat, 5-10x revenue potential (defensibility). Long-term: $50K+/mo possible.

## MESSAGING PILLARS (Core Themes)

### Pillar 1: SaaS Content Crisis
**Theme:** "Most SaaS companies fail at content. Here's why."
- Content angle: B2B storytelling, narrative marketing, SEO best practices
- Audience: SaaS founders, marketing leaders, product teams
- Substack depth: 2-3 essays/month (3,000+ words each)
- Twitter frequency: 3-5 tweets/week on content trends
- **Direct revenue:** P2 service inquiries (customers hire for content writing)

### Pillar 2: Freelance Writing as Skill + Business
**Theme:** "Anyone can charge $600-2K/month for writing. Here's how."
- Content angle: Pricing strategy, sales techniques, positioning
- Audience: Freelancers, indie hackers, aspiring entrepreneurs
- Substack depth: 2-3 essays/month (personal narrative, case studies)
- Twitter frequency: 3-5 tweets/week on freelance economics
- **Direct revenue:** P2 service inquiries (consultants want to learn, become customers)

### Pillar 3: AI Writing Tools & Ethics
**Theme:** "AI is changing content creation. Here's what actually works."
- Content angle: Tool reviews, use cases, ethical implications
- Audience: Content creators, marketers, AI-curious indie hackers
- Substack depth: 1-2 essays/month (trend analysis, opinion)
- Twitter frequency: 2-3 tweets/week on AI writing news
- **Direct revenue:** P3 affiliate traffic (Jasper, Copy.ai, Writesonic reviews)

### Pillar 4: Revenue + Recovery Journey
**Theme:** "Building passive/low-maintenance revenue streams (the recovery journey)."
- Content angle: Transparency, vulnerability, real numbers
- Audience: Bootstrapped founders, those recovering from setbacks
- Substack depth: 1 essay/month (monthly revenue report, lessons learned)
- Twitter frequency: 1-2 tweets/week (milestones, decisions, learnings)
- **Direct revenue:** Credibility spillover to all 3 streams (transparency builds trust)

## DISTRIBUTION CHANNELS (Sequenced)

### Channel 1: Substack (Owned, Weekly Newsletter)
**Frequency:** 2-3 essays/week
**Format:** Long-form (2,000-3,500 words), deep dives

**Schedule:**
- **Monday 9:00 AM:** Pillar 1 or Pillar 2 (SaaS content or freelance economics)
- **Wednesday:** Pillar 3 or Pillar 4 (AI/tools or personal updates)
- **Friday (optional):** Short-form opinion piece or Q&A

**Metrics target:** 200 subscribers by Jun 30 (from ~50 at Apr 1)

**Subscriber growth strategy:**
- Week 1-2: Grow from email list (existing followers, Twitter audience)
- Week 3-4: Promote best essays on Twitter (drive signups)
- Week 5-8: Cross-link with Medium (evergreen discovery)

**CTA:** Each article ends with "If you need professional content writing at scale, let's talk" (P2 service link)

---

### Channel 2: Twitter (Real-Time, Daily Engagement)
**Frequency:** 1-2 threads + 3-5 single tweets/day
**Format:** Short-form (1-3 tweets), punchy, engagement-focused

**Tweet types (rotation):**
- **Monday/Wednesday/Friday:** Product updates or lesson/insight (original thinking)
- **Tuesday/Thursday:** Retweet + comment on relevant founders/content
- **Weekend:** Personal reflection or funny behind-the-scenes (human side)

**Thread formula (2x/week):**
1. Hook: Problem statement or controversial take
2. 5-7 tweets: Breakdown, data, examples
3. CTA: Link to Substack essay OR "what am I missing?" (engagement)

**Metrics target:** 500 followers by Jun 30 (from ~200 at Apr 1)

**Follower growth strategy:**
- Week 1-2: Daily engagement in #IndieHackers #Bootstrapped communities (authentic comments, not spam)
- Week 3-4: Share product wins (transparent about growth)
- Week 5-8: Controversial takes or contrarian opinions (high engagement)

**Tags:** #IndieHackers #Bootstrapped #SaaSMarketing #Freelance #Writing #AI

---

### Channel 3: Medium (SEO Distribution, Evergreen)
**Frequency:** 1-2 articles/week
**Format:** Mid-form (1,500-2,500 words), optimized for search

**Publishing strategy:**
- Mirror best Substack essays to Medium (repurpose, don't duplicate)
- Optimize titles for SEO (keyword research: "freelance writing," "content marketing," "AI writing tools")
- Link back to Substack (conversion funnel)

**Series to build:**
- "Freelance Writing Mastery" (8-10 articles by Jun 30)
- "AI Writing Tools Tested" (6-8 articles by Jun 30)
- "SaaS Content Strategy" (6-8 articles by Jun 30)

**Metrics target:** 300 followers by Jun 30, 100+ viewers per article by Week 8
