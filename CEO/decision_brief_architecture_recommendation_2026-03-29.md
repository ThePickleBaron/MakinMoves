## The 6 Alternatives: Quick Assessment

### 1. Agency Model (Done-for-You Services)
- **Upside:** $3,000–5,000/mo by Month 6; recurring revenue; highest defensibility
- **Downside:** 8–12 week sales cycle = first revenue Month 3; requires hiring/project management; more active work
- **Verdict:** ⚠ STRONG OPTION for Phase 2 (Month 4+), NOT optimal for initial launch

### 2. Email-First (Substack to 1,000 subscribers)
- **Upside:** Sponsor revenue + higher conversion + owned audience; potential $1,700–4,700/mo by Month 6
- **Downside:** $0 revenue Months 1–2; requires 2x/week writing; slow ramp
- **Verdict:** ⚠ GOOD as Phase 1.5 hybrid (parallel to 3-stream, not instead of)

### 3. Hyperspecialization (SaaS Niche)
- **Upside:** 2–3x higher conversion rate; premium pricing; expert positioning
- **Downside:** Narrower TAM; niche saturation risk; requires strong positioning
- **Verdict:** ✓ EASY to test (Week 1, 2 hours); if 2–3x conversion, double down

### 4. Unified Platform (Kajabi/Teachable)
- **Upside:** Single email list; unified analytics; cross-sell optimization
- **Downside:** $150–300/mo cost; 1–2 week setup delay; platform lock-in; no early revenue advantage
- **Verdict:** ⚠ Revisit Month 4+ after validating 3-stream model

### 5. SaaS Tool (Freelancer Dashboard)
- **Upside:** Passive revenue; $10K achievable with 200 customers; high defensibility
- **Downside:** 2–4 week build time; no revenue until Month 2–3; product-market fit uncertainty; tech overhead
- **Verdict:** ✗ NOT recommended for immediate launch (too much upfront investment)

### 6. Membership/Community
- **Upside:** Recurring revenue; community moat; strong LTV
- **Downside:** Requires 5–10 hrs/week community management; slow member ramp (0 Month 1 → 50 Month 4); higher churn risk
- **Verdict:** ⚠ Consider Phase 2 (Month 4+) if 3-stream validating
