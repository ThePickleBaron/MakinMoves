## PART 1: STRATEGY & NICHE SELECTION (Days 1–2)

### Step 1: Understand the 2026 Reality

**What changed (Feb 2025):**
- Redbubble added tiered account fees: $0 for <$2/month earnings, up to $97/month for earnings >$500/month
- This means: If you earn $500/month, Redbubble takes $97 + ~20% in transaction fees = $197 in costs. Net: $303.
- Merch by Amazon: No account fees, 40–50% royalty (varies by price tier)
- **Result:** Merch by Amazon now significantly more profitable per item

**What works in 2026:**
- Hyper-niche designs (not broad/generic)
- Designs targeting passionate communities (DevOps, solopreneurs, specific hobbies)
- Designs solving specific problems (not just "funny" shirts)

**What doesn't work:**
- Generic funny quotes ("Live Laugh Love" style)
- Extremely broad niches
- Low-effort designs competing on price alone

---

### Step 2: Choose Your Niche (1–2 hours)

**Goal:** Pick 1 niche where you can create 5–10 designs quickly and authentically.

**The Test:** Can you explain your niche in 1 sentence? If not, too broad.

**Process:**

1. **List 5 communities you understand:**
   - Professions (DevOps engineers, real estate agents, therapists)
   - Hobbies (rock climbing, sourdough baking, indie gaming)
   - Problems (ADHD productivity, freelancer burnout, startup founders)
   - Demographics (coffee lovers, new moms, early retirees)
   - Identities (cats > people, introvert humor, productivity obsessed)

2. **For each, ask:**
   - Would THIS person buy a t-shirt about it? (High certainty = yes)
   - Do existing designs exist in this niche? (Yes = proven market)
   - Are existing designs weak/generic? (Yes = opportunity to differentiate)

3. **Google test:**
   - Google: "[niche] t-shirt" or "[niche] merchandise"
   - Do real designs exist? (Proof of market)
   - Are they well-designed and specific? (Competition level)
   - Are they outdated or generic? (Opportunity)

4. **Reddit validation:**
   - Find subreddit for your community
   - Search "[niche] shirt" or "[niche] design"
   - Do people ask for merchandise? (Demand signal)
   - Are there more than 10 posts/month about it? (Activity level)

**Good niches (proven 2025-2026):**
- **DevOps/SRE engineering** ("I don't need permission to reload," "YAML isn't code")
- **SaaS founder humor** ("I lost $50K on this idea")
- **Content creator identity** ("I survived 10 algorithm changes")
- **Solopreneur grind** ("Built my first $100 today")
- **Specific hobbies** ("Sourdough is just yeast management")
- **Professional identity** ("Therapist mode: ON")

**Bad niches:**
- General "motivation" shirts
- Overly broad ("developer" is too broad; "Python developer" is better, "Django full-stack developer" is best)
- Single-joke concepts (doesn't sustain 5+ designs)

**DECISION:** Pick ONE niche. Write it down: `_pod-niche.txt`

## PART 2: MERCH BY AMAZON SETUP (Days 3–4)

### Step 3: Apply to Merch by Amazon

**What it is:** Amazon's print-on-demand program. You design, Amazon manufactures/ships. No upfront cost.

**Why start here:**
- No account fees (unlike Redbubble)
- Highest profit per item (~$5–$10 per shirt sold)
- 73% more revenue than Redbubble (2025 study)
- Amazon's audience massive (100M+ Prime members)

**Limitations:**
- Approval takes 1–4 weeks (you need to provide design samples + prove understanding of their brand guidelines)
- Higher barrier to entry (stricter quality standards)
- Limited to Amazon ecosystem

**How to apply (takes 30 min):**

1. Go to merch.amazon.com
2. Click "Apply Now"
3. Fill out form:
   - Name, email, address, tax ID (US requirement)
   - Portfolio (3–5 design samples you've created)
   - Brief statement about your design style
4. Submit
5. Wait 1–4 weeks for approval (sometimes faster)

**While waiting for approval:**
- Proceed to Step 4 (create designs)
- Set up Redbubble as backup (Merch approval can take 4 weeks)

**IMPORTANT:** Your first 5 designs are critical. Amazon will review them carefully. Quality > quantity.

---

### Step 4: Create Your First 5 Designs (3–6 hours)

**Tools:**
- Free: Canva.com (has print-on-demand templates)
- Paid option: Adobe Express ($10/mo, or free tier)
- Advanced option: Photoshop ($20/mo student, $50+ regular) or GIMP (free)

**Recommended:** Start with Canva (easiest).

**Design specs for t-shirts:**
- Dimensions: 4500 x 5400 pixels (3:1 aspect ratio—shirt front)
- DPI: 300
- File format: PNG (transparent background) or PDF
- Resolution: High quality (avoid pixelation)

**5 Design Ideas (1 per day, Days 3–7):**

1. **Direct niche message** (Problem + solution)
   - "I Lost $50K On This Idea (And I'd Do It Again)"
   - Target: SaaS founders
   - Layout: Large text, dark shirt, clean font

2. **Humorous identity** (In-joke)
   - "YAML Is Not Code (It's A Lifestyle)"
   - Target: DevOps engineers
   - Layout: Bold, slightly sarcastic

3. **Specific achievement** (Pride/Lifestyle)
   - "My First $100 Revenue Day"
   - Target: Solopreneurs/indie hackers
   - Layout: Celebratory, uplifting vibe

4. **Reverse psychology** (Niche humor)
   - "[Niche] Wellness: Coffee + Imposter Syndrome"
   - Target: Creators/startup people
   - Layout: Relatable, witty

5. **Achievement + community** (Belonging)
   - "I Survived 10 Algorithm Changes (And All I Got Was This Shirt)"
   - Target: Content creators
   - Layout: Badge-style, achievement feel

**Process per design (30–45 min):**
1. Go to canva.com > Search "t-shirt template"
2. Use print-on-demand mockup (shows how it'll look on shirt)
3. Add text (your niche message)
4. Add graphic (simple icon or illustration from Canva library, or minimal)
5. Adjust colors for contrast (ensure readability)
6. Download as PNG (transparent if possible)

**Design best practices:**
- ✓ Large, readable text (test at small size)
- ✓ Specific to niche (not generic)
- ✓ Unique point of view (not copied existing designs)
- ✓ Professional quality (even if simple)
- ✓ High contrast (readable on all shirt colors)
- ✗ Don't: Overly complex, too much text, blurry graphics

**Quality bar for Merch:** If Amazon rejects for low quality, you'll know. Aim for design that could be sold in a real store.

## PART 3: REDBUBBLE SETUP (Parallel, Day 3)

### Step 5: Upload to Redbubble (Backup while waiting for Merch approval)

**Why:** Redbubble approval is fast (often <24 hours). Use while waiting for Merch approval.

**CAVEAT:** Redbubble account fees ($0–$97/month based on earnings, Feb 2025). Only keep if earning >$200/month to justify fees. Otherwise, treat as testing ground.

**Setup (20 min):**
1. Go to redbubble.com
2. Click "Sell"
3. Sign up (email, password)
4. Verify email
5. Go to "Your shop"

**Upload first design (10 min):**
1. Click "Create new design"
2. Upload your PNG file
3. Add title: "[Niche] [Message] T-Shirt"
4. Add description (100 words about niche + design meaning)
5. Set price: Start at $20 (Redbubble base cost ~$5; you make ~$5–$8 per sale)
6. Add tags: "[niche] t-shirt, [niche] design, [niche] gift, typography"
7. Publish

**Redbubble profit math:**
- Base shirt cost: ~$5.50
- Your price: $20
- Your profit: ~$4–$5 per shirt
- After account fees (if earning $200/mo): ~$3–$4 per shirt

**Reality:** Redbubble profitable only if earning >$300–$500/month (to offset fees).

### Step 7: Promote Your Designs (Ongoing, Days 8+)

**Goal:** Get initial sales through organic/free channels (no ad budget).

**Platform 1: Reddit (Start Day 8, 30 min)**
- Find 3 relevant subreddits:
  - r/[niche] (specific community)
  - r/solopreneurs, r/Entrepreneur, r/startup (general business)
  - r/[hobby] (if hobby-based niche)
- **Read the rules:** Check if self-promotion allowed
- **If allowed:** Create post
  - Title: "Designed a shirt for [niche]. What do you think?" (not salesy)
  - Include image (screenshot of design)
  - Link in comments only (if self-promo restricted)
- **If not allowed:** Comment on existing threads naturally, link in profile

**Platform 2: Twitter/X (Start Day 9, 20 min)**
- Tweet your designs to niche community
- Use hashtags: #[niche], #Entrepreneur, #indiehackers, #solopreneurs
- Example tweet:
  ```
  I designed a shirt for [niche].
  [Image]
  Link: [Merch/Redbubble link]

  Also available on Merch by Amazon (waiting for approval).
  ```

**Platform 3: Community Groups (Day 10–14, 30 min)**
- Find Facebook groups for your niche
- Contribute genuinely, mention shirt in comments (don't spam)
- Example: "Great question! I actually wrote about this [article], and made a shirt about it too: [link]"

**Platform 4: TikTok/Instagram (Optional, if comfortable)**
- Quick video showing design + shirt mockup
- Trend: "Made a shirt for [niche people]" (10–15 sec video)
- Link in bio

**IMPORTANT:** No paid ads yet. Test organic channels first. If sales come, consider small ads ($10–$20/day) later.

## PART 5: ITERATION & SCALING (Weeks 2–4)

### Step 8: Monitor Sales (10 min/week)

**Track (both platforms):**
- Impressions: How many people saw your design?
- Clicks: How many visited product page?
- Sales: How many bought?
- Conversion rate: Clicks → Sales (aim for 2%+)

**If design gets 0 clicks in 2 weeks:**
- Redesign or reposition
- Niche might be wrong, or design isn't resonating
- Create a new design, test different message

**If design gets clicks but no sales:**
- Price too high? (Test lower)
- Product page copy unclear? (Revise)
- Design quality? (Get feedback, iterate)

### Step 10: Monitor Redbubble Account Fees (Monthly)

**By end of Month 1, check earnings:**
- If >$200: Keep Redbubble (fees justify the extra platform)
- If <$200: Monitor; if stays low, delete products (avoid fees) or focus on Merch only

**Do the math:**
- Redbubble profit per shirt: $4–$5 (baseline)
- Account fee: $0 (<$2 earnings) to $97 (>$500/mo)
- At $200/mo earnings: ~$10 account fee
- Your actual profit: $200 - $10 = $190 (5% fee)
- At $100/mo earnings: $0 fee (falls in free tier)

**Decision rule:**
- <$100/mo: Keep free (no fees)
- $100–$300/mo: Monitor fees (might be 5–10% cost)
- >$300/mo: Worth the investment

## PART 10: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Generic designs:** "Funny" doesn't sell. Specific niche humor wins.
2. **Too many designs too fast:** Better to nail 5 great designs than 50 mediocre ones.
3. **Wrong price:** Start $20, test down to $18 if needed. DON'T price at $12 (race to bottom).
4. **No marketing:** Building designs is half the battle. 50% of effort should be promotion.
5. **Ignoring Redbubble fees:** Check your earnings monthly. Fee structure changed Feb 2025.
6. **Forgetting Merch approval time:** Apply ASAP; approval takes 4 weeks. Use Redbubble as testing ground.
7. **Over-relying on one design:** Build portfolio of 20+ designs. Diversification = stability.
8. **Giving up too early:** Takes 4–8 weeks to see meaningful sales. Be patient.

## FAQ

**Q: How long until first sale?**
A: Realistic: 2–4 weeks if you promote aggressively. Passive (no marketing): 8+ weeks or never.

**Q: Can I do this with zero design experience?**

**Q: Should I focus on Merch or Redbubble?**
A: **Merch by Amazon first** (better margins, no account fees). Redbubble for testing while waiting for Merch approval.

**Q: What if Merch never approves me?**
A: Redbubble or TeePublic as backup. Merch rejection is rare if designs are quality + clear niche.

A: Average case: 15–25 designs to hit $100+/month. Some get lucky with 1–2 viral designs; most take volume.

**Q: Can I keep adding designs forever?**
A: Yes. Best performers scale to $500–$2K/month with 30+ quality designs + consistent promotion.
