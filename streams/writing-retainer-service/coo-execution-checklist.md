# COO Execution Checklist — Writing Retainer Service
## Week-by-Week Action Items (12-Week Launch Plan)

**Owner:** COO
**Timeline:** Weeks 1–12 (March 29 — June 30, 2026)
**Goal:** Launch service, sign 3–5 pilot customers week 3–4, 20 customers by week 12

---

## Week 1: Foundation (March 29 — April 4)

### Task 1: Recruit & Vet Writers (6 hours)
- [ ] Create list of 5–10 SaaS/B2B writers to reach out to
  - Sources: Twitter, LinkedIn, Upwork, WriterAccess, referrals
  - Criteria: 3+ years SaaS/B2B experience, strong portfolio samples
- [ ] Send outreach emails to 10 writers (template below)
- [ ] Receive interest responses; shortlist top 5
- [ ] Send writing test to top 5 (topic: "How to Price a SaaS Product")
  - Payment: $200–400 per test
  - Timeline: 3 days for completion
- [ ] Review and score writing tests (clarity, depth, SEO awareness)
- [ ] Hire top 3 writers; send contracts

**Outreach email:**
```
Hi [Writer Name],

I'm launching a writing service for SaaS teams, and I'm looking for 3–5
dedicated writers to work on retainer with me.

Your work caught my eye. You clearly understand SaaS/B2B topics.

Quick test: I'll pay you $300–400 to write one 1,500-word blog post
on SaaS pricing. This helps me assess fit.

Interested? Let me know.

[Your name]
```

---

### Task 2: Build Landing Page (4 hours)
- [ ] Choose platform: Carrd.co (simplest), Webflow, or Wix
- [ ] Outline key sections:
  - **Hero:** One-liner + value prop image
  - **Problem:** Upwork pain (vetting, inconsistency, time)
  - **Solution:** Our retainer model (consistency, relationship, expertise)
  - **How it works:** 4-step process (discovery → match → deliver → feedback)
  - **Pricing:** 3-tier table (Starter $600, Growth $1,000, Scale $1,500)
  - **Why different:** vs. Upwork, vs. agencies, vs. hiring in-house
  - **CTA:** "Start a free consultation" + link to Calendly
- [ ] Write copy (focus on founder pain)
- [ ] Add 2–3 sample articles (your best writing or examples)
- [ ] Set up domain: WritingRetainer.co or similar
- [ ] Launch by end of week

**Key landing page quote:**
*"Stop vetting 50 Upwork writers. Get a dedicated SaaS writer who learns your market and delivers consistent quality at $600–$1,500/month."*

---

### Task 3: Set Up Operations (4 hours)
- [ ] CRM choice: Pipedrive free tier (easiest for sales pipeline)
- [ ] Project management: Notion or Asana (track articles, deadlines)
- [ ] Invoicing: Stripe, Wave, or Freshbooks (auto-renewals)
- [ ] Communication: Slack workspace for team + customers
- [ ] Spreadsheet: Google Sheets for metrics tracking (MRR, CAC, churn)

**Spreadsheet columns:**
- Customer name, signup date, plan (Starter/Growth/Scale)
- Monthly revenue, writer assigned, article count
- Churn status, NPS, testimonial collected

- [ ] First call discovery questions (15-min call)
- [ ] Customer onboarding email (post-sale)
- [ ] Weekly status email (to customer)

---

**End of Week 1 Checklist:**
- [ ] 3–5 writers under contract
- [ ] Landing page live
- [ ] CRM + tools set up
- [ ] Sales templates drafted

## Week 3: Tier 1 Launch (April 12 — April 18)

### Task 1: Warm Network Outreach (5 hours)
- [ ] Send personalized emails to top 30 warm network contacts
  - Personalization: Mention something specific about their company / situation
  - Call to action: "Interested in being a pilot customer?" or "Open to feedback on this idea?"
  - Incentive: $100 off first month if they sign up this week
  - **Goal:** 50% response rate = 15 replies; 30% conversion = 4–5 customers
- [ ] Track responses in CRM
- [ ] Follow up on non-responses after 3 days

**Outreach email template:**
```
Hi [Name],

Remember when you mentioned you'd like to build a blog for [Company] but content hiring is always a nightmare?

I'm launching a writing service for founders exactly like you. Basically:
instead of vetting 50 Upwork writers, you get a dedicated writer who
learns your market and delivers consistently.

Pricing: $600–$1,500/month for 2–4 articles/month.

I'm looking for 5 pilot customers to prove the model. Interested?

I can do $100 off the first month if you're in.

Portfolio: [landing page link]

Worth 20 minutes to chat?

[Your name]
```

### Task 4: Cold Outreach Setup (2 hours)
- [ ] Prepare LinkedIn cold outreach approach:

### Task 5: First Consultation Calls (3 hours)
- [ ] Conduct first discovery calls with warm network responses
- [ ] Call script:

  4. "If we could solve that, would this be something you'd try?"
  5. Close: "Let's do 1 month as a pilot. No long commitment. What plan resonates with you?"
- [ ] Expected close rate: 40–60% of calls
- [ ] Goal: Sign 2–3 customers this week

## Red Flags (Escalate to CEO)

- [ ] **Conversion rate <10%:** Messaging not resonating; need to adjust
- [ ] **Churn >15% per month:** Quality or satisfaction issue; review customer feedback
- [ ] **CAC >$500:** Acquisition channels too expensive; focus on organic
- [ ] **Writer quality issues:** Customer complaints about delivery / quality; replace writer
- [ ] **Can't scale writers:** Hit capacity wall; need to hire more writers or pause growth

## Resource Allocation

**Time:**
- Week 1–4: ~20 hours/week (recruitment, setup, first launches)
- Week 5–12: ~15 hours/week (operations + outreach)
- Post-launch: ~10 hours/week (if hire ops person)

**Cost:**
- Initial setup: $3,200 (landing page, tools, writer tests)
- Monthly (variable): 40% of revenue to writers + $200 tools
- Monthly (optional, week 3+): $1,000 if hire freelance ops

**Leverage:**
- Writers are your leverage (each writer = 2–3 customers)
- Testimonials are your leverage (reduces CAC, increases conversion)
- Systems are your leverage (onboarding, brief templates = less time per customer)

## Go/No-Go Decision Points

**Week 3:** Did we sign 2–3 customers from warm network?
- **YES:** Continue to Tier 2 (cold outreach)
- **NO:** Adjust messaging, try different channels, or pivot

**Week 6:** Do we have 8–10 customers? Is churn <10%?
- **YES:** Scale with cold outreach + paid ads
- **NO:** Diagnose issue (quality, fit, or messaging) before scaling

**Week 12:** Are we at 18–25 customers? Is unit economics positive?
- **YES:** Plan year 2 growth (hire ops, expand to adjacent services)
- **NO:** Decide on continued investment or shift focus
