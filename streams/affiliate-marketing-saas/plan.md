## PART 1: RESEARCH & POSITIONING (Days 1–3)

### Step 1: Choose Your SaaS/AI Niche
**Why:** AI/SaaS is the hottest affiliate niche in 2025-2026 (20–40% recurring commissions). But specificity wins.

**The Test:** Can you write 10 blog post titles about this topic right now?

**Process (1–2 hours):**

1. **List 3 AI/SaaS problems you understand:**
   - Example: Content creators struggling with AI tools, freelancers overwhelmed by project management, small agencies needing automation
2. **Google each problem + "SaaS" + "tool":**
   - Does a $9–$99/mo SaaS product exist?
   - Are people searching for solutions? (Use Google Trends, Ahrefs free tier)
3. **Check affiliate program maturity:**
   - Is there a SaaS with affiliate program? (Most do in 2025)
   - Commission structure: 20–40% recurring = ideal

**Good niches (recurring revenue, underserved):**
- **AI Writing Assistants for Course Creators** (Teachable, SendFox, Substack pro integrations)
- **Email Marketing for Solopreneurs** (ConvertKit, Beehiiv, Substack pro—all have affiliate programs)
- **AI Automation for Freelancers** (Make.com, Zapier, Airtable)
- **Project Management for Agencies** (Notion, Monday.com, Asana, ClickUp)
- **SEO Tools for Content Creators** (Ahrefs, Semrush, SE Ranking)
- **Scheduling Tools for Social Media** (Buffer, Later, Hootsuite)

**Bad niches (saturated, low commission):**
- General "productivity apps"
- "Best tools" roundups (too much competition)
- One-time purchase products (no recurring revenue)

**DECISION:** Pick ONE niche and write it down: `_affiliate-niche.txt`

### Step 2: Validate the Niche (2–3 hours)

**Test if people care:**

1. **Search volume check (free tools):**
   - Go to google.com/trends
   - Search: "[niche] [SaaS tool]" (e.g., "AI writing for course creators")
   - Is there a line graph? Yes = people searching. No = maybe niche too small.

2. **Affiliate program check:**
   - Google: "[SaaS product] affiliate program"
   - Commission structure? (Look for 20%+ recurring or $100+ per sale)
   - Example: ConvertKit affiliate = 30% recurring (great), Beehiiv = 30% lifetime value (great)

3. **Content saturation check:**
   - Google: "[niche] best tools 2025"
   - How many results? If >1M, niche is competitive but proven. If <100K, maybe untapped.
   - Scan top 3 results: Are they weak? (Short, outdated, low production value) = Opportunity.

4. **Reddit proof-of-concept (30 min):**
   - Find subreddit for your audience (e.g., r/entrepreneurs, r/freelance, r/contentcreators, r/solopreneur)
   - Search "[problem] tool" or "[SaaS product] review"
   - Do people ask questions? Are there active discussions? Yes = niche has demand.

**Validation checklist (all 4 = green light):**
- [ ] Google Trends shows consistent search interest
- [ ] 2+ SaaS products exist with affiliate programs (20%+ commission)
- [ ] Reddit has active discussions about the problem
- [ ] Top Google results are weak (opportunity to rank)

**If all 4 check: GREEN LIGHT. Proceed with niche.**

### Step 3: Competitive Landscape (1–2 hours)

**Goal:** Understand what already exists and where you'll differentiate.

**Search "top X keywords for your niche":**
- "[Niche] comparison"
- "[Niche] best tools"
- "[Niche] guide 2025"
- "[Problem] solution"

**For top 3 results, analyze:**
- How many words? (If <2K, you can go deeper)
- How old? (If >1 year, outdated = your advantage)
- Do they have affiliate links? (If yes, proof of monetization path)
- Design/production value? (If poor, you can outrank with better design)

**Your differentiation angle:**
Don't write "Best AI Tools for Course Creators" (too broad, competed).
Write: "How I Chose Email Software for My Cohort Course (Compared 5 Tools)" (specific, personal, unique).

### Step 7: Create First 5 Articles (5–8 hours, split Days 4–7)

**Goal:** Get initial content live for Google to crawl (SEO takes 4–8 weeks to show).

**Article #1: Pillar Article (Long-form, 2,500+ words)**
**Title:** "[Niche] Tools 2025: I Tested 10 Solutions (Detailed Comparison)"

**Structure:**
- Intro (300 words): Problem statement, why you tested, what you found
- 1 comparison table (tools, price, best for, pros, cons)
- 5–10 detailed reviews (200–400 words each)
  - Product name
  - Price
  - Key features
  - Pros
  - Cons
  - Affiliate link ("Click here to try [product]")
- Conclusion: Which tool to pick based on use case
- Disclosure: "I earn a commission if you buy via my links (at no extra cost to you)"

**Time:** 3–4 hours (research + writing)

---

## PART 5: MONETIZATION (Starting Month 2, when you have 20+ articles)

### Step 13: Add Affiliate Links (Ongoing)

**Strategic placement (NOT spammy):**
1. **In content:** Only when genuinely relevant to reader
2. **In resource lists:** "Tools I use and recommend"
3. **In email:** Share affiliate link for relevant articles
4. **Bottom of article:** "If this post helped, try [tool] (affiliate link, no extra cost)"
5. **Disclosure:** Always disclose affiliate relationships (FTC requirement + trust)

**Link strategy:**
- Product comparisons: Link to 3–5 tools (reader picks one)
- Deep dives: Link to that one tool + 1–2 alternatives
- How-tos: Link to the tool you're teaching with
- Avoid: Linking to 10+ tools (reader paralysis, lower conversion)

## PART 11: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Too broad niche:** "AI tools" fails. "Email marketing for solopreneurs" wins.
2. **Impatient with SEO:** Expecting traffic in Week 1. Takes 4–8 weeks minimum.
3. **No email list:** Relying only on Google. Build email list from Day 1.
4. **Quantity over quality:** 50 bad articles < 20 great articles.
5. **Spammy affiliate links:** Linking to 10 tools looks desperate. 3–5 genuine = higher conversion.
6. **No disclosure:** FTC requires affiliate disclosures. Always include.
7. **Forgetting to promote:** Write it once, promote it 10 times (Reddit, Twitter, email).

## FAQ

**Q: How long until first affiliate commission?**
A: 4–8 weeks realistic (time for Google to crawl + rank your articles). Some see clicks in Week 2, commission by Month 2.

**Q: Do I need to be an expert in the niche?**
A: No. Willingness to research + honesty about affiliate relationships > expertise. You'll learn as you write.

**Q: What if my niche idea fails?**
A: Pivot. Write 5 articles, see if any rank. If zero traffic after 8 weeks, pick new niche. Time cost: 40 hours (expensive lesson, but valuable data).

**Q: Can I do this part-time (30 min/day)?**
A: Not for first month (setup + initial articles are 40+ hours). After Month 2, yes—maintenance is 3–4 hours/week.

**Q: Should I outsource article writing?**
A: At first, no. Write yourself (you know your niche best). After Month 6 (when revenue justifies it), hire freelance writers ($50–$100/article).
