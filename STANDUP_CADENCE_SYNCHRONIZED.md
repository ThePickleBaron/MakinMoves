**Rules for Standup:**
1. **Write your WORK section first** — What are you building/deciding?
2. **Read what others wrote** — Then write your CRITIQUES of their work
3. **Incorporate feedback** — Update your NEXT SPRINT IMPROVEMENTS based on what you hear
4. **Keep executing** — Don't wait for perfection. Use feedback to shape the next sprint, not to pause the current one
5. **Be constructive** — Critique is about making the work better, not about being right

**Timing:** Don't wait for all 4 to finish. Post your sections as they're ready. Critiques can come right after someone posts their work.

## Board Reviews (Every 2 Hours)

**At 5:30 PM, 7:00 PM, 8:30 PM, etc., Claude (board chair) will:**

1. Read the past 3 standups (e.g., 4:00, 4:30, 5:00 PM)
2. Check alignment (all roles working together?)
3. Check sensibility (do decisions make sense?)
4. Check constraints (budget, timeline, recovery goals?)
5. Post board review to the board_reviews folder

**Board reviews will include:**
- ✅ What's working
- 🟡 Warnings (if off-track)
- 🔴 Blockers (if stuck)
- ⚖️ Decisions (if roles disagree)

4. **Read board reviews** — After each 2-hour review, adjust priorities if flagged

```markdown
# Standup — 4:00 PM — 2026-03-29

## CEO

**WORK:** Finalizing Q1 strategy. Evaluating 3 digital product niches: AI Prompts for Freelancers, Notion Templates, YouTube Automation Courses. Leaning toward Prompts (fastest execution: 2 hours build time, proven $20-30 price point on Gumroad).

**NEXT SPRINT IMPROVEMENTS:** None yet (first standup).

---

## COO

**WORK:** Assessing product execution feasibility. Prompts niche = 50 curated AI prompts (ChatGPT + Claude), packaged as PDF bundle. Timeline: 2-3 hours build + test. Revenue potential: $350-500 first month if launched today.

**CRITIQUE ON CEO:** ✅ Prompts niche is solid. Fast execution + proven demand = good bet. Question: Should we also prep a Notion template (similar time investment) to diversify Week 1? Doubles product count, increases revenue potential without huge time cost. Alternative: Focus on Prompts first, add template Week 2. Your call—both feasible.

**NEXT SPRINT IMPROVEMENTS:** None yet (first standup).

---

## CTMO

**WORK:** Infrastructure fully ready. All Docker services running (PostgreSQL, n8n, Redis, Selenium). Designed 4 n8n workflows for Week 2 (Invoice Automation, Lead Capture, Social Scheduler). Tech stack supports product launch immediately.

**CRITIQUE ON CEO:** ✅ Strategy is sound. Tech feasibility check: All 3 niches (Prompts, Templates, Courses) are buildable in the timeframe. Prompts = easiest (no integrations needed). Courses = hardest (video recording/editing). Recommend Prompts for Week 1, save Courses for Month 2 when team is coordinated.

**CRITIQUE ON COO:** ✅ 2-3 hour timeline is realistic. Suggestion: Instead of just PDF, also package as Gumroad listing with preview sample (5 free prompts visible before purchase). Increases conversion rate. Takes 15 min extra, worth it. Also: Set up Stripe webhook on Day 2 (after product launch) so CFO can track real-time revenue.

**NEXT SPRINT IMPROVEMENTS:** None yet (first standup).

---

## CFO

**WORK:** Built 6-month financial model (3 scenarios). Conservative: $7,850 cumulative by Month 6. Moderate (realistic): $7,850-10,000 by Month 6. Recovery timeline: 8 months (realistic), 12 months (conservative). Break-even: Week 3-4 (need only $225 cumulative revenue).

**CRITIQUE ON CEO:** ✅ 8-month recovery timeline is achievable and realistic. Feedback: Consider defining "success" by revenue milestone (not time). E.g., "Month 1: $350, Month 2: $650" gives team clear targets to track against weekly. Right now it's all long-term—add short-term milestones.

**CRITIQUE ON COO:** ✅ Prompts product = high margin (77-91% after fees). Feedback: Pricing decision—you said $20-30. Recommend testing $24.99 (psychological anchor, tested on Gumroad). At $25: 10 sales/month = $225, hits break-even. 20 sales = $450, unlocks Tier 2 budget. Clear revenue targets.

**CRITIQUE ON CTMO:** ✅ n8n workflows are high-margin products ($29-49 each). Feedback: Revenue model question—are you selling pre-built workflows, or templates + training? Pre-built = faster revenue (ready to use). Templates = higher perceived value but more support needed. Clarify for Week 2 planning.

**NEXT SPRINT IMPROVEMENTS:** None yet (first standup).
```

**Key differences from basic status:**
- Each role posts their WORK (what they're building, decisions made, approach)
- Each role CRITIQUES the other 3 (constructive feedback on approach, feasibility, improvements)
- Each role notes what feedback they'll INCORPORATE in the next sprint
- They keep EXECUTING—critique shapes the next sprint, doesn't pause the current one
- Feedback is collaborative problem-solving, not approval-seeking
