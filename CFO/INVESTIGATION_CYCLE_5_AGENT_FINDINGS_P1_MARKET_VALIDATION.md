## CRITICAL FINDING #1: CONVERSION RATES ARE 4X LOWER FOR UNKNOWNS

**CFO assumption:** 2-5% conversion (implied from $250 M1 forecast)

**Agent finding:**
- Baseline for new, unknown templates: 0.5-2% (NOT 2-5%)
- 8% conversion is explicitly described as "very good" — suggesting it's exceptional
- 2.0-2.2% is industry standard for templates specifically
- Global ecommerce average: 1.6-2.95% (Q3 2025)

**Implication:** "Very good" at 8% means MOST sellers are well below 2%. The 5-8% benchmark lumps high-performing courses/software with struggling templates.

**For P1:** New seller with zero audience → expect 0.5-1.5% conversion, NOT 2-5%

## CRITICAL FINDING #2: TRAFFIC IS THE ACTUAL BOTTLENECK (NOT PRICE OR PRODUCT QUALITY)

**Agent discovery:** Gumroad is NOT a traffic generator — it's a payment processor

**Real traffic sources:**
- Direct: 58% (returning customers you already have)
- YouTube: 11.66% (not even Gumroad's primary source)
- Google: 10-15% (organic search, requires ranking)
- Reddit/Community: 5-10%
- **Gumroad discovery itself:** ~5%

**Case study validation:**
- Creator with 2,000 Twitter followers: 3 sales from Twitter
- Most sales from Reddit + Google (owned by Creator, not Gumroad)
- **Implication:** You need an external audience FIRST

**For P1:** Can't just upload templates and wait. Requires:
- Blog posts ranking on Google (3-6 months)
- YouTube videos (existing channel or 2-3 months)
- Email list (requires lead magnet, takes months)
- Reddit/community presence (takes time to build)

## CRITICAL FINDING #3: WHITE-LABEL TEMPLATES ARE SPECIFICALLY HIGH-RISK

**Agent discovered:** Gumroad has hidden algorithm flags for:
- Resold/duplicated content → silent shadowban
- Keyword-stuffed products → algorithm throttles visibility
- Generic templates → deprioritized in discovery

**Risk:** Buying white-label templates and reselling them could trigger:
- Account suspension (violates ToS)
- Algorithmic shadowban (no visibility in Gumroad search/discovery)
- Revenue clawback (withheld until investigation)

**For P1:** White-label strategy has HIGHER risk than original assumption — the plan didn't account for this policy constraint
