## Critical Path Metrics (Monitor Weekly)

### 1. Sales Cycle Length (Days from Outreach to Contract)

**Why:** Baseline assumption is 8–12 weeks (56–84 days). If >100 days, demand is softer than expected.

| Week | Prospect | Contact Date | Discovery Date | Proposal Date | Close Date | Cycle Days | Status |
|------|----------|-------------|--------|--------|--------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Company A | 3/29 | TBD | TBD | TBD | TBD | Outreach |
| 1 | Company B | 3/29 | TBD | TBD | TBD | TBD | Outreach |
| — | — | — | — | — | — | — | — |

**Tracking rule:**
- Week 1–8: Log every prospect (initial outreach)
- Week 9+: Log conversion (if closed) or note as "lost" (if cycled out)

**Green flag:** Median cycle <90 days, close rate >30%
**Yellow flag:** Median cycle 90–120 days, close rate 15–30%
**Red flag:** Median cycle >120 days, close rate <15% → PIVOT

**By Month 3, should have:** 3–5 prospects in pipeline, 1 customer closed

---

### 2. Customer Churn Rate (% Lost Monthly)

**Why:** Assumption is 3–5% monthly. If >10%, quality or fit issue.

**Tracking template:**

| Month | Customers Start | New Acquired | Customers Churned | Customers End | Churn % | Notes |
|-------|---------|---------|---------|-------|--------|-------|
| 2 | 0 | 1 | 0 | 1 | 0% | First customer |
| 3 | 1 | 1 | 0 | 2 | 0% | On track |
| 4 | 2 | 1 | 0 | 3 | 0% | All renewing |
| 5 | 3 | 1 | 0–1 | 3–4 | 0–33% | Watch if any |
| 6 | 4 | 1 | 0–1 | 4–5 | 0–25% | Normal range |

**Early warning indicators:**
- Month 2–3: Any churn = RED FLAG (something wrong with onboarding/quality)
- Month 4–5: >5% churn = YELLOW FLAG (investigate quality/fit)
- Month 6+: >10% churn = RED FLAG (systemic problem)

**Exit rule:** If churn >20% for 2 consecutive months, shut down stream

---

### 3. Writer Capacity Utilization (% of Time on Writing Tasks)

**Why:** If >100% sustained, burnout incoming in 2–4 weeks. If <60%, underutilized (efficiency problem).

**Weekly tracking:**

| Week | Articles Assigned | Hours Required | Capacity Available | Utilization | Quality Issues | Notes |
|------|---------|---------|---------|-------|--------|-------|
| 1 | 2 | 40 | 40 | 100% | None | Sustainable |
| 2 | 3 | 60 | 40 | 150% | OVERLOAD | RED |
| 3 | 2 | 40 | 40 | 100% | None | Back to baseline |

**Calculation:**
- 1 article @ 2,000 words = 15 hours (research + writing + revisions)
- 1 article @ 1,500 words = 12 hours
- Overhead (communication, onboarding, QA): ~5 hours/week
- Available capacity: 40 hours/week

**Green zone:** 70–90% utilization
**Yellow zone:** 91–110% (sustainable for 1–2 weeks only)
**Red zone:** >110% (unsustainable; burnout in 2–4 weeks)

**Action:**
- Red zone sustained >2 weeks = hire backup writer immediately
- Yellow zone sustained >4 weeks = plan backup writer hire

---

### 4. Customer Satisfaction Score (NPS or Simple Rating)

**Why:** Early signal of quality problems before churn happens.

**Monthly check-in template:**
After delivering Month 1/2/3 output, ask each customer:

> "On a scale of 1–5, how satisfied are you with the quality and turnaround? Any adjustments for next month?"

| Customer | Month 1 | Month 2 | Month 3 | Trend | Action |
|----------|---------|---------|---------|-------|--------|
| A | 5 | 5 | 5 | ✓ Stable | Renew as-is |
| B | 4 | 3 | 2 | ↓ Declining | Investigate; offer revisions |
| C | 5 | 4 | 4 | ↓ Slight dip | Normal adjustment |

**Green flag:** 4–5 across board
**Yellow flag:** 3–4 average; investigate why
**Red flag:** <3 or declining trend → risk of churn

**Action rule:** Any score <4 = call customer within 48 hours to understand issue

### 5. Revenue per Customer (ARPU)

**Why:** Baseline assumption is $1,200. If <$900, pricing power degrading.

**Tracking:**

| Month | Customers | Total MRR | ARPU | Notes |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------|-------|
| 2 | 1 | $1,200 | $1,200 | Baseline |
| 3 | 2 | $2,200 | $1,100 | Slight pressure |
| 4 | 3 | $3,000 | $1,000 | Pricing negotiation |
| 5 | 4 | $4,000 | $1,000 | Margin squeeze |
| 6 | 5 | $5,000 | $1,000 | Lost pricing power |

**Green flag:** ARPU >$1,100
**Yellow flag:** ARPU $900–$1,100
**Red flag:** ARPU <$900 → market commoditizing, pricing strategy broken

**Action:** If ARPU drops >10%, stop discounting; pause acquisition and focus on retention

## Early Warning System: Trigger-Based Actions

### Trigger 1: Sales Cycle Extends Beyond 12 Weeks
**Signal:** First 3 prospects not closing within 90 days
**Implication:** Demand weaker or messaging wrong
**Action:**
- [ ] Week 4: Pause aggressive outreach; audit sales messaging
- [ ] Week 8: If still no closes, reduce ask ($600/mo starter tier to test fit)
- [ ] Week 12: If no closes, consider stream pivot (shift to digital products P1)

**Success criteria:** Shorten cycle to <90 days or pivot by Week 12

---

### Trigger 2: First Customer Churns in Month 2
**Signal:** Customer 1 doesn't renew after Month 1
**Implication:** Severe onboarding, quality, or expectation-setting problem
**Action:**
- [ ] Immediate: Conduct exit interview (what went wrong?)
- [ ] Within 48 hours: Document root cause
- [ ] Within 1 week: Fix systems (QA, communication, expectations)
- [ ] Pause new acquisition until root cause fixed

**Success criteria:** No further churn in Month 3–4

---

### Trigger 3: Writer Capacity Utilization >110% for 2+ Weeks
**Signal:** Articles assigned exceed sustainable output
**Implication:** Quality degradation or burnout incoming
**Action:**
- [ ] Immediate: Hire backup writer on emergency basis (even if Month 5 was plan)
- [ ] Within 1 week: Have backup writer take 20–30% load
- [ ] Pause new acquisition until capacity normalized

**Success criteria:** Utilization back to 80–90% within 3 weeks

---

### Trigger 4: Customer Satisfaction Score <4 for Any Customer
**Signal:** Single customer reports dissatisfaction
**Implication:** Quality issue or unmet expectations
**Action:**
- [ ] Within 24 hours: Call customer to understand issue
- [ ] Within 3 days: Offer solution (revisions, discount, adjustment)
- [ ] Within 1 week: Implement fix for all customers (quality standard)

**Success criteria:** Score back to 4.5+ by next month

---

### Trigger 5: MRR Flatlining for 2+ Months
**Signal:** New customer acquisition stalls
**Implication:** Sales cycle broken or market fit issue
**Action:**
- [ ] Audit: Are sales activities ongoing? (outreach frequency, response rate)
- [ ] If no sales activities: Restart outreach immediately
- [ ] If low response rate: Iterate messaging, offer
- [ ] If low close rate: Product fit issue; pivot or adjust positioning

**Success criteria:** Resume customer acquisition or pivot to P1 by Month 7

## Success Criteria (3-Month Decision Point)

**At end of Month 3, should have:**

- ✓ Sales cycle validated: <90 days average
- ✓ First 2–3 customers closed and satisfied (4.5+/5)
- ✓ ARPU holding at $1,100–$1,200
- ✓ Writer capacity at 70–90%
- ✓ Churn rate at or below 5%/month
- ✓ Cumulative burn within 10% of budget

**If any metric fails:**
- Diagnose root cause
- Implement fix by Month 4
- Reassess at Month 6

**If 2+ metrics failing by Month 4:**
- Trigger pivot decision (continue P2 with changes, or shift focus to P1)
