## ASSUMPTION 1: "P1 Customers Will Opt Into Email List at 60%+"

**Original assumption:**
- P1 customers buy template (assume 8-10 per month M1)
- 60% opt into email list = 5-6 new subscribers per month
- Plus external growth (50-100/week)
- Total: 500 email list by April 1

**Reality check:**

#### 1a. Opt-in rates for digital products (actual data)
- Gumroad opt-in (post-purchase email): 20-40% typical
- Our model assumes: 60%
- **Gap: 20-40 percentage points** (we're overestimating by 50-200%)

#### 1b. P1 Revenue Timing Matters
- INVESTIGATION_CYCLE_5 shows P1 may not hit $250 M1 (white-label pivot)
- Creator Toolkit ramp slower than white-label white-label ramps slower
- If P1 does $100-150 M1 (not $250): Only 3-5 customers
- 60% opt-in = 2-3 subscribers (not 5-6)
- **Implication: Email list growth is SLOWER than forecast**

#### 1c. Platform Dependency (Gumroad)
- Gumroad owns customer relationship, not us
- No direct access to customer email
- Opt-in is "subscribe to creator updates" (low friction, but low engagement)
- Industry data: Creator update subscribers = 15-25% engagement (opens/clicks)
- **Implication: Email list quality may be poor**

#### 1d. Email Deliverability Risk
- New email list (starting March 2026) has low sender reputation
- Gumroad emails may be flagged by Gmail/Outlook as promotional
- ISP filtering could reduce open rates 30-50%
- **Implication: List grows slow + delivers poorly**

**Verdict: ASSUMPTION FAILS**
- Actual opt-in rate: 30-40% (not 60%)
- Actual email list by April 1: 250-300 (not 500)
- Actual list engagement: 10-15% opens (not 40%)

**Financial impact:**
- Forecast: 500 list × 40% open × 5% convert = 10 inquiries, 2 customers
- Reality: 250 list × 15% open × 5% convert = 2 inquiries, 0-1 customer
- **Revenue miss: 50-100%**

## ASSUMPTION 2: "Warm Conversion Rate is 5-10%"

**Original assumption:**
- Email list: 500 people
- Warm conversion to inquiry: 5-10%
- Close rate: 20%
- Expected customers: 5-10

**Reality check:**

#### 2a. What Does "Conversion" Mean?
Our model: Conversion = inquiry (not close)
- 500 email × 5-10% = 25-50 inquiries
- Inquiries → discovery calls (conversion #2)
- Discovery calls → proposals (conversion #3)
- Proposals → closes (conversion #4, ~20%)

**Problem:** Warm audiences need fewer conversions steps (they know us), but 5-10% inquiry rate assumes they're already buyers.

#### 2b. P1 Customers Are Wrong Profile
- P1 customer mindset: "I bought your template to use myself"
- Email message: "Want to hire us for $1,200/month writing?"
- Mismatch: They came for DIY, not outsourcing
- Conversion rate for wrong profile: 1-2% (not 5-10%)

#### 2c. Email Sequence Quality Risk
- We have no track record with email sequences
- Freelance writing service offers are complex (need value proof, trust)
- Poor copy = 0.5% conversion (catastrophe)
- Mediocre copy = 2-3% conversion (disappointing)
- Good copy = 5% conversion (requires testing)

**Verdict: ASSUMPTION PARTIALLY FAILS**
- Estimated conversion if list is right audience: 5-10% holds
- Actual conversion with wrong audience: 1-2% (mismatched)
- **Revenue miss: 50-80% vs forecast**

**Financial impact:**
- Forecast: 500 list × 5% = 25 inquiries, 5 customers ($6k M2)
- Reality: 250 list × 2% = 5 inquiries, 1 customer ($1.2k M2)
- **Revenue miss: 80%**

## ASSUMPTION 7: "Warm-First is Lower-Risk Than Cold-First"

**Justification:**
- Warm audience known (we sold to them)
- Conversion proven (5-10% industry benchmarks)
- No new skills required (just email)

**Reality check:**

#### 7a. Concentration Risk
- Warm strategy: All revenue dependent on 1 email list, 1 campaign launch
- Single point of failure: Email list doesn't hit 500, sequence tanks, no revenue
- Recovery: Must pivot to cold, delays first close 4+ weeks
- **Risk: HIGH (single point of failure)**

#### 7b. Validation Speed
- Warm: 1 campaign, 1 data point, wait 2-3 weeks for signal
- Cold: Weekly campaigns, 10 data points by week 5, optimize each week
- Cold gives clearer signal faster
- **Risk: Cold is actually LOWER (faster learning)**

#### 7c. Execution Dependency
- Warm: Requires CEO to grow email list 500, requires conversion to 5%+
- Cold: Requires CEO to research prospects, write good copy, follow up
- Execution difficulty: Similar (both CEO-heavy)
- **Risk: Similar across both**

#### 7d. Market Risk
- Warm: If P1 customers are wrong profile (DIY, not outsource), conversion crashes to 1-2%
- Cold: If B2B SaaS founders don't want service, conversion crashes (but can pivot to different segment)
- Cold is more pivotable (test different segments weekly)
- **Risk: Warm is actually HIGHER (less pivotable)**

**Verdict: ASSUMPTION FAILS**
- Warm-first is lower-risk: ❌ No (single point of failure, less pivotable)
- Cold-first is lower-risk: ✅ Yes (multiple batches, weekly optimization)
- **Risk profile: Cold < Warm**
