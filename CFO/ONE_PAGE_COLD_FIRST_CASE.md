# P2 Strategy: Cold-First Is Better Than Warm-First (One Page)

**Date:** 2026-03-29 | **CFO Investigation:** CYCLE 3 | **Confidence:** HIGH

---

## THE ARGUMENT IN 30 SECONDS

Current plan: Warm-first (email list 500 by April 1, launch sequence April 7)
**Problem:** Email list won't exist. P1 customers are wrong audience. Revenue delayed 2-3 weeks.

**Better plan:** Cold-first (start April 1, first customer April 21-28)
**Why:** Faster, certain, right audience, 6-14 days earlier revenue.

---

## SIX KEY FACTS

| # | Factor | Warm-First | Cold-First | Winner |
|---|--------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **Audience** | P1 template buyers (DIY, $30 budget) | B2B SaaS/agencies (outsource, $1.2k budget) | **Cold** (100% relevant) |
| 2 | **Timeline** | April 7+ launch, May 5+ revenue | April 1 launch, April 21-28 revenue | **Cold** (6-14 days faster) |
| 3 | **Email List Exists?** | No (would need 500 by April 1; realistic 150-300 by May 1) | N/A (doesn't need list) | **Cold** (no dependency) |
| 4 | **Conversion Rate** | 5-10% assumed, 1-2% realistic (wrong audience) | 1-2% cold standard, testable weekly | **Cold** (predictable) |
| 5 | **Testing** | 1 campaign, 1 data point, static | Weekly batches, 10 data points, optimizable | **Cold** (learnable) |
| 6 | **Revenue M1-6** | $27.4k (warm alone) | $27.4k (cold alone) OR $45.4k (cold + warm May 1+) | **Cold** (same or 65% better) |

---

## WARM-FIRST BREAKS (3 Critical Failures)

1. **Email list won't hit 500 by April 1**
   - Current subscribers: ~0 (P1 not launched yet)
   - Realistic by May 1: 150-300
   - Assumption fail = 60% revenue miss

2. **P1 customers are wrong audience for P2**
   - P1: "I need templates to work faster myself" (DIY)
   - P2: "I need someone to write for us" (outsource)
   - Warm conversion drops from 5-10% to 1-2%
   - Assumption fail = 60-80% revenue miss

3. **Can't launch sequence until April 21+ (no list)**
   - April 7 target: Impossible (no subscribers yet)
   - Realistic launch: April 21+ (when list hits 30-50)
   - Timeline fail = 2-3 week delay

---

## COLD-FIRST ADVANTAGES (3 Key Wins)

1. **Starts tomorrow, no dependencies**
   - April 1: CEO identifies 50-100 cold targets
   - April 1-7: Personalized emails sent
   - April 8: First responses
   - April 21: First customer
   - No waiting for P1 email list growth

2. **Right audience from day 1**
   - Target: B2B SaaS founders, agencies, newsletters
   - These people actually hire freelance writers
   - Conversion: 1-2% realistic (not wrong-audience 1-2%)
   - Better fit = less churn, better NPS

3. **Testable every week**
   - Send 20-30 batch Week 1
   - Measure response/inquiry/close rate
   - Adjust copy, targeting, angle Week 2
   - Repeat weekly = can optimize or pivot quickly
   - Warm: 1 campaign, can't optimize

- M2: $2.4k (cold: $1.2k + warm: $1.2k)

## THE DECISION

### APPROVE COLD-FIRST (Primary)

**Execution by March 31:**
- **CEO:** Research 50-100 cold targets, write 3 email versions
- **COO:** Prepare to launch cold outreach April 1 (20-30/week)
- **CTMO:** No changes (Gumroad integration same)

**Keep warm as secondary option:**
- If email list >300 by May 1: Launch warm sequence May 7
- If cold response >1%: Keep cold going (dual channels)
- If cold response <1%: Focus on cold optimization or pivot to P3

**Checkpoint metrics:**
- April 14: Cold response rate >1%? (2-3 responses from ~100)
- April 21: First customer closing?
- May 1: Email list available for warm launch?

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS

Cold-first doesn't just match warm-first. It:
- Starts **6-14 days earlier** (April 21 vs May 5)
- Hits **right audience** (100% vs 30% relevant)
- **Tests faster** (10 data points vs 1)
- **Scales up** (hybrid = 65% more revenue if both work)
- **Risk is lower** (multiple batches, not one-shot campaign)

**Bottom line:** We should have been cold-first all along. Warm is the fallback, not the primary.
