- Sales cycle: 4–6 weeks
- Churn: 0–1%/month
- Single writer scales to 5+ customers without backup
- Pricing: $1,200/month average maintained
- **Month 6 outcome:** 5–6 customers, $6,500+ MRR

### Scenario 2: Base Case (30% Probability) — Current CEO Plan
- Sales cycle: 4 weeks
- Churn: 0% (unrealistic)

- Pricing: $1,200 ARPU
- **Month 6 outcome:** 5 customers, $6,000 MRR

### Scenario 3: Realistic/Downside (50% Probability) — Stress-Tested
- Sales cycle: 8–12 weeks
- Churn: 3–5%/month
- Need backup writer by Month 4–5 (cost impact)
- Pricing: 10% discount to close deals = $1,080 ARPU
- **Month 6 outcome:** 3–4 customers, $2,400–$3,600 MRR

## Cash Flow Sensitivity: Key Risks

### Risk 1: First Customer Churns (Month 4)
**Impact:** Delays 2nd customer acquisition by 4–6 weeks

| Scenario | Month 6 MRR | Month 8 MRR |
|---------|------|------|
| No churn | $3,240 | $5,000+ |
| 1st customer churns at Month 4 | $2,160 | $3,600 |
| **Cash impact** | –$1,080 | –$1,400 |

**Mitigation:** Build customer success protocol by Month 2 (satisfaction guarantee, monthly check-ins)

### Risk 2: Single Writer Can't Scale (Month 5)
**Impact:** Hiring backup writer cost adds ~30% to COGS

Without backup writer: Quality issues → additional churn
With backup writer: Margin compression from 60% → 45%

| Scenario | Month 6 Gross Margin | Month 6 Gross Profit |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Single writer (risky) | 48% | $1,555 |
| Backup writer hired | 45% | $1,460 |
| **Difference** | –3 points | –$95 (but avoids churn risk) |

**Decision point:** Hire backup writer by Month 4 regardless (quality insurance > margin hit)

### Risk 3: Sales Cycle Extends Beyond 12 Weeks
**Impact:** Each 4-week delay = 1 customer pushed into next month

| Sales Cycle | Month 6 Customers | Month 9 Customers |
|--------|---------|---------|
| 8–10 weeks | 3–4 | 4–5 |
| 12–14 weeks | 2–3 | 3–4 |
| 16+ weeks | 1–2 | 2–3 |

**Early warning:** By Month 4, if first customer not yet closed, sales cycle is longer than assumed.
