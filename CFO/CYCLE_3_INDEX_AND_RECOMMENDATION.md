## KEY FINDINGS (EXECUTIVE SUMMARY)

### Finding 1: WRONG AUDIENCE PROBLEM
- **P1 customers:** Template buyers (DIY, $30 budget)
- **P2 buyers:** Agencies/SaaS founders (outsource, $1.2k budget)
- **Mismatch:** 30-50% relevant (warm) vs. 100% relevant (cold)
- **Impact:** Warm conversion 1-2% (not 5-10%)

### Finding 2: EMAIL LIST WON'T EXIST BY APRIL 1
- **Target:** 500 subscribers by April 1
- **Reality:** 0-30 subscribers (P1 not launched yet)
- **Realistic:** 150-300 by May 1
- **Impact:** Can't launch sequence April 7; must wait April 21+

### Finding 3: COLD IS FASTER, NOT SLOWER
- **Warm:** April 7 launch → May 5+ first revenue (36+ days)
- **Cold:** April 1 launch → April 21-28 first revenue (22-30 days)
- **Advantage:** Cold is 6-14 days faster
- **Impact:** Earlier cash flow signal, better Q2 runway

### Finding 4: REVENUE IS SAME OR BETTER
- **Warm-only M1-6:** $27.4k
- **Cold-only M1-6:** $27.4k (same)
- **Cold + Warm hybrid M1-6:** $45.4k (65% more)
- **Upside:** Hybrid strategy available only with cold-first

### Finding 5: COLD IS MORE TESTABLE
- **Warm:** 1 campaign (500 list), hard to optimize
- **Cold:** 10 batches (100-300 emails), easy to optimize weekly
- **Learning:** Cold gives 10 data points; warm gives 1
- **Advantage:** Can pivot quickly if cold doesn't work

### Finding 6: RISK IS LOWER WITH COLD
- **Warm risk:** Single point of failure (email list)
- **Cold risk:** Multiple batches (distributed risk)
- **Downside:** Cold fails = adjust copy. Warm fails = no backup (then cold)
- **Verdict:** Cold is lower-risk than warm

### Finding 7: COLD WORKS FOR B2B SERVICES
- **Evidence:** Copywriting, design, writing services all use cold email (1-3% response proven)
- **Not experimental:** Validated playbook for service businesses
- **Adaptation needed:** Tight audience segmentation (not generic)
