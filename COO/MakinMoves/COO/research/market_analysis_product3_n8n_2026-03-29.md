## Market Gap Analysis

### What's NOT Being Sold Well

**1. Niche-Specific Single-Use-Case Templates (High Quality, Not Bulk)**
- Example: "AI Research Agent" template that saves 10+ hours weekly — this sells because it solves ONE clear problem
- The market rewards specificity. "50 templates for freelancers" will likely outperform "5000 generic templates"

**2. Solopreneur / Freelancer-Specific Automations**
- Bulk packs target "founders, marketers, and scaling teams" — not individual freelancers
- Freelancers have specific workflow needs: client onboarding, invoice follow-ups, proposal automation, project status updates
- **This audience overlaps directly with Product #1 buyers** — natural upsell opportunity

**3. AI-Powered Workflow Templates (Claude/GPT integration focus)**
- Most packs are pre-AI era workflows or vague about AI integration
- Templates that specifically automate Claude or GPT tasks are underrepresented

**4. "Done-For-You Setup" with Documentation**
- Most packs dump JSON files with no context
- Products with setup guides, video walkthroughs, and troubleshooting docs command premium prices

## Pricing Analysis

| Tier | Product Type | Price Range |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Bulk commodity | 1,000–8,000 generic templates | $19–$49 |
| Niche specialized | 5–20 targeted templates with docs | $29–$79 |
| Premium single-workflow | 1 complex workflow, full setup guide | $29–$99 |
| With resell rights | Any of the above + PLR | +$20–$50 premium |

**Pricing recommendation (preliminary):** Position at $39–$49 for 5–10 high-quality, well-documented freelancer-specific workflows. Avoid competing in the $19–$29 bulk range — we can't win on quantity.

## COO Product Hypothesis (Requires CRITIQUE Phase Validation)

**Working Title:** "The Freelancer Automation Pack — 8 n8n Workflows to Run Your Business on Autopilot"

**Concept:** 8 production-ready n8n workflow templates specifically designed for independent freelancers, each with:
- JSON import file
- Plain-English setup guide (no technical jargon)
- What the workflow does and why it saves time
- Customization notes (where to change variables)

**Proposed workflows (8 total, subject to CTMO input):**

1. **New Client Onboarding Automation** — Gmail triggers welcome email sequence, creates project folder, fires Slack/email reminders
2. **Invoice Follow-Up Sequence** — 3-step overdue invoice reminder series with escalating tone
3. **Cold Outreach Drip** — Personalized 3-email sequence for new prospect from LinkedIn/CSV input
4. **Proposal Auto-Follow-Up** — Trigger when proposal sent; auto-follow-up at Day 3 and Day 7 if no reply
5. **Weekly Revenue Report** — Pulls from Gumroad + time tracking tool, emails summary every Monday
6. **Client Meeting Prep Brief** — Reads calendar event, pulls prior email history, generates context brief via Claude
7. **Project Status Update** — Scheduled auto-email to client with this week's progress (template-fill)
8. **New Lead Alert** — Monitors contact form / Gmail label, sends Slack/email ping with lead data

**Market differentiation:** No one is selling n8n workflows specifically for freelancers. All bulk packs target "businesses," "founders," and "marketing teams." This product is the first freelancer-native n8n pack.

**Upsell vector:** Product #1 buyers (AI Prompts for Freelancers) are natural buyers for Product #3. Bundle opportunity at ~$59 (both products).

## Competitive Risks

1. **Build complexity:** Unlike Product #1 (writing prompts) and Product #2 (Notion), n8n workflows require technical build and testing. CTMO involvement required.
2. **Platform dependency:** Buyer needs n8n installed. Free self-hosted version exists, but setup friction is higher than Notion or a PDF.
3. **Market timing:** n8n user base is growing rapidly but still technical-leaning. Mass-market appeal is 12–18 months away.

**COO recommendation:** Prioritize Product #3 after Product #1 and Product #2 are live and generating revenue. Don't delay revenue for a more complex build.
