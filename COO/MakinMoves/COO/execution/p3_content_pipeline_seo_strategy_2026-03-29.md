## 2. Keyword Strategy

### Keyword Research Process

**Step 1: Seed Keywords (per niche)**
Generate 10 broad seed terms per site:
- Site A (Freelancer Tools): "best project management tools for freelancers", "freelancer invoicing software", "time tracking app freelancer"
- Site B (Home Office): "best standing desk under $500", "ergonomic office chair for back pain", "home office setup ideas"
- Site C (AI Tools): "best AI writing tools 2026", "AI tools for small business", "ChatGPT alternatives for business"

**Step 2: Keyword Expansion**
For each seed keyword, use free tools to generate 20-30 variants:
- Ubersuggest: related keywords + questions
- Google "People Also Ask": question-format keywords
- Google autocomplete: type seed + read suggestions
- AnswerThePublic: question clusters
- AlsoAsked.com: related question trees

**Step 3: Keyword Qualification**
Filter all candidates through this matrix:

| Criteria | Target | Tool |
|----------|--------|------|
| Monthly search volume | >100 | Ubersuggest / KW Planner |
| Keyword difficulty | <30 (new site) / <50 (established) | Ubersuggest |
| Commercial intent | Medium to High | Manual assessment |
| Affiliate potential | Product can be recommended | Manual assessment |
| Competition quality | Beatable (thin content, outdated, no real reviews) | Manual SERP analysis |

**Step 4: Keyword Mapping**
Assign each qualified keyword to:
- One specific article
- One article type (buyer's guide, comparison, how-to, listicle)
- One content cluster (pillar topic)
- Priority tier (P1 = highest value, publish first)

### Cluster Model

Each site gets 3-5 content clusters (pillar topics). Each cluster has:
- 1 Pillar article (2,500-3,500 words, targets high-volume keyword)
- 4-6 Supporting articles (1,500-2,000 words, target long-tail keywords)
- All supporting articles link to pillar; pillar links to all supporting articles

**Example — Site A Cluster: "Freelancer Invoicing"**
```
PILLAR: "Best Invoicing Software for Freelancers in 2026" (2,500 words)
├── "FreshBooks vs Wave vs Zoho Invoice — Which Is Best for Solo Freelancers?"
├── "How to Create a Professional Invoice (Free Template + Guide)"
├── "5 Invoicing Mistakes Freelancers Make (and How to Fix Them)"
├── "Freelancer Tax Prep: How Your Invoicing Software Can Save You Hours"
└── "Invoicing for International Clients: Currency, Taxes, and Payment Methods"
```

### Target: 30 Keywords Per Niche

| Tier | Count | Volume | Difficulty | Priority |
|------|-------|--------|------------|----------|
| High-volume (pillar) | 5 | 500-5,000/mo | 20-50 | Month 1-2 |
| Medium (supporting) | 10 | 100-500/mo | 10-30 | Month 1-3 |
| Long-tail (easy wins) | 15 | 50-200/mo | <15 | Month 1 (publish first) |

## 4. Editorial Workflow Checklist

### Assignment Phase
- [ ] Keyword brief created (target keyword, secondary keywords, search intent)
- [ ] Article type selected (buyer's guide, comparison, how-to, listicle)
- [ ] Outline approved by COO
- [ ] Competitor articles analyzed (top 3 SERP results — what to beat)
- [ ] Writer assigned with deadline

### Draft Phase
- [ ] Draft submitted on time
- [ ] Word count meets target (±10%)
- [ ] All required sections present per template
- [ ] Original research/analysis included (not just rehashed competitor content)
- [ ] Tone appropriate for audience and site

### SEO Phase
- [ ] Primary keyword in: title, H1, first 100 words, 2-3 H2s, conclusion
- [ ] Secondary keywords used naturally (3-5 instances)
- [ ] Meta title: ≤60 characters, includes primary keyword, compelling
- [ ] Meta description: ≤155 characters, includes keyword + CTA
- [ ] URL slug: short, keyword-rich, no stop words
- [ ] Image alt tags: descriptive, include keyword where natural
- [ ] Internal links: 3+ to other site articles (cluster-aware)
- [ ] External links: 1-2 to authoritative sources

### Affiliate Integration Phase
- [ ] FTC disclosure at top of article
- [ ] Affiliate links inserted at natural decision points
- [ ] ThirstyAffiliates cloaking applied
- [ ] All affiliate links tested (click through, verify landing page)
- [ ] CTA buttons styled consistently
- [ ] No excessive link density (feels natural, not spammy)

### Publishing Phase
- [ ] Published to WordPress
- [ ] URL submitted to Google Search Console
- [ ] XML sitemap updated
- [ ] Internal links from older articles added
- [ ] Shared on social channels
- [ ] Added to content calendar as "Published"

## 5. SEO Optimization Roadmap

### Month 1: Foundation (Index + Long-Tail Wins)

**Technical SEO:**
- Google Search Console verified for all 3 sites
- XML sitemap submitted
- Robots.txt configured correctly
- Site speed: <3 second load time (test via PageSpeed Insights)
- Mobile responsive confirmed
- SSL active on all pages
- Schema markup: Organization, Article, FAQ, Product Review

**Content SEO:**
- Publish 12 articles (4 per site) targeting long-tail keywords (KD <15)
- Focus on comprehensive, original content that beats current SERP results
- Every article interlinked within its cluster

**Expected results:** Pages indexed within 1-2 weeks. Long-tail keywords may start ranking page 2-3 by month end.

### Month 2-3: Build Authority

**Content:**
- Publish 20-24 articles/month (ramp with first writer)
- Begin pillar articles (higher difficulty keywords)
- Content refreshes on Month 1 articles if not ranking

**Link Building (low effort):**
- Guest posting: pitch 2-3 guest posts/month to relevant blogs
- Directory submissions: submit sites to 10-15 relevant directories
- Resource page outreach: find "best tools" pages, suggest your content
- HARO / Connectively: respond to journalist queries for backlinks

**Expected results:** Some long-tail keywords ranking page 1. Pillar content ranking page 2-3. 100-300 daily visits combined.

### Month 4-6: Climb + Monetize

**Content:**
- 32-36 articles/month (2 writers)
- Focus on medium-difficulty keywords
- Update and expand top-performing articles
- Begin converting traffic with email capture (pop-ups, content upgrades)

**Link Building (moderate effort):**
- Broken link building: find broken links on competitor sites, offer your content as replacement
- Skyscraper technique: find popular articles, create better versions, outreach
- Strategic partnerships: cross-link with complementary (non-competing) sites

**Expected results:** 10-20 keywords on page 1. 500-1,000 daily visits combined. First meaningful affiliate revenue ($100-500/month).

### Month 6+: Dominate + Diversify Revenue

**Content:** 48-60 articles/month (3 writers). Cover entire keyword map.

**Additional revenue streams:**
- Display ads (apply to Mediavine at 50K sessions/month, or Ezoic earlier)
- Sponsored posts ($200-500 per post once traffic is established)
- Email list monetization (product recommendations, newsletter sponsorships)
- Digital product cross-sell (link to P1 Gumroad products from relevant articles)

## 6. Outsourcing Roadmap for Content

### When to Hire First Content Writer

**Trigger:** Jason can't sustain 3 articles/week AND manage P1 + P2. Likely Month 2.

### Writer Requirements (Different from P2 Writers)

Affiliate content writers need specific skills:
- SEO knowledge (keyword placement, meta tags, internal linking)
- Product review experience (authentic, detailed, not generic)
- Affiliate disclosure compliance awareness
- Ability to follow strict templates and formatting

### Cost Per Article at Scale

| Scale | Writer Rate | Editor Rate | Total/Article | Monthly Cost (20 articles) |
|-------|------------|-------------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Jason only | $0 | $0 | $0 | $0 (time cost) |
| 1 writer | $100-150 | $0 (Jason edits) | $100-150 | $2,000-3,000 |
| 2 writers | $100-150 | $25 (part-time editor) | $125-175 | $2,500-3,500 |
| 3 writers + editor | $100-150 | $25 | $125-175 | $5,000-7,000 |

**Break-even calculation:** At $125/article average cost and $5-15 average affiliate revenue per article per month (cumulative), each article pays for itself within 8-25 months. The portfolio compounds — Month 6 articles still earn in Month 12.

### Hiring Timeline

- **Month 2:** Hire Writer #1 (test with 4 articles, ramp to 8-10/month)
- **Month 3:** Evaluate Writer #1 quality + revenue trajectory. Hire Writer #2 if justified.
- **Month 4:** If both writers performing, add part-time editor to free Jason from QA
- **Month 6:** Review: is content investment generating positive ROI? Scale or hold.
