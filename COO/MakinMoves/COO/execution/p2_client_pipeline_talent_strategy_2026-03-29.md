## 2. Client Acquisition Playbook

### Channel 1: Cold Email Outreach (Primary — Week 1-4)

**Target verticals:**
- SaaS startups (Series A/B) that blog but inconsistently
- E-commerce brands needing product descriptions at scale
- Coaches/consultants who need LinkedIn ghostwriting
- Local service businesses needing website copy refresh

**Prospecting process:**
1. Find targets: search LinkedIn (anonymous browsing), Google "[industry] + blog" to find sites with stale content
2. Find decision-maker email: use Hunter.io (free 25/mo) or Apollo.io (free tier)
3. Send cold email (Template below)
4. Follow up at Day 3 and Day 7 if no response
5. If no response after 3 touches → move to "nurture" list

**Cold Email Template (Version A — Pain Point):**
```
Subject: Quick question about [Company]'s blog

Hi [Name],

I noticed [Company]'s blog hasn't been updated since [month]. For businesses in [their industry], that's leaving organic traffic on the table.

I help companies like yours publish 4-8 blog posts per month that actually rank — without adding to your team's workload.

Would it make sense to chat for 15 minutes this week? I can share what's been working for similar [industry] companies.

[Signature — brand name only, not Jason's personal name]
```

**Cold Email Template (Version B — Value Offer):**
```
Subject: Content idea for [Company]

Hi [Name],

I was researching [their industry] and came up with 3 blog post ideas that could drive traffic to [Company]:

1. [Specific topic based on their business]
2. [Specific topic based on their business]
3. [Specific topic based on their business]

I write content for [industry] businesses and would love to help bring these to life.

Worth a quick call this week?

[Signature]
```

**Volume targets:**
- Week 1: 5 emails/day (25 total)
- Week 2: 10 emails/day (50 total)
- Week 3+: 10 emails/day sustained
- Expected response rate: 3–5%
- Expected close rate from responses: 20–30%
- Math: 50 emails → 2 responses → 0.5 clients per week

### Channel 2: Freelancer Platforms (Secondary — Week 2+)

**Platforms to list on:**
- Upwork (create profile, bid on 5 projects/week)
- Contra (free, no fees — good for early traction)
- Fiverr (list writing gig at premium tier)
- PeoplePerHour (UK-focused but good for diversification)

**Pricing on platforms (slightly lower to win first reviews):**
- Blog posts: $200–$350 (vs. $300–$500 direct)
- Email sequences: $150–$250
- Website copy: $300–$500

**Goal:** Win 2–3 platform clients in first month for reviews/social proof, then migrate to direct relationships.

### Channel 3: Content Marketing / Inbound (Month 2+)

- Publish 2 articles/week on Medium or Substack about content strategy, freelancer tips
- Anonymous byline (brand name)
- Each article has soft CTA linking to services page
- Goal: establish authority → inbound leads find us → higher close rate + higher prices

### Channel 4: Referral Program (After First Client)

- Offer: $200 referral bonus per successful client signup
- Communicate to every client at Day 14 follow-up
- Create referral tracking in client spreadsheet
- Expected: 1 referral per 3 clients (33% rate with incentive)

### Channel 5: Agency Partnerships (Month 3+)

- Target: design agencies, web dev shops, marketing agencies that don't have in-house writers
- Offer: white-label writing at wholesale rate ($150–$200/post)
- Volume: 1 agency partner = 5–10 posts/month
- Lower margin but predictable recurring revenue

## 3. Pipeline Management

### Pipeline Stages

```
PROSPECT → CONTACTED → RESPONDED → QUALIFIED → DISCOVERY CALL → PROPOSAL SENT → CLOSED WON / CLOSED LOST
```

### Pipeline Tracking (Notion or Spreadsheet)

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Company | Client business name |
| Contact | Decision maker name + email |
| Source | Cold email / platform / referral / inbound |
| Stage | Current pipeline stage |
| First Contact | Date of initial outreach |
| Last Touch | Date of most recent interaction |
| Next Action | What needs to happen next + when |
| Deal Value | Estimated monthly or project value |
| Close Probability | % likelihood (25% at qualified, 50% at proposal, 75% after verbal yes) |
| Weighted Value | Deal Value × Close Probability |
| Notes | Key info, objections, preferences |

| Stage | Target Time |
|-------|------------|
| Contacted → Responded | 3 days |
| Responded → Qualified | 2 days |
| Qualified → Discovery | 3 days |
| Discovery → Proposal | 1 day |
| Proposal → Close | 5 days |
| **Total cycle:** | **~14 days** |

## 4. Writer Recruitment Strategy

### When to Hire First Writer

**Trigger:** Jason has 2+ active clients AND is spending >20 hours/week on writing (not client management, not sales). At that point, writing is the bottleneck.

**Timeline:** Likely Week 4–6 based on growth trajectory.

### Where to Find Writers

| Source | Quality | Cost | Speed |
|--------|---------|------|-------|
| Upwork (targeted search) | Medium-High | $0.05–$0.15/word | Fast (3-5 days) |
| Twitter/X writing community | High | $0.08–$0.20/word | Medium (1-2 weeks) |
| Contently / nDash | High | $0.15–$0.30/word | Fast |
| Writing subreddits (r/freelanceWriters, r/HireaWriter) | Variable | $0.05–$0.15/word | Fast |
| Referrals from other freelancers | High | Market rate | Slow |
| LinkedIn (job post) | Variable | $0 to post | Medium |

**Recommended first hire source:** Upwork or r/HireaWriter — fast, low-risk, easy to test.

### Writer Job Description

```
FREELANCE CONTENT WRITER — [Brand Name]

We're a growing content agency serving SaaS and tech companies.
Looking for a reliable freelance writer for ongoing blog content.

WHAT YOU'LL WRITE:
- Blog posts (1,500–2,500 words)
- Buyer's guides and comparison articles
- How-to content for technical and business audiences

REQUIREMENTS:
- 2+ years freelance writing experience
- Portfolio with published B2B/SaaS samples
- Reliable turnaround (3-5 business days per article)
- Clean, engaging prose — no AI-generated filler
- Comfortable with SEO basics (keywords, headers, meta descriptions)

PAY:
- $150–$250 per article depending on complexity and length
- Consistent volume: 4–8 articles per month
- Payment within 7 days of client approval
- Bonus: $50 for pieces that receive explicit client praise

TO APPLY:
- Send 2-3 published writing samples
- State your per-article rate
- Describe your typical writing process and turnaround time
```

### Screening Checklist

| Step | Criteria | Pass/Fail |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 1. Portfolio Review | 2+ strong samples in relevant niche | Must pass |
| 2. Writing Test | 500-word test piece on assigned topic ($50 paid) | Must score 7+/10 |
| 3. Communication | Responds within 24 hours, professional tone | Must pass |
| 4. Rate Alignment | Within $150–$250/article range | Must pass |
| 5. Availability | Can commit to 4+ articles/month | Must pass |
| 6. References | 1 reference from past client (optional but preferred) | Preferred |

**Writing Test Scoring (out of 10):**
- Clarity & readability: /3
- Topic accuracy & research depth: /3
- SEO awareness (headers, keyword usage): /2
- Voice & tone match to brief: /2
- Pass threshold: 7/10

### Onboarding Process (3-Day)

**Day 1: Welcome**
- Send Writer Handbook (brand voice, style guide, formatting rules)
- Give access to shared Drive folder
- Walk through Project Brief template
- Assign first paid test article (real client work, with backup plan if quality fails)

**Day 2: First Draft**
- Writer submits first draft
- COO reviews with detailed feedback
- Revision if needed — use this as calibration

**Day 3: Calibration**
- Deliver revised article to client
- Debrief with writer: what went well, what to adjust
- If quality passes: add to active roster
- If quality fails: pay for work done, don't continue

### Writer Retention

- Pay on time, every time (within 7 days)
- Increase rates at 3-month mark if quality is consistent (+$25/article)
- Give writers first right of refusal on new projects in their niche
- Annual rate review
- Build personal relationship: know their goals, refer them to others when you can't use them

### When the Model Breaks
- At 5 clients with 1 writer: writer is at capacity (20 articles/month). Need writer #2.
- At 10 clients: need 2-3 writers + potentially a part-time editor/QA person.
- At 15+ clients: Jason's role shifts entirely to sales + client management. Need operations help.

### Pricing Lever Opportunities
- **Raise retainer to $1,500/mo** after first 3 clients establish value proof
- **Add premium tier:** $2,500/mo for 8 posts + 2 email sequences + monthly strategy call
- **Rush delivery surcharge:** +50% for 24-hour turnaround
- **Industry specialization premium:** +25% for specialized niches (fintech, healthcare, legal)

## 6. 90-Day Hiring Roadmap

### Day 1–30: Jason Solo
- Jason writes all content
- Build pipeline to 2–3 active clients
- Begin collecting writer applications (passive — post job description on Upwork, r/HireaWriter)
- Save 3–5 strong applicant profiles for later

### Day 31–45: Evaluate & Prepare
- Review: is Jason at >20 hrs/week writing? Are clients waiting?
- If YES: activate first contractor hire
- Screen top 3 applicants from saved pool
- Run paid writing tests ($50 each = $150 total investment)

### Day 46–60: First Contractor Onboard
- Bring on Writer #1 (best test performer)
- Start with 2 articles/week, ramp to 4
- Jason shifts: 50% writing, 50% sales + management
- Quality check every deliverable for first 2 weeks

### Day 61–75: Stabilize
- Writer #1 fully ramped and delivering independently
- Jason focuses on acquiring clients 4 and 5
- Begin collecting applications for Writer #2

### Day 76–90: Scale Decision
- Review: revenue, margins, client satisfaction, writer quality
- If growth trajectory holds: hire Writer #2
- If growth stalled: double down on sales before adding cost
- CEO strategic review: continue scaling P2 or redirect resources?

## 7. Risk Management

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Writer quits mid-project | Medium | High | Always have 1 backup writer screened and ready; Jason can cover short-term |
| Quality drops with contractors | Medium | High | QA every piece for first month; detailed style guide; feedback loops |
| Client disputes work quality | Low | Medium | Clear Project Brief; 2-revision policy; escalation to Jason |
| Payment collection issues | Low | Medium | 50% upfront policy; follow-up sequence; small claims for >$500 |
| Market too competitive | Medium | Medium | Specialize in niche (SaaS, fintech); compete on reliability not price |
| Jason burns out managing | Medium | High | Hire operations help at 8+ clients; automate where possible |
