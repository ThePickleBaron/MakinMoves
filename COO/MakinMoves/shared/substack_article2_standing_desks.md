## Quick FAQ

**Will a standing desk really reduce back pain?**
Not by itself. The benefit comes from alternating—sitting 30 minutes, standing 30 minutes. The key is movement and variety, not position.

**How much should I spend?**
The functional difference between a $400 and $500 desk is minimal. The real ROI is using it correctly, not owning a premium model.

**Wobble—how much is acceptable?**
Under 1mm is imperceptible. 1-2mm is acceptable. 3mm+ becomes noticeable and annoying. Uplift and Branch excel here.

**What's the ideal sitting vs. standing ratio?**
Research suggests 1:1 or 2:1 (sit twice as long as you stand). Aim for 2-3 hours standing, split across the day.

**Do I need memory presets?**
Presets are genuinely useful. They eliminate the decision and make standing automatic. Nice to have, not essential.

**Can I use a standing desk with a monitor arm?**
Yes, absolutely. Monitor arms are recommended. They keep your screen at eye level whether sitting or standing.

**How long will a standing desk last?**
With normal use, 5-10 years. Motors have rated cycles (Uplift is 50,000 cycles—about 10 years of daily use). Frames last longer.

**Do I need a standing desk mat?**
Not essential, but helpful. A padded mat ($50–80) reduces fatigue and prevents floor damage.
