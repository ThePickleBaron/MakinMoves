## 1. FreshBooks — Best Overall for Growing Freelancers

**Price:** $15-55/month
**Best For:** Freelancers scaling from part-time to full-time

FreshBooks is the polished, full-featured option. It goes beyond invoicing—you get time tracking, expense capture, basic project management, and financial reporting that makes tax season less painful.

The interface is intuitive. You can create a professional invoice in under 2 minutes. Client portals let customers pay directly from the invoice, and you see payment status in real-time.

Integration breadth is impressive: Stripe, Square, PayPal, and 100+ apps via Zapier. The mobile app is truly functional, so you can invoice on-site.

**What we didn't love:** Pricing jumps significantly at higher tiers. If you invoice fewer than 20 clients per month, Wave might be smarter financially.

**Pricing breakdown:**
- Lite: $15/month (up to 20 invoices/month)
- Plus: $25/month (unlimited invoices, time tracking)
- Premium: $55/month (advanced reports, team collaboration)

## 2. Wave — Best Free Option (Seriously)

**Price:** Free-$20/month
**Best For:** Side hustlers and minimal-overhead freelancers

Wave's free tier is genuinely free—no limitations on invoice volume, clients, or features. You can invoice unlimited clients, accept online payments, track expenses, and file financial reports. This isn't a freemium trap.

The catch? Wave makes money from payment processing (2.2% + $0.30 per transaction), not software subscriptions. So they want you to use the platform.

For freelancers who invoice sporadically, Wave is genuinely all you need.

**What we didn't love:** Mobile app is basic. Reporting is minimal compared to FreshBooks. No time tracking or project management features.

**Pricing breakdown:**
- Free: $0 (unlimited invoices, expense tracking, Wave footer)
- Premium: $20/month (removes Wave footer, priority support)

API access on paid tiers means you can build custom workflows. Developers love this option.

**What we didn't love:** Learning curve is steeper than competitors. The interface feels cluttered if you only need basic invoicing. Mobile app feels disconnected from desktop.

**Pricing breakdown:**

- Standard: $10/month (advanced automations)

## How to Choose: A Simple Framework

**If you're a beginner or part-time freelancer:** Start with **Wave**. It's free, feature-complete for basic invoicing, and you can upgrade later.

**If you're a growing freelancer (5+ clients, regular invoicing):** Choose **FreshBooks**. The time tracking and reporting justify the $25/month cost.

**If you want to automate heavily:** Go **Zoho**. It has the deepest automation and API flexibility.

**If you're a service-based provider:** Pick **HoneyBook**. The proposal-to-payment workflow and client portal are worth the premium.

**If you're a creative professional:** Try **Bonsai**. Contract templates and project workflows are tailored for your work.

## Quick FAQ

**How much faster will I get paid?**
On average, 10-20 days faster. Automated reminders reduce late payments significantly. Online payment options are immediate or next-day versus 5-7 days for checks.

**Do I need to accept online payments?**
You don't need to, but you should. Clients prefer it. You can set payment terms and include payment options without forcing online payment.

**Can I use free invoicing software forever?**
Yes. Wave and Zoho have solid free tiers. Both make money from payment processing (2.2% + $0.30 per transaction). As your volume grows, that fee adds up.

**Can I send recurring invoices automatically?**
FreshBooks, Zoho, and Bonsai support recurring invoices. Wave and HoneyBook do not.

**Do these tools integrate with accounting software?**
Yes. FreshBooks has built-in accounting features. Wave, Zoho, and Bonsai integrate with QuickBooks Online and Xero.
