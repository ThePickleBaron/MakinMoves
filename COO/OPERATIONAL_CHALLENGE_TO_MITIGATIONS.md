## MITIGATION #1: Writer Hire by Day 25

### What CEO Says
"If articles < 3/week for 2 consecutive weeks, hire writer by Day 25"

### Your Reality Check

**Is Day 25 realistic?** NO.
```
Day 15: "We need a writer"
Day 15-16: Post job, collect resumes (1 day)
Day 17-18: Review applications (2 days)
Day 19-20: Interview (2 days)
Day 21-22: Sample test + evaluation (2 days)
Day 23-24: Contract, onboarding docs (2 days)
Day 25: Writer's FIRST day (no production yet)
Day 26-31: Writer produces first article (ramping up)
Day 35: First article from writer hits queue (first real help)

Reality: Hiring takes until Day 35-40. Production help = Day 40+.
```

**Timeline problem:** By Day 25, you've already been at 4 articles/week for 25 days straight. Founder is fried. Hiring a new person is MORE stressful, not less (training burden + coordination overhead).

### Execution Trap #1A: Training Cost
**New writer needs:**
- Voice/style guide (read all 15 articles founder wrote)
- Topic selection process (understand founder's niche research)
- Quality bar (what does "good enough" mean?)
- Tools setup (Notion, Airtable, email, whatever system founder uses)

**Training time:** 5-10 hours of founder time, exactly when founder is most burned out (Days 22-31).

**Your job:** Plan this training BEFORE Day 22. Pre-write documentation + guides by Day 10 so onboarding is 2-3 hours, not 10.

### Execution Trap #1B: "First Hire Might Be Bad"
**Reality:** 40-50% chance first hired writer is below quality threshold.

**Scenarios:**
- Scenario A (30%): Writer is great, matches founder's quality, saves the day
- Scenario B (40%): Writer is OK, produces 80% of founder's quality, founder spends 5-10 hours/week revising
- Scenario C (30%): Writer is weak, produces 50% of founder's quality, founder fires them by Week 4

**You need backup:** CEO should have 2-3 writer backups identified + pre-vetted by Day 10.

**Your job:**
1. Create "writer job description" by Day 2 (specific to your niche, not generic)
2. Pre-vet 5-10 writers by Day 10 (don't wait until Day 15 when crisis hits)
3. Have "Writer B" and "Writer C" ready to onboard if Writer A underperforms
4. Set explicit quality threshold: "If revisions needed on >20% of articles, terminate Week 2"

### Execution Trap #1C: Budget Contingency
**Cost:** $400-600/week × 4 weeks = $1,600-2,400

**Problem:** CFO stress test shows P2 needs $10K runway for realistic timeline. If $2.4K goes to marginal writer in Month 1, runway becomes $7.6K (tighter).

**Your job:** Before hiring, confirm with CEO/CFO:
- "Do we have $2.4K in contingency for writer?"
- "If writer is bad, do we have budget to hire Writer B?"

### VERDICT: Mitigation #1 is Necessary But Risky
**Recommendation:**
- Pre-vet writers now (Days 1-5), don't wait until Day 15
- Have Writer B + Writer C ready
- Lock quality bar (70/100+ on checklist)
- Plan training docs by Day 8 (reduce Jason training burden)
- Budget for writer contingency ($2-3K, not $1.6K)

**If CEO skips these, writer hire becomes a crisis, not a solution.**

## MITIGATION #2: Daily Monitoring (Weeks 1-3)

### What CEO Says
"I'll call founder every day if energy < 6/10. If detected, activate scope reduction."

### Your Reality Check

**Is daily energy reporting reliable?** NO.
```
Day 5: Founder reports 9/10 (launch high, probably lying)
Day 10: Founder reports 7/10 (honeymoon phase, not yet real stress)
Day 15: Founder reports 7/10 (stress mounting, not admitting it yet)
Day 20: Founder reports 5/10 (finally honest, but burnout already 50%)
Day 25: Founder reports 3/10 (crisis mode, too late to prevent)
```

**Problem:** Founder's honest self-report comes too late. By Day 20, content quality is already dropping, energy is half-spent.

### Execution Trap #2A: Detection Lag
**Better indicators than "energy level":**
- Email response time (from 2 hours to 12+ hours = burnout signal)
- Standup brevity (goes from 3-min detailed to 1-min vague = tired)
- Content quality (articles go from thorough to rushed = overload)
- Missed deadlines (first missed deadline = warning sign)
- Tone (becomes flat, less enthusiastic = stress)

**CEO monitors energy. Should monitor quality + timeliness + tone.**

**Your job:**
1. Track content production metrics daily: articles on schedule? quality stable? deadlines met?
2. Report to CEO: "Day 10: 3 articles on track, quality good, tone energized" or "Day 10: 2 articles late, quality declining, tone flat"
3. Trigger scope reduction at Day 12 (not Day 20), based on objective metrics

### Execution Trap #2B: Scope Reduction Is Reactive
**CEO's plan:** Detect burnout (Day 20) → Schedule meeting (Day 20) → Plan reduction (Days 20-21) → Implement (Day 22)

**Problem:** Decision-making during crisis is messy. Founder is exhausted, CEO is stressed, COO is caught in middle.

**Better:** Pre-plan scope reduction by Day 8.
```
Scenario 1: If articles are on track (4/week) by Day 12
  → Continue, monitor for next gate

Scenario 2: If articles are behind (2-3/week) by Day 12
  → Immediately activate Plan B: reduce to 2 articles/week, adjust forecasts, hire writer

Don't wait to Day 20. Make the decision proactively at Day 12 when data is available.
```

**Your job:**
1. By Day 8, prepare TWO execution plans: High Scope (4 articles/week) + Low Scope (2 articles/week)
2. Give both to founder + CEO by Day 9
3. On Day 12 (first metric review), CEO chooses which plan based on actual production data
4. No crisis meeting. Decision is automatic based on pre-written plans.

### Execution Trap #2C: Daily Calls Are Overhead
**CEO says:** "I'll call founder every day, 15 min"
**Reality:** 15 min per day × 21 days = 5.25 hours of CEO time in Weeks 1-3

**Problem:** CEO has 3 other roles (COO, CTMO, CFO to manage). 5+ hours of daily calls is a distraction.

**Better approach:** Async daily pulse
```
Founder sends 2-min daily Slack message (before bed, Day 1):
  "Day 1: 3 articles completed, quality 8/10, energy 9/10, no blockers"

Founder sends 2-min daily Slack message (before bed, Day 2):
  "Day 2: 2 articles completed, quality 7/10, energy 8/10, struggling with niche research"

Founder sends 2-min daily Slack message (before bed, Day 12):
  "Day 12: 1 article completed, quality 6/10, energy 5/10, burnout signals"

CEO reads async (2 min total), spots burnout signals by Day 12, triggers Plan B immediately.
```

**Your job:**
1. Set up daily Slack pulse template for founder
2. CEO reads async, doesn't call unless urgent
3. Founder doesn't have overhead of live calls (can sleep, work, rest)
4. More data collected, faster decision-making possible

### VERDICT: Mitigation #2 Has Right Idea, Wrong Execution
**Recommendation:**
- Replace "daily energy calls" with "async daily metric pulse" (articles, quality, energy 1-10, blockers)
- CEO reviews async, triggers decisions on Day 12 (not Day 20)
- Pre-write decision plans by Day 8 (scope reduction in 1 hour, not crisis meeting)
- Monitor quality + timeliness, not just energy feeling

**If CEO skips async setup, daily monitoring becomes burden, not value.**

## MITIGATION #3: Scope Reduction (Weeks 4-6)

### What CEO Says
"By Week 4 (Day 22), reduce from 4 articles/week to 2-3 articles/week"

### Your Reality Check

**Is Week 4 the right time?** NO. Week 2 is better.
```
Week 1: 4 articles/week at 8-9/10 energy (fine)
Week 2: 4 articles/week at 7-8/10 energy (fine, but fatigue mounting)
Week 3: 4 articles/week at 5-6/10 energy (slog phase starting, quality drops)
Week 4: 4 articles/week at 3-4/10 energy (crisis burnout, content rushed)

By Week 4, damage is done. Founder has been at high intensity for 21 days.
Even with scope reduction, recovery takes 2-3 weeks (Days 22-35).

Better: Reduce by Week 2 (Day 12), before crisis hits.
```

### Execution Trap #3A: Too Late to Prevent Burnout
**Timeline of burnout:**
```
Days 1-7: Launch energy masks fatigue (founder thinks they're fine)
Days 8-14: Fatigue accumulates, quality starts declining (founder in denial)
Days 15-21: Burnout approaching, short-tempered, mistakes increase (founder admits it's hard)
Days 22-28: Crisis burnout, mistakes spiral, content quality tanks (too late to prevent)
```

**Scope reduction by Day 22 doesn't prevent burnout. It helps recovery, but crisis already happened.**

**Better:** Pre-emptive scope reduction by Day 12.

### Execution Trap #3B: Revenue Impact Not Calculated
**From CFO stress test:** Email list growth is critical for P1 launch.

**If scope reduces from 4 to 2 articles/week in Week 4:**
```
Week 1-3: 12 articles (each → 5-10 new subscribers)
Week 1-3 email growth: 12 × 7 = 84 new subscribers

If reduced to 2 articles/week for Weeks 4-6:
Week 4-6: 6 articles (vs. 12 if no reduction)
Lost email growth: 6 × 7 = 42 subscribers

P1 launch (Week 4) impact:
With 150 email subscribers → 300-500 launch visitors → $250-500 revenue
With 100 email subscribers → 200-300 launch visitors → $150-300 revenue
Lost revenue: ~$100-200
```

**Your job:** Before scope reduction, calculate:
1. What's the cost in lost email subscribers?
2. What's the cost in lost P1 launch revenue?
3. Is preventing founder burnout worth $100-200 lost revenue?
4. If yes, approve. If unsure, ask CEO.

### Execution Trap #3C: "2-3 Articles/Week" Is Still Too Much
**Research on sustainable output by stress level:**
```
Low stress (8-10/10): 4-5 articles/week sustainable
Medium stress (6-7/10): 2-3 articles/week sustainable
High stress (4-5/10): 1-2 articles/week safe
Burnout (0-3/10): 0 articles/week (need break)
```

**Week 4 context:** By Week 4, founder is in high-stress territory (4-5/10 energy).
- Sustainable output: 1-2 articles/week (not 2-3)
- If "reduce to 2-3 articles/week" happens, founder is STILL overloaded

**Better:** Reduce to 1-2 articles/week by Day 12 (preventive).

### VERDICT: Mitigation #3 Is Necessary But Poorly Timed
**Recommendation:**
1. Move scope reduction from Week 4 → Week 2 (Day 12, not Day 22)
2. Reduce to 1-2 articles/week (not 2-3)
3. Pair with writer hire (so new articles are replaced with writer articles, not lost)
4. Calculate revenue impact before reducing (founder needs to see trade-off)
5. Plan for 2-3 week recovery period (if scope reduced Day 12, recovery by Day 28-35)

**If CEO skips this, founder burns out anyway by Day 22.**
