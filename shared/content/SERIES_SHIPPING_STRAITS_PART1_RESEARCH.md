## Part 1: Tanker Shipping Cycles 101

### Normal Cycle (Pre-2024)
Tanker shipping (especially crude carriers) is driven by:
1. **Demand cycle** (global oil consumption, seasonal, geopolitical oil flow reroutes)
2. **Supply cycle** (new ship deliveries, demolition of old ships, vessel utilization)
3. **Distance cycle** (when supplies are far from demand, longer routes = more ton-miles = higher rates)

Typical cycle: 5-7 years
- Trough (oversupply, rates ~$10K/day)
- Expansion (demand up, rates $30-50K/day)
- Peak (supply tight, rates $80-150K/day)
- Contraction (new supply delivered, rates collapse)

**Key point:** Shipping rates = distance × demand ÷ supply

## Part 2: The Strait of Hormuz Disruption (2024-2025)

### What's Happening
- **The Strait:** ~30% of world's seaborne oil passes through (Hormuz, between Iran & Oman)
- **The Risk:** Iranian attacks, Houthi strikes, potential full closure
- **The Impact:** Ships now routing around Cape of Good Hope (Africa) instead of Suez/Suez
  - Normal route (Hormuz → Suez): ~20,000 km, 30 days
  - Cape route (via Africa): ~25,000 km, 40-50 days
  - **5,000 km longer = 20-25% longer voyage = 20-25% more ton-miles**

### Why This Matters for Tanker Stocks

**Short-term (2024-2025):** Rates SPIKE because
- Same oil demand, but ships travel 20% longer
- Same number of ships carrying less oil (long distances = slow utilization turnover)
- Supply-side tight (fewer effective ships for demand)

**Result:** Tanker rates 2-3x normal during disruption period
- Stock like **DHT (Ardmore Tankers)**, **AET (Atlantica)**, **INSW (Insperity)** etc. surge on rates

## Part 3: The Supply Chain Cascade (The Real Divergence)

### What Happens During Extended Closure

**Scenario:** Strait stays closed (partial or full) for 6+ months

1. **Demand Backup:** Oil can't ship east via normal route
   - Refineries in Singapore, China, Japan face supply constraints
   - Producers accumulate oil in silos (storage full)
   - Wells shut down because storage is full (no place to put oil)

2. **Inventory Depletion:** Oil ALREADY in transit or stored deplets
   - Supply lines to Asia hit lows
   - Prices spike for Asian markets (regional premium emerges)
   - Demand for SPOT shipments explodes

3. **Silo Crisis:** Producers (Saudi, Iraq, Russia) face existential problem
   - Can't pump, can't store, can't ship
   - Either: Shut wells down (lost revenue) OR destroy infrastructure with attacks
   - More attacks → longer closure → deeper crisis

### The Tanker Stock Implication

**During closure:** Rates are high but unstable (closure could end, volatility)

**6 months before reopening is clear:** Massive divergence forms
- **Market assumes:** Strait reopens, rates normalize, tanker stocks crash
- **Reality:** Silos are FULL of oil, wells are shut down, demand is suppressed

**When Strait Reopens:**
- Initial assumption: Oil floods market, rates collapse
- **Actual outcome:** Backlog is massive
  - Silos need to empty (explosive demand for ships)
  - Shut wells restart (ramping production into a full supply chain)
  - Demand outstrips ship supply for 6-12 months after reopening
  - **Rates stay elevated AFTER the strait opens, not collapse**

### The Divergence Play

**Market narrative:** "Strait closing = tankers rally, reopening = crash"

**Reality:** "Strait reopening = tankers experience a SECOND rally because silos are full and wells are starting up"
