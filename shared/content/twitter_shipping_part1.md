# Twitter Thread: "The Tanker Cycle Wall Street Missed"

30% of the world's oil moves through one strait. When that strait gets risky, shipping rates don't just spike—they create a hidden divergence.

Here's the play.

---

Normal tanker cycle: 5-7 years of supply/demand equilibrium. Ships move oil from producers to refineries. When demand is high and ships are scarce, rates explode ($100K+/day). When supply floods, rates crash to $15K/day.

Simple cycle, repeatable, knowable.

Then: geopolitical shock.

---

The Strait of Hormuz handles ~30% of seaborne oil. When risks rise (attacks, threats, uncertainty), ships reroute around Africa via Cape of Good Hope.

Distance changes:
- Normal route (Suez): 20,000 km, 30 days
- Cape route: 25,000 km, 45 days

The same oil now takes 50% longer to ship.

---

What happens immediately:
1. Fewer ships available (all ships are 50% slower on long routes)
2. Demand for shipping explodes (same oil, more ton-miles needed)
3. Rates SPIKE: $60K → $150K/day

Tanker stocks (DHT, EURN, FRO) rally hard. Everyone sees it as temporary.

---

Here's where the divergence forms:

6-12 months into route disruption, the real problem emerges:

**Silos are full.**

Producers can't empty storage because the route is blocked. Wells shut down. Supply chain backs up. Eastern refineries face scarcity.

---

Market narrative at month 6: "When the strait reopens, rates crash."

Reality: When the strait reopens, DEMAND EXPLODES.

Silos have been accumulating oil for 6+ months. Wells are offline. Refineries are running low. When the route opens, all that oil needs to move simultaneously.

Rates don't crash for 12+ months. They stay elevated.

---

The play: Buy tanker stocks when the market prices in "temporary rate spike" but BEFORE the market understands the silo problem.

Position: DHT, EURN, FRO before rates peak
Exit: When 6-month outlook shows "silos at capacity, demand will explode on reopening"
Re-enter: Before the reopening spike becomes obvious

---

This is how divergence becomes conviction.

The cycle repeats, but the disruption changes the timescale. Knowing that changes the position.
