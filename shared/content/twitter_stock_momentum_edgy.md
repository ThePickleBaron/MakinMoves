# Twitter Thread: "The Stock Up 300% Is About to Crash (Unless Fundamentals Say Otherwise)"

Every market cycle: One stock goes VERTICAL.

"Best performing stock of the year!"
"Can't go lower!"
"Institutional buying!"

Then it crashes 40% in 3 months.

Here's why, and how to tell which ones will survive.

---

The lie: "Stock up 300% = strong company."

The truth: "Stock up 300% = retail FOMO > fundamentals."

When price runs that far that fast, it's because retail is buying an idea, not cash flows.

Check this: Does the stock's revenue growth match its price growth?

Stock up 300% but revenue only up 40%? = Crash incoming.
Stock up 300% and revenue up 250%? = Correction then higher.

---

Here's the mechanic:

A stock rallies on some legitimate catalyst (earnings beat, new product, whatever). Early institutions load up. Stock goes up 50%.

Then FOMO.

Late retail (seeing friends get rich) piles in. Stock goes up another 250%. 

Now you have 100M shares held by bagholders who bought at $80. Stock is at $400. They're thinking: "I got in at the peak."

Someone's gonna sell.

---

Three types of 300% movers:

**Type 1 (Crash to -70%):**
P/S ratio 50x+ (vs peers at 10x). Revenue growth 20% but price growth 300%. No competitive moat. Cult stock. DUMP.

**Type 2 (Correction -30%, then higher):**
P/S at 20x (vs peers 10x). Revenue growth 150%. Earnings accelerating. Real demand, just overheated. HOLD through dip.

**Type 3 (Higher from here):**
P/S at 20x but growing into it (EPS growth 200%+). Expanding TAM. Network effects building. Moat widening. POSITION for dip, add on weakness.

---

The mistake: Treating them all the same.

Market sees "stock is up 300%" = "it will crash."

But Type 2 and 3 stocks RECOVER and go higher. You only make money if you buy the dip and identify which type BEFORE the correction.

---

How to tell the difference in 5 minutes:

1. Check P/S ratio (Price ÷ Annual Sales per Share)
   - Compare to industry peers
   - Stock at 50x peers? = Type 1 (danger)
   - Stock at 20x peers? = Type 2/3 (investigate further)

2. Check revenue growth last 4 quarters
   - Is growth ACCELERATING or decelerating?
   - Accelerating = demand real, just ahead of fundamentals
   - Decelerating = hype play, dump incoming

3. Check earnings (not just revenue)
   - EPS growing even faster than revenue? = Real profitability, Type 3
   - EPS flat while revenue up? = Unprofitable growth play, Type 1

4. Check competitive moat
   - Network effects? Switching costs? Brand dominance?
   - Strong moat = survives correction
   - No moat = crash and stay crashed

---

Example play:

Stock XYZ up 250% in 6 months. Peers trading at 10x sales. XYZ at 35x sales.

BUT: Revenue up 180%, EPS up 220%, market expanding, moat building.

Market assumes crash. You assume correction + recovery. BUY THE DIP.

---

The edge: Most traders buy momentum, exit on "correction signal."

You buy the correction on fundamentals. Hold through the recovery.

That's where the 500% total return comes from. Not the original spike. The RECOVERY after everyone exits thinking it's dead.
