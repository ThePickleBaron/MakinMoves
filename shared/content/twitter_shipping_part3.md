# Twitter Thread: "How to Track the Tanker Play (Data Points You Need)"

Part 1: Rates spike on disruption.
Part 2: Silos fill, creating a second spike on reopening.
Part 3: Here's HOW to track it and stay ahead of consensus.

---

The Data Layer:

You don't need secret sources. Public data is there if you know where to look. Track these 4 things:

1. **Vessel Tracking (KPLER / Vortexa)**
   - Volume of ships going around Africa vs through Suez
   - When Cape route volume is >50% of normal, silo problem is forming
   - KPLER also shows floating storage (oil sitting in tankers = storage full on land)

2. **Tanker Rates (Clarkson Index)**
   - VLCC (very large crude carriers) spot rates
   - Aframax rates (smaller regional tankers)
   - When rates are high but declining despite disruption ongoing = market missing silo problem

3. **Production & Storage (EIA, OPEC reports)**
   - Middle East strategic reserves filling?
   - Production cuts announced?
   - Refineries running at reduced rates (supply constraint)

4. **Stock Signals (Earnings Calls)**
   - What are tanker CEOs saying about "sustainability of rates"?
   - Are they buying back stock at peak rates or holding cash? (= CEO confidence)
   - Are they guiding for higher dividends? (= rates expected to stay high)

---

The Weekly Tracking Framework:

**Every Friday, spend 10 min checking:**

1. KPLER: What % of oil is routing via Cape? (Baseline: 15-20%. >40% = severe disruption)
2. Clarkson: Are VLCC rates up, down, or stable?
3. EIA: Any new production/storage data?
4. Tanker stock news: Any announcements on rate outlook?

If silos are filling AND rates are falling = DIVERGENCE SIGNAL = opportunity

---

The Signals That Matter:

**Green light (Buy signal):**
- Silo capacity >85% (KPLER)
- Rates declining despite disruption ongoing
- Production cuts announced
- Tanker stocks selling off on "reopening soon" narrative

**Red light (Sell signal):**
- Silos below 50% capacity
- Rates sustaining 3+ months after disruption normalized
- New refinery capacity added in Asia (reduces backlog)
- OPEC increases production (demand strong, no supply crisis)
