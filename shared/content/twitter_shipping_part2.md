# Twitter Thread: "The Oil Crisis Wall Street Can't See Yet"

Yesterday I talked about tanker rates spiking when routes get disrupted.

Today: the silo problem that creates the REAL divergence. This is where the money is.

---

Here's the constraint nobody mentions:

Oil producers (Saudi, Russia, Iraq, Iran) don't have infinite storage. Silos and tanks fill up. When they're full, you can't pump. You have to stop.

That's a problem when your export route is blocked.

---

Scenario: Hormuz route gets risky (Houthis, Iranian threats). Ships reroute to Cape. But the slow Cape route means supply piles up in Middle East ports.

Silos fill.
And fill.
And fill.

---

After 6-8 months of disruption, silos hit capacity. Producers face a choice:

1. Shut down production entirely (lose revenue, destroy infrastructure)
2. Keep pumping, overflow into ocean (ecological disaster, financially insane)
3. Cut wells off (same as 1)
4. Hope the route reopens soon (bet against their own security)

Most shut down or cut production.

---

Now the supply chain breaks:
- Eastern refineries (China, Singapore, Japan) are starved for oil
- Prices spike in Asian markets
- Global demand is suppressed (refiners cutting runs because supply is tight)

The market sees: "Low global demand → tanker rates will fall"

Wrong.

---

What's actually happening:
- Silos are FULL upstream (Middle East producers)
- Refineries are STARVED downstream (Asia)
- The constraint is PHYSICAL (storage) not demand

When the Strait reopens, all that backlogged oil FLOODS through at once.

---

Rates don't crash. They spike AGAIN.

Why?
- All the backed-up oil needs to move NOW
- Ships were slow (they're still on long routes)
- Demand hasn't changed; supply has just restarted

For 6-12 months after reopening, rates stay elevated or go higher.

---

The Divergence Play:

Month 0-3: Disruption announced, rates spike. Everyone knows it. Stock surges.

Month 4-6: Silo problem becomes visible to insiders. Rates are still elevated. Most traders assume peak. Divergence forms.

Month 7-9: Reopening becomes possible. Market prices in "rates will crash." Stock gets sold.

Month 10-12: Strait actually reopens. Rates don't crash—they spike again. Stock rallies hard. Bagholders from month 7-9 FOMO back in.

---

How to trade this:

**Position 1 (Month 0-3):** Buy tanker stocks when disruption news hits. Ride the spike. Exit at 50% gain (quick money).

**Position 2 (Month 5-7):** Re-buy AFTER the smart money has exited on "reopening soon" narrative. Silos are full; demand will explode. Hold for the second rally.

This is macro thesis + divergence timing = outsized returns.

---

Track these signals:
- KPLER data: Are silos in Middle East at >90% capacity?
- EIA reports: Are Asian strategic reserves at lows?
- Tanker rates: Are they declining while you'd expect spike? (=supply backlog, not demand weakness)
- Production cuts: Are Saudi/Russia officially cutting? (Signals silo problem)
