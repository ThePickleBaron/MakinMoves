## Email 2: The Framework (Part 1) — Divergence Detection

**Subject:** Why the market misprices everything

**Body:**

The most profitable trades I've made share one pattern:

**The market prices a leading indicator. I price the lagging one.**

Time is what converts "you're wrong" into "you're right."

Example: Housing thesis I'm short right now.

**What the market sees:**
"New apartment lease rents down 6% in 4 years. Housing is solved. Buy XHB [homebuilders]."

**What the data shows:**
"Renewal rents up 2.9% year-over-year. New leases are 5-8% of market. Renewals are 92-95%. Gap closes in 12-16 months, closed *upward*."

Same market. Opposite conclusions. The difference is what I trade.

**The Divergence Framework (Step 1):**

1. **Find the narrative.** What does everyone believe?
2. **Find the data.** What does the data actually show?
3. **Find the lag.** How long before the lagging indicator catches the leading one?
4. **Quantify the gap.** If new leases are -6% and renewals are +2.9%, the gap is 9 points closing in 12-16 months.
5. **Position for convergence.** You're not betting on a crash. You're betting on math.

The framework doesn't predict. It just says: "This gap cannot hold. Time will close it. Which direction?"

In housing, the data says rents go up (or stay flat), not down. So I'm short homebuilders and bullish on existing real estate (because renewals are your cash flow).

**Next email:** I'll break down how to *structure* the trade once you have conviction.

Conviction comes first. Structure comes second. Most traders reverse this and lose money.

## Email 3: The Framework (Part 2) — Conviction Structuring

**Subject:** How big to position when you're sure

**Body:**

Yesterday I walked you through divergence detection: find the gap between narrative and data, quantify the lag, position for convergence.

Today: How to *size* that position.

Most people use portfolio theory. I use conviction.

**Conviction Level 1 (Hypothesis):**
- You've seen some data
- You have 2-3 counter-arguments
- You haven't stress-tested the thesis
- Position size: 5-10% of conviction allocation
- Example: "Hmm, rents might be higher later"

**Conviction Level 2 (Thesis):**
- You've quantified the divergence (9-point gap, 12-16 month lag)
- Counter-arguments are weak
- You've thought about what breaks the thesis
- Position size: 20-30%
- Example: "The data shows renewals are up. New leases are down. They converge upward."

**Conviction Level 3 (Tested):**
- You've held the thesis through volatility
- Market moved against you, thesis stayed intact
- You've refined entry, exits, and risk management
- Position size: 40-50%
- Example: "I'm short housing because supply is exhausted and BLS rents are at all-time high. I've held this 8 weeks, thesis is intact, I'm increasing size."

**Here's what most traders do wrong:**

They size first ("I'll put 50% in this"), then convince themselves of the thesis. By the time doubt creeps in, they're underwater and hopeful.

**Here's what I do:**

Size matches conviction. If I'm not sure, position is small. If thesis is tested and intact, position is bigger.

**Your next step:**

Pick a macro theme you believe in (supply/demand, rate environment, sentiment divergence, sector rotation, whatever). Quantify the divergence from consensus. Then ask: "Am I 50% sure? 80% sure? 20% sure?" That percentage is your position size (as a % of conviction allocation).

Conviction drives size. Size doesn't drive conviction.

The market will test your thesis. Make sure you sized it so you can afford to be right.

—Jason
