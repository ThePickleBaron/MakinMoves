## Thread 2: Technical + Fundamental Mashup

**Tweet 1:**
Most traders split into camps: "technicians" vs. "fundamentalists."

This is a false dichotomy.

Technical analysis = price/volume patterns (what the market is *doing*)
Fundamental analysis = thesis + data (what the market *should* be doing)

The divergence between these two is where conviction trades are born.

**Tweet 2:**
Example: Housing thesis I'm short.

**Fundamentals say:** Supply wave exhausted, construction starts down 74%, BLS rents at all-time high, market is mispriced for relief.

**Technicals say:** Housing ETFs (XHB, IYR) broke support at $78 and $52 respectively. Volume on breakdown is accelerating.

Both point the same direction. Conviction increases.

**Tweet 3:**
Where technicals and fundamentals *disagree* = risk.

If fundamentals say "short" but technicals show support holding and volume weak on breakdown = thesis under pressure.

Could be early. Could be wrong.

This is why you size according to thesis conviction, not technical signals alone.

**Tweet 4:**
The framework:

1. Start with thesis (macro data + divergence)
2. Confirm with technicals (price/volume patterns)
3. If both agree = higher conviction, bigger size
4. If they disagree = lower conviction, smaller size or wait
5. Set OCO stops at thesis break level (not technical support)

Thesis > technicals. Always.

**Tweet 5:**
Your thesis is your conviction.
Your technicals are confirmation.
Your stops are your thesis break level.

When thesis breaks, exit. Don't wait for technicals to confirm a loss. By then, the divergence is gone and you're holding the market's new "correct" price.

Discipline > prediction.

## Thread 3: The Cascade Effect

**Tweet 1:**
Institutional money moves slow. Market prices slow.

But once the cascade starts, it accelerates.

Here's how divergence cascades from first movers to herd:

**Tweet 2:**
Stage 1: Divergence (you see it, few others do)
- Thesis is clear to independent thinkers
- Price hasn't moved
- Position size: Medium conviction, measured entry

Stage 2: Early adoption (smart money wakes up)
- First institutional players start positioning
- Volume picks up
- Price moves 10-20%
- Position size: Increase if thesis intact

Stage 3: Acceleration (FOMO hits)
- Everyone's watching now
- Volume spikes
- Price moves 30-50%
- Position size: This is when technicals confirm. Don't add.

**Tweet 3:**
Stage 4: Normalization (thesis becomes consensus)
- What was "contrarian" is now "obvious"
- New price reflects old thesis
- Your thesis is now the market's baseline
- Exit: 60-80% target on conviction trades. Let 20-40% ride.

**Tweet 4:**
The key to cascade trading:

1. Enter at Stage 1 (size: 30-40% of conviction)
2. Add at Stage 2 if thesis intact (size: 30-40% more)
3. Hold at Stage 3 (don't FOMO add, thesis is now consensus)
4. Exit at Stage 4 (thesis becomes the market's new floor)

You don't need to call the exact top. You just need to exit before the cascade exhausts.

**Tweet 5:**
Most traders miss cascades because they:
- Wait for "confirmation" (it's Stage 3 by then, 70% of move is gone)
- Don't have thesis conviction (can't tell Stage 1 from noise)
- Can't hold thesis through doubt (exit at Stage 2 when volatility spikes)
- Exit at the top (there's no top until consensus)

Start with thesis. Trust the data. Let time prove you right.

## Thread 4: Conviction Structuring

**Tweet 1:**
How do you size a conviction trade?

Not by portfolio allocation %. Not by Kelly Criterion. Not by some risk management formula.

By thesis conviction.

Here's my framework:

**Tweet 2:**
Conviction Level 1 (Hypothesis stage):
- Data is suggestive, not conclusive
- Thesis has 1-2 counter-arguments that matter
- Position size: 5-10% of conviction allocation
- Stop: At thesis break level
- Example: "Rents could fall" (not yet committed)

Conviction Level 2 (Thesis stage):
- Data is clear, counter-arguments are weak
- Multiple data points confirm direction
- Position size: 20-30% of conviction allocation
- Stop: At thesis break level
- Example: "Rents will be higher in 12 months" (committed but not aggressive)

**Tweet 3:**
Conviction Level 3 (Cascade stage):
- Data is overwhelming, thesis is battle-tested
- You've held through volatility, thesis is intact
- Position size: 40-50% of conviction allocation
- Stop: At thesis break level
- Example: "This is a 3-5 year structural trade" (maximum conviction)

**Tweet 4:**
Your conviction should match your position size, not vice versa.

If you're "kind of sure" = small position.
If you're "very sure" = medium position.
If you're "absolutely certain (thesis tested, data clear, thesis intact through volatility)" = large position.

Most traders do the opposite. They size first, then convince themselves of thesis.

**Tweet 5:**
Conviction structuring:

1. Thesis conviction = position size (not portfolio theory)
2. Thesis break = automatic exit (not hope for recovery)
3. Time in trade = until thesis converges to market consensus
4. Profit target = let it run until thesis is obvious, then exit

You're not trading the market. You're trading the gap between what you know and what the market prices.

When that gap closes, you're done.

## Post 3: r/macroeconomics — Supply Exhaustion & Rent Dynamics

**Title:** Why the Housing Narrative Doesn't Match the Data (And What Happens Next)

**Body:**

There's a fascinating divide in housing metrics right now, and it's worth understanding because it affects everything from inflation to asset valuations to policy.

**The Contradiction:**

The White House announces: "Rents at 4-year low."

The BLS (government's official rent measure) reports: "Rents at all-time high."

Same market. Opposite stories. Let me explain what's actually happening.

**The Data Breakdown:**

**New Lease Rents (Apartment List, cited by White House):**
- January 2026: $1,353
- Down 6.2% from August 2022 peak
- Six consecutive monthly declines
- Covers: ~5-8% of renter population (new signers only)

**Renewal Rents (BLS Consumer Price Index):**
- December 2025: +2.9% year-over-year
- All-time high (440.667)
- Covers: ~92-95% of renter population (what most renters actually pay)

**Why They Diverge:**

Supply explosion in 2022-2024 (590K+ new units) hit the *new lease* market first. Landlords slashed asking rents on new leases to fill vacancies.

But renewals take 12-16 months to cycle. Most renters are locked in 2-year leases signed in 2024-2025 (when new lease rents were still elevated). They renew in 2025-2026 when new leases are lower, but their renewal offers are based on competitive market (new leases), not their old lease rate.

Result: 
- New renters get 6% off (supply effect)
- Existing renters get 2-3% raises (market level resets up on them)

The market is *redistributing* cost from new renters to existing ones. Aggregate rent is *not* falling.

**Why This Matters:**

1. **For inflation:** BLS is right. Consumer rent (what renters actually pay) is up. CPI rent is up. This is real inflation, not measurement artifact.

2. **For sentiment:** The narrative is wrong. "Rents falling" is superficially true for 5% of renters. False for 95%.

3. **For policy:** If housing was solved by 12 months of Trump policy, supply dynamics would flip by now. They haven't. Supply exhaustion is real.

4. **For trading:** Short side (XHB, IYR, REM) likely has more runway. The market is pricing relief that the data doesn't support.

**What Happens Next:**

By 2027-2028:
- New lease rents will stabilize around current levels (supply exhaustion)
- Renewal rents will stabilize around BLS levels (market reset)
- They'll converge, but at the BLS level, not the new lease level
- Investors holding for rent-driven returns will see slowing but not decline

The supply wave that drove 2022-2026 is over. We're about to find out if housing is actually "solved" or just differently distributed.

The data suggests it's the latter.
