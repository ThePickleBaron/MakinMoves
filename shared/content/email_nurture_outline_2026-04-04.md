## Email 2: The Divergence Detection Framework

**Subject:** How I found housing short (and how you can too)

**Hook:**
"Here's the exact framework I use to find divergences. It's not complicated. But it requires discipline."

**Body Structure:**
1. **The framework (step-by-step):**
   - Find the narrative (what everyone believes)
   - Find the data (what's actually happening)
   - Find the lag (how long before they converge)
   - Quantify the gap (9 points in housing, closing in 12-16 months)
   - Position for convergence (directionality + time)

2. **Worked example (housing):**
   - Narrative: "Rents falling, housing improving"
   - Data: "New leases down 6%, renewals up 2.9%"
   - Lag: 12-16 months (when new leases cycle to renewals)
   - Gap: 9 points
   - Position: Short homebuilders (they overshoot on supply improvement narrative)

3. **Why this works:**
   - It's not a prediction. It's math.
   - Gap cannot hold indefinitely
   - Time is your ally
   - Technicals will eventually confirm

4. **Common mistakes to avoid:**
   - Confusing narrative with data (they're often opposite)
   - Forgetting the lag (too early = pain, too late = missed move)
   - Wrong directionality (which way does gap close?)

**Call to action:**
"Find one divergence in your own research. Use this framework. I'll show you how to size it tomorrow."

**Tone:** Instructional, patient, using housing as case study

## Email 3: How to Size Conviction Trades

**Subject:** This is where most traders lose money

**Hook:**
"You've found a divergence. Now comes the part that separates rich traders from broke ones: How big?"

**Body Structure:**
1. **The problem:**
   - Portfolio theory says "allocate 5-10% to this theme"
   - Conviction theory says "allocate based on how sure you are"
   - Most traders follow portfolio theory, then conviction wavers under pressure

2. **Conviction levels (and sizing):**
   - Level 1 (Hypothesis): "I have some data" → 5-10% conviction allocation
   - Level 2 (Thesis): "Data is clear, thesis is solid" → 20-30%
   - Level 3 (Tested): "I've held this through volatility, thesis is intact" → 40-50%

3. **How to build conviction:**
   - Start small (5-10%, test the thesis)
   - If thesis holds through volatility, increase (20-30%)
   - If thesis survives countermoves, maximum size (40-50%)
   - If thesis breaks, exit immediately (don't average down)

4. **Why this works:**
   - You never overcommit to an untested thesis
   - You increase as evidence mounts
   - You're mentally prepared for each position size
   - You exit on thesis breaks, not emotion

5. **Real example:**
   - April: I'm hypothesis on housing short → 5% size
   - May: Data comes in supporting, thesis strengthens → 20% size
   - June: Thesis holds through Fed pivot, housing underperforms → 50% size
   - If August: BLS rents suddenly drop 3%, thesis breaks → 0% size

**Call to action:**

**Tone:** Practical, emphasizing patience and evidence-based conviction building

## Email 4: Trade Structuring 101

**Subject:** Options, shorting, and how not to blow up

**Hook:**
"You have conviction. You've sized appropriately. Now: How do you structure the actual trade? Here's what I do."

**Body Structure:**
1. **Three ways to play a bearish thesis:**
   - Short the underlying (highest conviction, highest risk)
   - Put spreads (moderate conviction, defined risk)
   - Call spreads (if you want to collect theta while waiting)

2. **My framework (housing short example):**
   - Thesis: Homebuilders overpriced on supply relief narrative
   - Conviction: Medium-high (data is clear, but macro is unpredictable)
   - Structure: Short call spreads on XHB (30% return on risk, 45 DTE, rolling monthly)
   - Why: Collect theta while thesis plays out, defined risk, don't overcommit

3. **Key principles:**
   - Match structure to conviction
   - Define risk before entry
   - Build in thesis-break level (NOT technical support)
   - Let time work for you

4. **Mistakes to avoid:**
   - Naked shorts (undefined risk, will destroy you)
   - Over-leveraged spreads (eat into capital too much)
   - Holding thesis breaks (discipline > hope)

5. **Example trade timeline:**
   - Jan: Short 3 XHB call spreads, roll monthly
   - Mar: Thesis intact, short more (conviction increased)
   - May: Thesis starts breaking? Exit cleanly
   - Or: Thesis intact, hold until convergence (housing reverts to higher rental levels)

**Call to action:**

   - All thesis-driven, all sized by conviction

2. **Example portfolio (conceptual):**

   - Theme 2: Rate complexity (thesis: Fed policy mispriced, 6-9 months)
   - Theme 3: EM rotation (thesis: dollar weakness + carry, 9-12 months)
   - Theme 4: Small-cap divergence (thesis: valuation gap closing, 3-6 months)
   - Theme 5: Commodity cycle (thesis: supply constraint + inflation, 12-18 months)

3. **Risk management across portfolio:**
   - No single thesis > 50% of conviction allocation
   - Diversify by time horizon (not all 12-month thesis)
   - Diversify by direction (some short, some long, some neutral)
   - All positions have OCO stops (thesis breaks = exit)

4. **Why this works:**
   - You're not betting on one call
   - Some theses will be wrong, others will be right
   - Different time horizons mean steady revenue (rolling thesis convergences)
   - Diversification reduces single-idea risk

5. **How I build it:**
   - Spend Sunday night analyzing 5 potential divergences
   - Pick the 3 with strongest data
   - Rank by conviction
   - Size accordingly
   - Execute Monday
   - Wait, review weekly, exit on thesis breaks
