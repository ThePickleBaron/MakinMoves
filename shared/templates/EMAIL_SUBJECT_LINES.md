# Email Subject Line Swipe File
**For:** Briefing, nurture sequences, marketing

---

## HIGH-PERFORMING PATTERNS

### Pattern 1: Curiosity Gap + Number
**Formula:** "[X thing] is [surprising direction] (it's [number]%)"

Examples:
- "The Fed just did the opposite of what traders expect (here's why)"
- "Shipping rates fell 45% — but insurance costs just went up"
- "Earnings are beating by 12% — yet equity selling accelerates"
- "Unemployment at 4.2% — yet wage pressure easing (divergence)"
- "Oil dropped 20% — refined products up 8% (basis divergence)"

**Why it works:** Poses contradiction, promises explanation

---

### Pattern 2: Institutional Positioning Hint
**Formula:** "[Institutions] are quietly [action] for [timeframe]"

Examples:
- "Wall Street is quietly buying EM currencies (here's the catalyst)"
- "Hedge funds just tripled their [sector] bets (next 6 weeks matter)"
- "Banks just repositioned their macro books (what I see coming)"
- "Insiders just loaded up on [company] (divergence signal)"
- "Central banks shifted their reserves (2-month timeline ahead)"

**Why it works:** Implies inside information, promises edge

---

### Pattern 3: Contrarian + Timeline
**Formula:** "[Consensus] is wrong about [timeline]. Here's what breaks first."

Examples:
- "The market's wrong about Fed timing. Here's the real schedule."
- "Earnings recovery is 4 months away, not 2. Here's why."
- "Recession odds are rising faster than bonds reflect. 6-week view."
- "Tech earnings divergence unfolds over next 45 days. Here's the pattern."
- "Supply normalization delays another quarter. Here's the cascade."

**Why it works:** Positions you as contrarian analyst, promises specific timing

---

### Pattern 4: Second-Order Effects Teaser
**Formula:** "[Event] happened, [consensus reaction], but [second-order effect]"

Examples:
- "Suez cleared, rates crashed, but insurance repricing hits in 6 weeks"
- "Fed pivoted dovish, stocks up, but here's what unfolds next"
- "Tech earnings missed, stocks hammered, but opportunity in Y"
- "Energy supplies surge, prices crash, but storage dynamics matter"
- "Dollar weakens, but that triggers something worse (for someone)"

**Why it works:** Teases unique insight into cascades

---

### Pattern 5: Specific Data Point
**Formula:** "[Specific metric] just [moved/changed]. [Why this matters]."

Examples:
- "Basis curve just inverted. Here's what I'm watching next."
- "Options market just repriced earnings risk 15% higher"
- "Shipping futures just signaled something the equity market missed"
- "Credit spreads gapped down 35bps. That's a 3-year signal."
- "Crypto liquidations hit $2B last week. Here's the cascade."

**Why it works:** Specificity = credibility, promises actionability

---

### Pattern 6: Risk/Opportunity Polarization
**Formula:** "[Market believes] safe, but [actual] risk is [direction]"

Examples:
- "Everyone thinks this is safe. It's actually [risk] risk."
- "This looks risky, but the real danger is [elsewhere]"
- "Most traders are hedging the wrong tail"
- "The squeeze isn't where consensus thinks it is"
- "Max pain isn't the risk — [X] is"

**Why it works:** Contrarian, positions you as risk identifier

## WHAT WORKS FOR THIS AUDIENCE

✅ **Specific** (numbers, timeframes, details)  
✅ **Contrarian** (opposite of consensus)  
✅ **Macro-focused** (second-order effects, divergence)  
✅ **Urgency** (timeliness, catalyst windows)  
✅ **Insider-positioning hint** (what institutions do)  
❌ **Generic** ("You won't believe")  
❌ **All-caps SCREAMING**  
❌ **Emoji spam**  
❌ **Vague promises** ("secrets revealed")  
❌ **Hyperbole** ("make $10K in one trade")

## MOBILE OPTIMIZATION

**Character count:** 
- 40 chars = preview cutoff on mobile
- 60 chars = full on desktop
- 50 char sweet spot (works everywhere)

**Test subject lines:**
1. Short punchy (40 chars)
2. Medium specific (50 chars)
3. Longer detailed (60 chars)

Example (50 char)
"Institutions loading EM for what's coming (timeline)"

**Best send times for macro audience:**
- 8 AM ET (morning briefing, traders reviewing overnight)
- 5 PM ET (end-of-day thesis review, position planning)

**Test each pattern at different times:**
