# Publishing Strategy: No Domain Yet

**Researched by:** Cowork | April 2, 2026
**Problem:** We have affiliate article outlines ready but no domain or WordPress site. What's the fastest path to publishing?

---

## Recommendation: Start on Substack or Hashnode, NOT Medium

### Why NOT Medium (Despite Being the Obvious Choice)

1. **Medium doesn't let you connect to your own domain** — you can't use a subdirectory or root domain. Your SEO juice stays on medium.com, not your brand.
2. **Medium's algorithm controls distribution** — your articles may get buried or boosted unpredictably.
3. **Medium paywall conflicts with affiliate content** — paywalled articles can't have affiliate links (TOS issue). Free articles get less algorithmic push.
4. **Migration is painful** — when you eventually get a domain, moving from Medium loses all your Google ranking signals.

### Better Option A: Substack (Free, Immediate, Email List Built-in)

**Why Substack wins for our use case:**
- Free to publish, no domain needed
- Built-in email newsletter (this IS our email list — no separate Beehiiv setup needed)
- Substack has its own discovery network (substack.com/discover)
- Affiliate links are allowed in free posts
- Custom domain can be added later (seamless migration)
- Google indexes Substack posts well
- Fits the "active trader sharing insights" personal brand

**Setup:** 15 minutes. Jason creates a Substack at substack.com, picks a name like "The Active Trader" or "Portfolio Engineering" — publish Article 2 first (the personal story, which fits Substack's essay format perfectly).

### Better Option B: Hashnode (Free, Dev-Friendly, Domain Later)

**Why Hashnode could work:**
- Free hosting, custom domain supported from day one
- Strong SEO (canonical URLs, sitemap, clean structure)
- Markdown native (easy for Claude Code to produce content)
- Community of technical/engineering readers (matches Jason's background)
- Less competition in finance niche on Hashnode

**Downside:** Smaller audience than Substack. Better for the "engineering + trading" angle.

### Option C: Simple HTML Site on GitHub Pages or Netlify (Free, Owned)

- Claude Code can build a simple static blog in 30 minutes
- Host free on GitHub Pages or Netlify
- Buy domain later and point it
- Full SEO control from day one
- Downside: No built-in audience, no email list without additional tools

---

## Recommended Approach: Hybrid

1. **Week 1-2:** Publish Article 2 ("How I Track a $250K Portfolio") on Substack. This is the personal story — perfect for newsletter format. Include CTA to the Notion template on Gumroad.

2. **Week 2-3:** Publish Article 1 ("Best Portfolio Tracking Tools 2026") on a simple static site (GitHub Pages or Netlify). This is the SEO play — comparison articles need to be on an owned domain for long-term ranking. Claude Code can build the site skeleton in one sprint.

3. **Week 3-4:** Publish Article 3 ("Notion vs Excel vs TradingView") on Substack AND cross-post to the static site.

4. **Month 2:** Buy a proper domain, migrate the static site, set up WordPress or keep static. Substack continues as the email/newsletter channel.

**This way:** Substack handles audience-building and email from day one. The static site builds SEO authority. Both funnel to Gumroad.

## Affiliate Program Application Strategy

Most affiliate programs want to see a live website. Our workaround:

1. Create the Substack first (takes 15 min)
2. Publish Article 2 (the personal story)
3. Apply to affiliate programs using the Substack URL + Gumroad storefront as proof of online presence
4. Most programs approve within 1-7 days

**Apply to TradingView and Sharesight first** — they have the highest commissions and are most likely to approve a Substack-based creator.
