# Competitor Analysis: Notion Portfolio Tracker Templates

**Researched by:** Cowork | April 2, 2026
**Purpose:** Understand the competitive landscape before launching our Active Trader Portfolio Tracker

---

## Market Overview

The Notion investment/portfolio tracker template market is **active but not oversaturated**. Most competitors target casual investors or crypto holders. Very few specifically target active stock traders managing multiple brokerage accounts. This is our gap.

## Key Competitors on Gumroad

### 1. Hubbub — Notion Investment Portfolio Template
- **URL:** hubbub.gumroad.com
- **Key feature:** Live updating stock prices (free, no limitations on # of stocks or frequency)
- **Tracks:** Stocks, crypto, ETFs
- **Includes:** Portfolio holdings, profit/loss returns, permanent transaction log
- **Differentiator:** Step-by-step guide for live price updates
- **Our edge:** They don't target active traders specifically. No thesis notes, no multi-account views, no realized gains log for taxes.

### 2. Cullerful — Investment Portfolio Tracker
- **URL:** cullerful.gumroad.com
- **Key feature:** Track current positions across stocks, crypto, ETFs, NFTs
- **Differentiator:** Multi-asset class support
- **Our edge:** Broader but shallower. Designed for hodlers, not active traders. No trade journal, no sector allocation, no tax tracking.

### 3. Easlo — Investment Tracker
- **URL:** easlo.gumroad.com
- **Key feature:** Dashboard for tracking investments across multiple accounts (brokerage to crypto wallets)
- **Differentiator:** Multi-account tracking
- **Note:** Easlo is a well-known Notion template creator with a large audience
- **Our edge:** Similar multi-account concept, but ours is modeled on a REAL active trader's workflow (not a designer's guess). Thesis notes and realized gains log are differentiated features.

### 4. Applo — Stock Investment Journal and Portfolio
- **URL:** applo.gumroad.com
- **Key feature:** Track all investments in one place, insights on performance, returns, risk, diversification
- **Differentiator:** Includes journaling component
- **Our edge:** Closest competitor in concept. We need to ensure our trade journal/thesis notes are MORE practical (tied to positions, not standalone entries).

### 5. WizardNotion — Stock Portfolio Tracker
- **URL:** wizardnotion.gumroad.com
- **Key feature:** Track stock investments, monitor returns and profits, all-in-one dashboard
- **Our edge:** Generic stock tracker. Doesn't address the multi-account problem or thesis documentation.

### 6. AMIIC Wealth — Stocks Portfolio Tracker
- **URL:** Notion Marketplace + Gumroad
- **Key feature:** Analyze, track, grow investments
- **Our edge:** Branded as wealth management, not active trading. Different audience.

## Notion Marketplace

### 10. Automated Investment Portfolio Tracker
- **Key feature:** Uses Notion API Connector to sync stock & crypto prices from multiple finance APIs
- **Differentiator:** Automated price updates (this is significant)
- **Our edge:** Our template is simpler to set up (no API configuration needed). For active traders who update on trades (not real-time), manual entry is fine. Simplicity IS a feature.

## Pricing Intelligence

Most Notion investment templates on Gumroad range from **$0 (lead magnets) to $29**. The sweet spot appears to be **$9-$19** for templates with meaningful features. Premium templates with automation (API integration) command $19-$29.

**Our $9 launch price** is:
- At the LOW end of paid templates (good for velocity)
- Undercuts most featured competitors
- Leaves room to raise to $14.99 after reviews
- Competitive even against free templates because ours is purpose-built for active traders

## Strategic Recommendation

Our biggest differentiation is **authenticity + specificity**. Most competitors are built by Notion template designers who don't trade. Ours is built by someone who manages $250K across multiple accounts. Lean into this in ALL copy:
- "Built by a real active trader"
- "Modeled on an actual $250K multi-account portfolio"
- "Not designed by a template shop — designed by someone who uses this daily"
