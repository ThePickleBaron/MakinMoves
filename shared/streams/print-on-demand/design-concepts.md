## Design 1: "My Portfolio is Diversified" (But It's All Tech)

**Text:** MY PORTFOLIO IS DIVERSIFIED
**Subtext (smaller):** *narrator: it was not diversified*

**Layout:** Clean sans-serif font. "MY PORTFOLIO IS DIVERSIFIED" in bold white on dark background. Subtext in italic, smaller, offset below in a lighter gray.

**Color scheme:** Dark navy or black tee. White and gray text.

**Target buyer:** Active traders and investors who know the joke — everyone says they're diversified but they're 60% tech stocks. Self-deprecating humor for the r/wallstreetbets / r/investing crowd.

**Why it sells:** Identity humor. Every trader has been guilty of this. Shareable on social media. Works as a gift for a trading friend.

**Product types:** T-shirt, hoodie, mug

## Design 2: "Cost Basis: Don't Ask"

**Text:** COST BASIS
**Subtext:** DON'T ASK

**Layout:** Minimalist. "COST BASIS" in large bold serif font. "DON'T ASK" in smaller text below, slightly offset. Clean lines, no imagery — just typography.

**Color scheme:** Black tee with white text. OR white tee with black text.

**Target buyer:** Traders who've averaged down (or up) into a position they'd rather not talk about. Universal trader experience.

**Why it sells:** Relatable pain point turned into humor. Conversation starter. Low-key enough to wear outside of trading circles without explanation needed.

**Product types:** T-shirt, sticker, mug

## Design 3: "Engineer by Day, Trader by Night"

**Text:** ENGINEER BY DAY
**Subtext:** TRADER BY NIGHT

**Layout:** Two-line bold sans-serif. "ENGINEER BY DAY" in a professional, structured font. "TRADER BY NIGHT" in the same font but slightly more aggressive (could use a subtle chart line or candlestick as an underline accent).

**Color scheme:** Dark gray or charcoal tee. White text with a subtle green accent on "TRADER."

**Target buyer:** Engineers, developers, and technical professionals who trade actively. This is Jason's exact demographic — and it's underserved in the PoD market.

**Why it sells:** Dual-identity shirts sell well when both identities have passionate communities. Engineers + traders = specific enough to feel personal, broad enough to have volume.

**Product types:** T-shirt, hoodie

## Design 4: "I Read the 10-K"

**Text:** I READ THE 10-K

**Layout:** Bold, clean, single line. No imagery. Pure typography statement.

**Color scheme:** Navy blue or forest green tee. White text.

**Target buyer:** Fundamental investors who do real research (not meme traders). The kind of person who actually reads SEC filings before buying a stock. It's a flex, but a nerdy one.

**Why it sells:** Status signal for the "I do my homework" crowd. Works as a conversation starter in finance circles. Pairs well with the Stock Rover / Seeking Alpha audience.

**Product types:** T-shirt, mug, sticker

## Design 5: "Thesis: Buy and Hold Until Wrong"

**Text:** THESIS:
**Subtext:** BUY AND HOLD UNTIL WRONG

**Layout:** "THESIS:" in smaller text above, like a label. "BUY AND HOLD UNTIL WRONG" in larger, bolder text below. Minimalist design — could include a small red/green candlestick icon to the left of "THESIS:" as a subtle visual anchor.

**Color scheme:** Black tee, white text, small red/green accent on the candlestick icon.

**Target buyer:** Thesis-based traders who understand that conviction investing isn't "buy and forget" — it's "buy and hold as long as the thesis is intact." This aligns directly with our Notion template's thesis notes feature.

**Why it sells:** It's a philosophy statement, not just a joke. Appeals to serious traders. Cross-promotes the portfolio tracker concept (thesis-driven investing). Could include a tiny URL or QR code to the Gumroad listing on the back (if Merch allows it — check TOS).

**Product types:** T-shirt, hoodie

## Production Notes

**Canva setup:**
- Use Canva's t-shirt mockup templates for preview images
- Keep designs at 4500 x 5400px (Merch by Amazon standard)
- Stick to 2-3 colors max per design (reduces print costs, looks cleaner)
- Save as PNG with transparent background

**Merch by Amazon specifics:**
- Tier 10 (new accounts): can upload 10 designs
- Start with 5 designs, test which get traction, then expand
- Price at $19.99 for standard tees (royalty ~$5-7 per sale)
- Title and bullet points should include keywords: trader, investor, stock market, portfolio, finance humor
- Don't use trademarked terms (no specific broker names, no "Wall Street Bets")

**Testing strategy:**
- Upload all 5 designs
- Run for 2 weeks with no promotion
- Check impressions and sales
- Double down on winners with color variants
- Kill designs with zero impressions after 30 days
