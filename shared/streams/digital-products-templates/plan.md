## PART 1: FOUNDATION (Days 1–3)

### Step 1: Choose Your Niche
**Why:** Generic templates fail. Specificity = sales.

**Process (1 hour):**
1. List 5 problems YOU understand or have personal experience with:
   - Example: Solopreneur project management, freelancer invoicing, content creator planning, course creator templates
2. For each, ask: "Would I pay $10–$30 to solve this?"
3. Pick the ONE with highest personal relevance (you can build/iterate it faster)

**Good niches (high buyer intent):**
- SaaS founder project management
- Freelancer financial tracking
- Course creator curriculum planning
- Notion lead tracker for sales teams
- Content creator posting schedule + analytics

**Bad niches (too broad):**
- General productivity template
- Social media template
- Business planning template

**Research validation (30 min):**
- Search "[your niche] Notion template" on Google and Gumroad
- If you see 3+ existing products charging $15+, niche has demand
- Check YouTube: "[niche] template tutorial" — if 10K+ views exist, market exists

**Decision outcome:** Write your niche choice in `_niche-choice.txt` (for next session reference)

### Step 2: Understand Your Platforms

**Gumroad (Recommended for starting):**
- Best for: Passive discovery + direct customer relationships
- Fees: 10% + payment processor (2.8% + $0.30)
- Effective cost: ~13% of sale
- Advantage: Email list building + repeat customer messaging
- Time to set up: 10 min
- Action: Go to gumroad.com, create free account with email

**Etsy (Secondary, for templates):**
- Best for: Canva templates, printables, high traffic
- Fees: $0.20 per listing + 6.5% transaction fee
- Effective cost: ~7–8% of sale (better margins)
- Advantage: Massive existing traffic, algorithm favors new listings
- Time to set up: 20 min
- Action: Go to etsy.com, create seller account

**Notion Marketplace (Tier 3):**
- Best for: Notion-specific templates only
- Fees: Free, flat $20 listing fee
- Advantage: Lowest fees; niche audience
- Disadvantage: Limited audience vs. Gumroad/Etsy
- Time to set up: 15 min
- Action: Create Notion account if don't have

**Setup time (all three):** <45 min total

### Step 3: Set Up Your Notion Template (First 2 Hours)

**Goal:** Create a "good enough" first template MVP by end of Day 1

**Tools Needed (Free):**
- Notion.com account
- Canva.com account (free tier fine)
- Screenshots/screen recording (Gyazo, built-in)

**Process:**

#### Option A: Notion Template (Recommended for starting)
1. Create a new Notion workspace (or use existing if you have one)
2. Design your template around the problem. Example structure for "SaaS Founder Project Tracker":
   - Dashboard (overview of projects, deadlines, revenue impact)
   - Projects database (title, description, status, deadline, owner)
   - Tasks database (linked to project, priority, assignee, due date)
   - Timeline view (Gantt-style overview)
   - Sales tracker (linked to projects, monthly revenue)

3. Add example data (fill in 3–5 sample projects so buyers can see how it works)
4. Design for clarity (lots of whitespace, clear instructions in each section)
5. Add a README page at top: "How to use this template" (3–5 bullet points)

**Time estimate:** 2–4 hours if you're new to Notion; 1 hour if experienced

**Quality bar:** Template should be usable immediately; buyer shouldn't need to debug structure

#### Option B: Canva Template (Easier, if doing printables/graphics)
1. Go to canva.com, create free account
2. Choose template type (social media post, presentation, planner page, etc.)
3. Customize colors, fonts, text for your niche (e.g., "Freelancer Invoice Template")
4. Save as template (Canva allows this)
5. Download as PDF + editable file

**Time estimate:** 1–2 hours

**Quality bar:** Professional-looking, colors match niche, text is placeholder-clear

### Step 4: Create Listing Copy (1 Hour)

**Gumroad product page requires:**
1. **Product name:** Clear, benefit-driven
   - Bad: "Project Template"
   - Good: "SaaS Founder Project & Revenue Tracker (Notion)"

2. **Description (300–500 words):**
   - What is it? (1 sentence)
   - What problem does it solve? (2–3 sentences)
   - What's included? (bullet list)
   - How to use it? (3–5 steps)
   - Who is it for? (1 sentence)
   - Example:
     ```
     SaaS Founder Project & Revenue Tracker (Notion Template)

     Overwhelmed juggling multiple projects, tracking deadlines, and forecasting revenue?
     This Notion template gives you a single source of truth for all your SaaS projects, tasks, and monthly revenue tracking.

     What's included:
     - Project management dashboard
     - Task tracker with priority levels
     - Monthly revenue tracker (linked to projects)
     - Timeline view for deadline visualization
     - Fully editable, rename sections as you like

     Perfect for: Solo SaaS founders, bootstrapped product builders, indie hackers managing multiple projects

     How to use:
     1. Duplicate the template into your own Notion workspace
     2. Rename projects to match yours
     3. Add your tasks and track progress
     4. Update revenue tracker monthly
     5. Use timeline view for planning sprints
     ```

3. **Price:** Start with $15–$25 (test price, can raise after first 5 sales)

4. **Preview image:** Take a clean screenshot of the template (1–2 key views)
   - Use Canva to add arrows/highlights if needed
   - File: 1200x600px min

**Template for copy:** Save this structure in Google Docs for future templates (copy-paste-adjust)

## PART 2: LAUNCH (Days 4–7)

### Step 5: Publish to Gumroad (30 min)

1. Go to gumroad.com > Your profile > Create product
2. Fill in:
   - Product name
   - Description (from Step 4)
   - Price: $15–$25
   - Preview image (screenshot from Step 3)
   - File: Upload your Notion template (as PDF or shared link)
3. Set to "Published"
4. Copy product link
5. Set up email collection (Gumroad does this by default; good)

**Action:** Publish by end of Day 5

### Step 6: Publish to Etsy (if doing Canva templates) (45 min)

1. Create new listing on Etsy

**Note:** Etsy charges $0.20 per listing. Start with 1 listing; if it gets views, expand.

## PART 3: ITERATION (Weeks 2+)

### Week 2–4: Optimize Based on Data

**Metrics to track (Gumroad shows these automatically):**
- Views: How many people visited your product page?
- Conversion rate: Views → Sales (aim for 2%+ over time)
- Customers: How many bought?
- Revenue: Total $

**If no sales in first 2 weeks:**
1. Check if problem is positioning (niche is real?) or marketing (no one knows it exists?)
2. Try rephrasing product description + title (test 2–3 versions)
3. Post in 2–3 more communities
4. Ask first buyer (when you get one) why they bought (use Gumroad message feature)

**If getting sales (3+):**
1. Great! Now optimize:
   - Raise price by $5 (test demand ceiling)
   - Create second template in similar niche (expand revenue)
   - Start email list (for repeat buyers)

## PART 8: COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Generic templates:** "Productivity template" fails. "SaaS founder revenue tracker" wins.
2. **Too polished first draft:** Don't spend 20 hours perfecting. Launch MVP, iterate.
3. **Wrong price:** Too low = undervalues work; too high = no sales. Start $15–$25, test up.
4. **No marketing:** Building is 20% of work. Marketing is 80%. Post in communities, get visible.
5. **Ignoring customer feedback:** First buyer questions = next template ideas.
6. **Relying on one platform:** Gumroad + Etsy + Notion = insurance against algorithm changes.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need design skills?**
A: No. Canva + Notion templates don't require graphic design (Canva does the heavy lifting). Clear structure > flashy design.

**Q: How long to first sale?**
A: 2–8 weeks if niche is real and you market consistently. Most first sales come from Reddit/community posts.

**Q: What if no one buys?**
A: Niche is likely too broad or marketing isn't reaching right audience. Pivot to narrower problem or test in different communities.

**Q: Can I raise the price later?**
A: Yes. Start $15–$25, raise by $5 after first 5 sales if conversion is healthy (2%+).

**Q: Should I focus on Notion or Canva?**
A: Notion templates currently have higher perceived value. Canva printables easier to create. Pick based on your niche and skills.
