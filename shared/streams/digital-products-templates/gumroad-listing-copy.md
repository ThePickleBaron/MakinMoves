## Full Description

---

**Tired of spreadsheet chaos across multiple brokerage accounts?**

If you're actively trading and managing positions across an individual account, IRA, and maybe a third — you know the pain. Fidelity shows you one view. Schwab shows another. Your spreadsheet is 47 tabs deep and the formulas broke last Tuesday.

This Notion template gives you **one place to track everything.**

### What's Included

**Positions Database**
- Symbol, quantity, cost basis, current price, gain/loss (auto-calculated)
- Sector tags, dividend yield, dividend pay dates
- Thesis notes per position — why you bought, what your exit looks like
- Stop loss and target price fields
- Tags: Momentum, Value, Dividend, Hedge, Speculative, Scalp

**Realized Trades Log**
- Track every closed position with proceeds, cost basis, gain/loss
- Short-term vs long-term classification
- Wash sale tracking (yes, really)
- YTD P&L summary view

**Watchlist**
- Target entry prices with auto-calculated gap to current price
- Thesis and catalyst notes
- Priority ranking (Hot, Warm, Cold)

**Dashboard**
- Account-level summaries (Individual, IRA, Total)
- Sector allocation view — see concentration risk at a glance
- Top 10 holdings
- Recent trade activity
- Macro indicator tracking (oil, gold, DXY, VIX, 10Y)

**Thesis Journal**
- Dated journal entries for your macro view and daily trade rationale
- Template included so each entry takes 30 seconds

### 7 Pre-Built Views
1. All Positions (sorted by portfolio weight)
2. By Sector (board view)
3. By Account (board view)
4. Winners & Losers (filtered tables)
5. Dividend Calendar
6. Watchlist — Near Entry
7. YTD Realized P&L

### Who This Is For
- Active traders managing multiple positions across multiple accounts
- Investors who want thesis documentation alongside their numbers
- Anyone who's outgrown a basic spreadsheet but doesn't want to pay $30/month for a portfolio app
- Traders who track dividends, wash sales, and sector allocation

### Who This Is NOT For
- Buy-and-hold investors with 3 index funds (you don't need this)
- Day traders doing 100+ trades/day (you need a broker API, not Notion)

### How It Works
1. Duplicate the template to your Notion workspace (one click)
2. Delete the sample data
3. Add your current positions
4. Update prices at market close each day (2-5 minutes)
5. Log trades when you close positions
6. The dashboard and views auto-calculate everything else

**Built by an active trader who manages a real portfolio across multiple accounts. This isn't a generic "stock tracker" — it's the system I actually use.**
