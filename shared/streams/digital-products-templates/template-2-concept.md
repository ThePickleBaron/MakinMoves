## Option A: Dividend Income Dashboard

**What it is:** A Notion template focused entirely on tracking dividend income — projected annual income, monthly payment calendar, yield on cost, DRIP tracking, and income goal progress.

**Target audience:** Dividend-focused investors, FIRE community, income-oriented retirees, DGI (dividend growth investing) enthusiasts.

**Why this could win:**
- Large and passionate community (r/dividends has 300K+ members, r/FIRE is huge)
- Different buyer than Template #1 — expands our addressable market
- Higher price tolerance — dividend investors tend to be older, higher income, willing to pay for tools
- Natural cross-sell: "Got the portfolio tracker? Add the dividend dashboard for the full income picture."
- Content angle: Article topic "How to Build a Dividend Income Dashboard in Notion" writes itself

**Why it might not:**
- Narrower use case than the portfolio tracker
- Sharesight already does dividend tracking well (competes with a funded SaaS)
- Requires more data entry (quarterly dividend amounts, ex-dates, payment dates)

**Pricing:** $9-$12

**Competitive landscape:** Few dedicated dividend Notion templates on Gumroad. Most portfolio trackers include basic dividend fields but don't make it the focus. Gap exists.

**Effort to build:** Medium (1 new database + dashboard page, leveraging skills from Template #1)

---

## Option B: Options Trade Tracker

**What it is:** A Notion template specifically for tracking options trades — contracts, strikes, expirations, premiums, Greeks (optional), assignment risk, and P&L by strategy (covered calls, puts, spreads, etc.).

**Target audience:** Options traders from beginner (covered calls, cash-secured puts) to intermediate (spreads, iron condors).

**Why this could win:**
- Underserved niche — almost NO good Notion options trackers exist
- Options traders are active, engaged, and willing to pay for tools
- Higher complexity = higher willingness to pay ($12-$19)
- r/options has 1M+ members
- Cross-sell with Template #1: "Track your stocks in the Portfolio Tracker, track your options here"

**Why it might not:**
- Requires deep options knowledge to build authentically (does Jason trade options?)
- More complex database schema (expiration dates, strike prices, contract multipliers)
- Smaller total market than stock/dividend investors
- If Jason doesn't trade options, authenticity gap hurts the "built by a real trader" brand

**Pricing:** $12-$19

**Competitive landscape:** Nearly empty. One or two basic options journals on Gumroad, none with the depth this would need. Massive gap.

**Effort to build:** High (complex schema, multiple strategy types, need to handle expiration logic)

---

## Option C: Watchlist & Research Journal (Standalone)

**What it is:** A standalone Notion template for tracking stock research — watchlists, thesis documentation, earnings calendars, sector analysis, and a research workflow. NOT a portfolio tracker — this is the "pre-buy" system.

**Target audience:** Investors who spend significant time researching before buying. Overlaps heavily with Template #1 buyers.

**Why this could win:**
- Directly extends Template #1 (which includes a basic watchlist)
- Low effort to build — we already have the watchlist database structure
- Easy cross-sell: "Use this for research, then move to the Portfolio Tracker when you buy"
- Could bundle with Template #1 at a discount ($14 for both)

**Why it might not:**
- Significant overlap with Template #1's watchlist feature — buyers may feel they already have this
- Lower perceived value as a standalone product
- Harder to differentiate in marketing

**Pricing:** $7-$9

**Competitive landscape:** Some overlap with generic "stock research" templates. Not as differentiated as Options A or B.

**Effort to build:** Low (reuse watchlist DB, add research workflow pages)

---

## Recommendation

**Build Option A (Dividend Income Dashboard) first.**

Rationale:
1. **Largest addressable audience** — dividend investors + FIRE community is massive and spending money
2. **Different buyer** — expands market beyond active traders to income-focused investors
3. **Natural cross-sell** — complements Template #1 without cannibalizing it
4. **Content pipeline** — feeds Article 4+ ("How to Track Dividend Income in Notion")
5. **Medium effort** — can be built in one sprint cycle using skills from Template #1
6. **Price tolerance** — dividend investors will pay $9-$12 without hesitation

**Defer Option B (Options Tracker)** until we confirm Jason trades options. If he does, it becomes the highest-value Template #3 due to the empty competitive landscape.

**Skip Option C** — too much overlap with Template #1. Not worth a separate product. Instead, enhance Template #1's watchlist in a future update.
