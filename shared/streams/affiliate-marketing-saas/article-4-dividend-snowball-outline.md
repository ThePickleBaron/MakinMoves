### SECTION 1: THE MATH (250 words)
**"Why Dividends Compound"**

- **Example 1:** $10,000 in dividend stock, 3% yield
  - Year 1: $300 dividend
  - Reinvest $300 → New principal = $10,300
  - Year 2: $309 dividend
  - Vs. not reinvesting: $300 every year (flat)
  
- **Over 20 years:**
  - Reinvesting: $20,070 (107% growth from dividends alone)
  - Not reinvesting: $6,000 (30% growth)
  
- **The kicker:** Compound interest assumes consistent reinvestment and new contributions.

### SECTION 3: THE REINVESTMENT STRATEGY (350 words)
**"How to Actually Reinvest (Without Losing Your Mind)"**

**Option 1: Automatic Dividend Reinvestment (DRIP)**
- Set at your brokerage (Fidelity, Vanguard, Schwab, etc.)
- Dividends automatically buy fractional shares
- Pros: No effort, mathematically pure, tax-efficient (LTCG rates)
- Cons: You don't see the money (out of sight, out of mind)
- How: Log into broker → Holdings → Select stock → "Reinvest dividends" → Save

**Option 2: Manual Reinvestment**
- Dividend hits your cash account
- You manually buy more stock (or add to the position)
- Pros: Control + awareness (you see money moving)
- Cons: Effort + risk of spending it ("just this once")
- How: Same stock, or rotate to underweight sector

**Option 3: Dividend Ladder (Advanced)**
- Q1 dividend → Buy new stock A
- Q2 dividend → Buy new stock B
- Q3 dividend → Buy new stock C
- Q4 dividend → Buy new stock D
- Pros: Forces discipline, diversifies entry prices
- Cons: More complex tracking

**My approach:** DRIP on large holdings + manual for experimental positions.

### SECTION 4: THE TAX ANGLE (200 words)
**"Taxes + Wash Sales Don't Kill Dividends"**

- **Qualified dividends:** Taxed at LTCG rate (0%, 15%, or 20%) — much better than income tax
- **Non-qualified dividends:** Taxed as ordinary income (avoid these)
- **Wash sales:** Rules about selling at a loss + buying back within 30 days
  - Example: Sell Microsoft at a loss, buy back 15 days later = wash sale
  - Result: Loss disallowed, holding period restarts
  - Workaround: Use Sharesight to flag wash sales before you trade

- **DRIP + taxes:** DRIP purchases count as new buys (different cost basis). Good for tax planning.
