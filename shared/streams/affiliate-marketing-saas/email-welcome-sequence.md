## Email 3: Story + Cross-Sell (Day 7)

**Subject line:** The trade that taught me to keep a thesis journal

**Body:**

Last year I held a position through a 22% drawdown because I "just knew" it would come back.

It didn't. I sold at the bottom and watched it recover two weeks later — without me.

The problem wasn't the market. The problem was that I had no written thesis. No exit plan. No stop level documented anywhere. I was making decisions based on gut feeling and memory, and both failed me when the pressure was on.

That's why I added thesis notes to every position in my portfolio tracker. Now before I enter a trade, I write down:

- Why I'm buying
- My target price
- My stop loss
- What would change my mind

When a position is down 15% and my gut says "panic sell," I read the thesis instead. Half the time, nothing has changed and I hold with conviction. The other half, I realize the thesis broke and I exit with discipline — not emotion.

This one habit has saved me more money than any charting indicator or screening tool.

If you're not documenting your trades yet, start. A notebook works. A spreadsheet works. Or grab [the template I built with thesis notes baked in](GUMROAD LINK) — it's the system that made this a habit for me.

— [Name]

P.S. — I'm writing a full breakdown of the best portfolio tracking tools for active traders. If you want me to send it when it's ready, just stay subscribed. It's coming next week.
