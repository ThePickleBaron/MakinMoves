**Title:** Supply Chain Shock: Why the Market Is Pricing It Wrong

---

But that's the headline. That's the obvious.

Hormuz isn't just an oil chokepoint. It's a fertilizer, wheat, and equipment supply bottleneck. A 1–2 week closure cascades through agricultural supply chains. You know what follows?

Fertilizer shortage in North Africa + Middle East.

Then? Equipment replacement cycles accelerate (farmers who lost crop years now buy new equipment).

The market hasn't priced fertilizer shortage yet. The market hasn't priced equipment recovery yet.

That gap—between what's obvious and what's coming—is where you make money.

### SECOND-ORDER EFFECTS (250 words): The Cascade (Where Alpha Lives)

**The cascade unfolds like this:**

**Week 1–2:** Oil prices spike. Shipping costs rise. Obvious plays move.

**Week 3–4:** Fertilizer shipments delayed. Fertilizer prices in North Africa/Middle East spike. Regional agriculture margin-compressed.

**Month 2–3:** Agricultural yields suffer. Farmers who lose crop years (or face 30%+ margin compression) begin capital planning for new equipment (combines, tractors, irrigation).

**Month 4–6:** Equipment manufacturers (John Deere, AGCO, etc.) see order acceleration. Backlog increases. Prices firm. Margins expand.

**Who's undervalued?**

1. **Equipment manufacturers:** Stock prices haven't moved yet (market doesn't see the cascade). But order books are about to explode. Entry now, before consensus catches on.

2. **Agricultural input suppliers:** Short-term pain (fertilizer shortage hits margins). But long-term, as farmers replace equipment, these suppliers benefit from replacement-cycle demand.

3. **Shipping companies (longer horizon):** Rates firm if supply shortage persists 6+ months.

**The timing is critical:** Market usually prices this cascade 4–6 months after the trigger. By then, equipment manufacturers have moved 40–80% of their eventual move. You capture it now, while the chain is still incomplete.

**The conviction:** 8/10. Supply shocks always cascade. The only variable is speed (4–6 months historically) and magnitude (depends on geopolitical resolution).
