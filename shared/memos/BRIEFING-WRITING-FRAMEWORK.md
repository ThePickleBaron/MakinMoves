# BRIEFING WRITING FRAMEWORK
**Purpose:** Consistent, high-quality weekly briefings for Premium subscribers  
**Length:** 700–800 words per briefing  
**Frequency:** 1x/week (Tuesday morning)  
**Tone:** Same as article-6 macro thesis framework (specific, data-driven, contrarian)

---

## STRUCTURE (Follow This Every Week)

### SECTION 1: THE DIVERGENCE (100 words)
**What:** Open with the macro trigger Jason identified  
**How:** State clearly what institutions are pricing vs. what's actually happening

**Example opening:**
```
DIVERGENCE: Private Credit Stress vs. Soft Landing Pricing

The market is pricing a soft landing. But private credit—$2.8 trillion in loans held by non-bank lenders—is showing stress signals most traders miss.

Institutions are watching Fed rates. You're watching credit spreads widen, fund withdrawals accelerate, and collateral haircuts tighten. That's the divergence.
```

**Template:**
- Name the divergence (2–3 words)
- What the headline says (1–2 sentences)
- What's actually happening (1–2 sentences, contradict the headline)
- Closing: "That's the divergence."

---

### SECTION 2: FIRST-ORDER EFFECTS (150 words)
**What:** The immediate, obvious consequence (already mostly priced in)  
**How:** Answer "What physically must happen if the trigger is real?"

**Example:**
```
FIRST-ORDER EFFECTS: Credit Tightens Immediately

If private credit stress is real, here's what we see within days:

1. Non-bank lenders face increased redemptions (private credit funds have 30–60 day redemption windows)
2. To meet redemptions, lenders sell liquid assets (liquid leveraged loans, mortgage-backed securities)
3. Asset sales increase collateral haircuts across the system
4. Funding costs for all non-bank lenders rise (they compete for deposits/funding)
5. Lending standards tighten (less leverage offered to borrowers)

Market response (Days 1–3): Leveraged loan ETFs sell. High-yield bond spreads widen. Banks rally (deposit flows move from non-banks to banks). This is mostly priced within days.
```

**Template:**
- Scenario: "If [trigger] is real, here's what we see..."
- List 4–5 first-order effects (what MUST happen physically)
- Market response (obvious, priced quickly)
- Conclusion: "This is relatively obvious—most traders see it by day X"

---

### SECTION 3: THE CASCADE (250 words) ⭐ MOST IMPORTANT
**What:** The hidden second-order effects (what's NOT priced yet)  
**How:** Ask "Who profits? What breaks next? What's undervalued *before* the thesis plays out?"

**Example:**
```
THE CASCADE: Regional Banks Benefit, But Equipment Suppliers Get Crushed

Here's where the divergence creates your edge:

If private credit tightens AND traditional lending tightens in lockstep, companies need alternatives:

1. Smaller companies (can't access capital markets) turn to regional banks for credit
2. Regional banks' net interest margins widen (they can charge higher rates for the available risk)
3. Demand for credit from strong-balance-sheet companies increases → pricing power for banks

**But simultaneously:**

4. Equipment leasing companies (who lend to small manufacturers) face a liquidity crisis
5. Small manufacturers can't borrow to buy new equipment → equipment demand collapses
6. Equipment suppliers (CNH, AP, CAT) see capex orders disappear
7. These companies trade at trough valuations BEFORE the earnings collapse is visible

**The cascade chain:**
Private credit stress → Bank margin expansion + Equipment supplier earnings crunch → Equities repriced across 60–90 days

**Your window:** Days 3–14, before credit stress cascades to equipment suppliers and earnings guidance cuts.

**What's undervalued NOW (before the thesis unfolds):** Regional bank stocks (XRT, region-specific ETFs) trade at cheap multiples. Equipment suppliers (CNH, AP) haven't cut guidance yet but momentum is weak.
```

**Template:**
- Start: "Here's where the divergence creates your edge..."
- List 4–6 second-order effects (profit chains + pain chains)
- Draw the cascade visually: [Trigger] → [1st order] → [2nd order] → [Who wins/loses]
- Identify what's undervalued NOW (before market sees it)
- Define your window (days until market prices it)
- Specify which stocks/sectors look cheap *before* the thesis plays out

---

### SECTION 4: TIMELINE (100 words)
**What:** When does this unfold?  
**How:** Be specific about catalyst timing

**Example:**
```
TIMELINE: Earnings Guidance Cuts Drive the Repricing

**Days 1–7:** Credit stress spreads through non-bank lenders. Banks begin raising rates on new originations.

**Days 8–21:** Small manufacturers realize credit is unavailable/expensive. Equipment capex orders decline.

**Days 22–45:** Equipment suppliers issue Q2 earnings guidance cuts (early warning for Q2 results). Analysts downgrade.

**Days 45–90:** Cascade fully repriced. Regional banks repriced up, equipment suppliers repriced down.

Your exit: Sell half at 20%+ gain when equipment suppliers issue first guidance cut. Let conviction core ride to 45–60 day mark.
```

**Template:**
- Break timeline into 3–4 phases (days 1–7, 8–21, 22–45, 45–90)
- What happens in each phase
- When to watch for confirmation (key data points)
- When to exit (early scalps vs. conviction core)

---

### SECTION 5: WHAT YOU'RE WATCHING (100 words)
**What:** Weekly micro-signals that validate/invalidate the thesis  
**How:** Give Jason specific things to monitor before next briefing

**Example:**
```
WHAT YOU'RE WATCHING THIS WEEK

1. **Private credit fund redemptions** — Are they accelerating? (Check: BDC dividend cuts, fund reporting)
2. **Bank funding costs** — Are deposit costs rising? (Check: regional bank quarterly filings, CDs rates)
3. **Equipment order data** — Any early signals of capex pullback? (Check: manufacturing PMI, equipment lease defaults)
4. **Leverage loan ETF spreads** — Are they widening? (Check: JNK, HYLD daily prices)

If 3+ of these confirm this week → conviction rises. If none confirm → reassess the thesis next week.
```

**Template:**
- List 3–4 specific metrics to watch
- Where to find them (sources/data points)
- What would confirm vs. contradict the thesis
- Decision rule: If X signals align, conviction stays high

## TONE REQUIREMENTS (Match Article-6)

**DO:**
- ✅ Be specific (use real ticker symbols, real numbers, real companies)
- ✅ Show the causal chain (why something physically must happen)
- ✅ Identify what's undervalued *before* the thesis plays out
- ✅ Use contrarian framing ("most traders miss this", "that's your edge")
- ✅ Build conviction through logic, not enthusiasm

**DON'T:**
- ❌ Generic: "markets could go up or down"
- ❌ Hypothetical: "if this maybe happens..."
- ❌ Non-specific: "tech stocks will underperform"
- ❌ Overly bullish/bearish language
- ❌ Predictions without mechanism
