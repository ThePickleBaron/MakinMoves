## Key Takeaways

### 1. Base Case is Optimistic
CEO's model assumes:
- 4-week sales cycles (real: 8–12 weeks)
- Zero churn (real: 3–5% monthly)
- Single writer scales (real: burnout by Month 5)
- No pricing pressure (real: 5–10% discount to close)

**Result:** 46–76% overestimate of revenue and profit

### 2. Conservative Scenario is Realistic
Stress test model aligns with:
- B2B SaaS sales cycle benchmarks (Databox 2026)
- Retainer agency churn data (Focus Digital, Recurly 2026)
- Freelancer burnout research (Peak Freelance, Copy Hackers 2025)
- Market pricing trends (Clutch 2026, Upwork data)

**Result:** Achievable with disciplined execution

### 3. Writer Burnout is the Limiting Factor
Most risky assumption: Single writer scaling from 2 to 5 customers
- By Month 4: 80% capacity utilization (sustainable)
- By Month 5: 110%+ utilization (burnout incoming)
- By Month 6: 150%+ utilization (system failure)

**Solution:** Hire backup writer by Month 4 (non-negotiable cost of scale)

### 4. Financial Impact is Severe
If CEO's assumptions hold: $10,800 cumulative profit
If stress test is right: $2,636 cumulative profit
**Difference:** $8,164 (76% miss on profitability)

**CFO implication:** Plan for conservative; celebrate if base case hits
