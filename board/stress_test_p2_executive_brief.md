# Board Brief: P2 Freelance Writing Stress Test

**Date:** March 29, 2026
**For:** CEO, COO, CTMO, CFO, Board Chair
**Status:** Decision ready

---

## What Changed?

CEO's original model (delivered March 28):
- **Assumption:** 5 customers by Month 6 = $6,000 MRR
- **Basis:** Warm outreach, no churn, single writer scales

Stress test (March 29):
- **Finding:** Real-world data suggests 3–4 customers by Month 6 = $2,400–$3,600 MRR
- **Gap:** 40–60% below base case
- **Cause:** 3 realistic factors not in original model

---

## Three Critical Assumptions Challenged

### 1. Sales Cycle: 4 Weeks → 8–12 Weeks
**CEO assumption:** Discovery call to contract in 4 weeks

**Real data:**
- B2B services sales cycles average 3–4 months (per Databox 2026)
- 74.6% of B2B sales to new customers take 4+ months
- Writing services specifically: 8–12 weeks for cold/warm outreach

**Impact:** First customer closes Month 3 (not Month 2). Acquisition delayed 4–8 weeks across board.

**Month 6 result:** 3–4 customers instead of 5

---

### 2. Churn: 0% → 3–5%/Month
**CEO assumption:** All customers stay (no churn)

**Real data:**
- Retainer agencies experience 12–25% annual churn
- Early-stage services see 3–5% monthly (higher turnover risk)
- First 90 days = peak churn period

**Impact:** Each customer acquisition is net +0.95 to +0.97 (not +1.0)

**Month 6 result:** Loses 0.5 customer to churn; net = 4.5 instead of 5

---

### 3. Single Writer Burnout: Not Addressed → Critical Risk at 5 Customers
**CEO assumption:** One writer delivers 4–6 articles/month sustainably

**Real data:**
- Sustainable output: 2–3 articles/month without quality degradation
- Beyond 6 articles/month: Quality drops, burnout risk rises
- At 5 customers (14 articles/month): Requires 70+ hours/week (unsustainable)

**Impact:** By Month 5, need backup writer (adds 30% to COGS)

**Consequence:**
- Gross margin: 60% → 50% when backup hired
- Or: Take quality risk and lose customers to poor quality

---

## Financial Waterfall: Base vs. Downside

| Metric | Base Case | Downside | Variance |
|--------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Month 6 Customers** | 5 | 3–4 | –40% |
| **Month 6 MRR** | $6,000 | $2,400–$3,600 | –60% |
| **Month 6 Gross Profit** | $3,600 | $1,300–$1,800 | –50% |
| **Cumulative 6-mo Profit** | $10,300 | $2,600 | –75% |
| **Breakeven Timeline** | Month 3 | Month 5 | +2 months |
| **Runway Required** | 2–3 months | 5–6 months | +3 months |

---

## What This Means for Each Role

### For CEO (Strategy)
- **Base case is too optimistic.** Real timing is 8–12 weeks per customer, not 4 weeks.
- **Realistic Month 6: 3–4 customers (not 5).** Still validates the model, just slower.
- **Decision:** Proceed with conservative forecast or pivot to faster revenue stream (P1 digital products)?

### For COO (Execution)
- **Don't commit to 5 customers by Month 6.** Target 3–4 customers; celebrate if hit 5.
- **Plan for 5–6 month runway (not 2–3).** Ensure you have capacity/capital to sustain.
- **Hire backup writer by Month 4 (non-negotiable).** Quality is the limiting factor, not price.

### For CTMO (Tech/Ops)
- **Build QA systems NOW, before scaling.** By 6 articles/month, quality drops without process.
- **Hire backup writer by Month 4.** This is a people problem, not a technology problem.
- **Plan for 2-writer team by Month 5–6.** Single writer hits burnout wall.

### For CFO (Finance)
- **Allocate $10,000 runway (not $5,000).** Realistic timeline extends to Month 5–6 breakeven.
- **Monitor these metrics weekly:**
  - Sales cycle length (target: <12 weeks)
  - Churn rate (red flag: >5%/month)
  - Writer capacity (red flag: >100% utilization)
- **Pivot decision rule:** If MRR <$1,500 by Month 5, shutdown and redeploy capital.

## Recommended Path Forward

### Option 1: Proceed with Conservative Forecast (RECOMMENDED)
- Budget: $10,000 runway (5–6 months)
- Target: 3–4 customers by Month 6, $2,400+ MRR
- Hire backup writer by Month 4 (capacity insurance)
- Metrics: Sales cycle, churn, writer utilization (weekly monitoring)
- Decision point: If MRR <$1,500 by Month 5, pivot

**Pros:** Realistic, achievable, sustainable
**Cons:** Revenue growth slower than CEO hoped

### Option 2: Proceed with Base Case, Accept Downside Risk
- Budget: $5,000 runway (tight)
- Target: 5 customers by Month 6, $6,000 MRR (stretch goal)
- Bet on single writer scaling (risky)
- Risk: Quality collapse in Month 5, customer churn spiral

**Pros:** Potential for higher revenue if execution is perfect
**Cons:** High burnout/churn risk; no backup plan

### Option 3: Pivot to Digital Products (P1) Instead
- Current model: $1,200 MRR by Month 3, zero churn, passive
- P2 realistic model: $2,400 MRR by Month 6, 3–5% churn, active service
- **P1 is faster, more passive; P2 is slower, needs ops**

**Pros:** Faster path to first revenue
**Cons:** Lower ceiling long-term; misses service business opportunity

---

## Board Recommendation

**APPROVE P2 with conservative assumptions (Option 1):**

1. **Budget:** $10,000 (not $5,000)
2. **Forecast:** 3–4 customers, $2,400+ MRR by Month 6 (not 5 customers, $6,000)
3. **Staffing:** Hire backup writer by Month 4 (non-negotiable)
4. **Metrics:** Weekly monitoring of sales cycle, churn, writer utilization
5. **Pivot trigger:** If MRR <$1,500 by Month 5, shutdown and redeploy to P1

**Why:**
- Realistic timeline and assumptions
- Still validates service business model
- Manageable risk with clear pivot point
- Creates foundation for scaling (if it works, we have repeatable model + team)

**Timeline:**
- Week 1: Scout writers, warm outreach to first 20 prospects
- Month 3: First customer closes
- Month 4: Hire backup writer
- Month 5–6: Test 3–4 customer retention, profitability
- Month 7: Scaling decision (go aggressive or maintain 3–4 customers)
