# EXECUTION ADJUSTMENTS — Revised Cross-Sell Model
**For:** COO, CTMO
**From:** Board Analysis (CEO approved)
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Impact:** Month 2–3 priorities shift

---

## WHAT CHANGED IN STRATEGY

**Old Model (Optimistic):**
- Month 2: P1→P2 upsells drive $3K+ additional revenue
- Focus: Cross-sell optimization
- Timeline: $10K recovery by Month 6

**New Model (Realistic):**
- Month 2: P1→P2 upsells drive $200–800 additional revenue (not $3K+)
- Focus: Customer acquisition first, monetization optimization second
- Timeline: $10K recovery by Month 7–8

**Bottom line:** We're shifting from "depth monetization" to "breadth acquisition" for Month 1–3.

---

## EXECUTION IMPLICATIONS BY ROLE

### FOR COO (Operations)

#### Priority Shift: Acquisition > Conversion Optimization

**Old priority order:**
1. Launch P1 + optimize for upsell
2. Day 5: Start P2 warm email campaign
3. Week 2: Launch P3 affiliate + test embedded links

**New priority order:**
1. Launch P1 + focus on CUSTOMER ACQUISITION (500+ views, 10+ sales)
2. Day 10–15: Build email list to 50+ (not rushing to upsell)
3. Week 3: Start P2 organic pipeline (cold email, Reddit, cold outreach)
4. Week 4: Launch warm email upsell (now have audience to test)

**Rationale:** Can't optimize cross-sell on 10 customers. Need 50+ to have real conversion data.

---

#### Email Strategy Adjustment (Bottleneck #2 Fix)

**Old plan:** 3 launch emails + weekly nurture emails (too many, causes fatigue)

**New plan:**
1. **Launch sequence:** 2 emails (instead of 3)
   - Email 1: Day 0 (product announcement)
   - Email 2: Day 3 (reminder + social proof)
   - (Skip Day 5 reminder—causes fatigue)

2. **Ongoing frequency:** 1 email per week (not 2–3)
   - Wednesdays: Weekly digest (all P1 updates + wins + customer stories)
   - (Single email, not separate updates for each product)

3. **Separate list tiers:**
   - Tier 1: P1 customers (template buyers) → get P1 updates + testimonials
   - Tier 2: P2 leads (interested in freelance) → get P2 offer + case studies
   - (Stop sending everyone everything)

**Impact:** Email open rates stay at 30–35% (not collapse to 15–20% from fatigue)

---

#### P2 Organic Pipeline (New Month 2 Activity)

**We now need 2 acquisition channels for P2 (not just warm email):**

**Channel 1: Warm email (existing P1 customers)**
- Start: End of Week 2 (when email list is 50+)
- Frequency: Single email + 1 follow-up
- Expected: 1–3 meetings, 0–1 client by Week 4

**Channel 2: Cold email (new audiences)**
- Start: Week 1
- Target: Niche communities (Reddit, Twitter, LinkedIn)
- Message: "I help [niche] get clients. Here's how... [link to case study]"
- Expected: 2–5 meetings, 1–2 clients by Week 3–4

**Why parallel channels?**
- Warm email is slow (depends on P1 sales velocity)
- Cold email is fast (immediate launch, 2–5 leads/week)
- Together: Higher chance of landing 2–3 clients by end of Month 2

**Time allocation:**
- Week 1: 2 hours/day cold email research + outreach
- Week 2+: 1 hour/day maintenance + warm email sequences

### FOR CTMO (Technology)

#### P1 Product Focus: Quantity + Customer Capture (Not Optimization)

**Old: Build 1 perfect template, heavily promote**

**New: Build 2–3 good templates, capture emails systematically**

**Setup by Day 1:**
- P1A: AI prompts bundle ($24.99)
- P1B: Template bundle ($19.99)
- P1C: Workflow bundle ($29.99)

**Why 3 variants?**
- Test which resonates fastest (niche validation in real-time)
- Whichever sells 3+ copies first = double down
- Reduce risk of Month 1 = $0 revenue

**Platform setup:**
- Gumroad: Email capture MANDATORY (default, but confirm in config)
- Etsy: List all 3 variants (6 cents per variant = low cost to test)
- Notion Marketplace: Tier 2 (list 1–2 top variants after Week 2)

**Email funnel setup:**
- Gumroad thank-you email: "Thanks for buying! Here's bonus content..."
- Thank-you page: "Join 100+ template users in my free group" (link to email signup)
- Follow-up sequence: 2 emails (not 3) to avoid fatigue

---

#### P3 Affiliate Setup: Separate Blog Channel (Not Embedded)

**Old model: Embed affiliate links in templates**
**New model: Create dedicated affiliate blog**

**What changes:**
- Abandon embedded template links (converts at 0.1–0.5%, not worth the trust erosion)
- Launch separate blog: "AI Tools for [Niche]" (domain + WordPress)
- Content: 1 blog post per week (4–5 by Month 2, 12+ by Month 4)
- Affiliate links ONLY in blog (users expect affiliate content in blogs)

**Timeline:**
- Week 1: Domain + WordPress setup (1 hour)
- Week 2: 2 blog posts (4–6 hours)
- Week 3+: 1 blog post per week (3–4 hours)

**Expected revenue:**
- Month 1: $0 (waiting for Google crawl)
- Month 2: $10–50 (early organic traffic)
- Month 3: $50–150 (more articles ranking)
- Month 4: $150–300 (scaling)

**This is honest affiliate revenue, not spammy template monetization.**
