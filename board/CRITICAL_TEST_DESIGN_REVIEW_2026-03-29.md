---

## THE QUESTION

**Is the Apr 1-8 UTM attribution test (email vs. Twitter) actually valid for deciding which channel to prioritize?**

---

## THE ANSWER

**No. The test is flawed and will produce unreliable results.**

---

## 6 CRITICAL FLAWS

### Flaw 1: Sample Size (N=75) — CRITICAL
- 7-day test = 50-100 total visitors (expected)
- Too small to detect real channel differences (need N=200+ for 95% confidence)
- **Likely outcome:** Random noise, false conclusion

### Flaw 2: Launch Halo Effect — CRITICAL
- Apr 1 = abnormally high traffic (novelty + founder energy peak)
- Apr 2-8 = normalized traffic (founder energy drops)
- **Problem:** Can't separate launch noise from channel performance
- **Likely outcome:** Apr 1 spike makes both channels look good artificially

### Flaw 3: UTM Blind Spot (40% Unattributed) — CRITICAL
- UTM parameters only track intentional campaign codes
- 35-50% of traffic is organic/direct (no UTM, not tracked)
- **Problem:** 40% of revenue attribution is mystery
- **Likely outcome:** "Email won with 60% of tracked traffic" = false (email actually got ~30% of ALL traffic)

### Flaw 4: Confounding Variables (Effort Variation) — CRITICAL
- Week 1 (Apr 1-7): High effort (2-3 Twitter posts/day, dedicated email sequences)
- Week 2-4 (Apr 8+): Normal effort (1 post/day, sporadic emails)
- **Problem:** Can't separate channel quality from effort level
- **Likely outcome:** "Email won!" (actually, Jason had high energy during launch)

### Flaw 5: Missing Metric — Audience Growth — MEDIUM
- Test measures traffic only (week 1 snapshot)
- Doesn't measure list/follower growth (sustainability indicator)
- **Problem:** Email might win traffic but Twitter might build bigger audience
- **Likely outcome:** Measure week-1 winners, ignore month-1 sustainability

### Flaw 6: Product Quality Assumption — CRITICAL
- Test assumes product is good (unknown)
- If product doesn't convert, both channels will fail (not channel issue, product issue)
- **Problem:** Can't distinguish channel fit from product fit
- **Likely outcome:** "Email is broken!" (actually, product messaging is broken)

---

## WHAT THE TEST WILL SHOW (LIKELY)

Email: 30-40 visitors, 1-3 sales
Twitter: 15-25 visitors, 0-1 sales

**Apparent conclusion:** "Email is 2-3x better. Prioritize email."

---

## WHAT THE TEST ACTUALLY MEANS

**Very little.** Sample size too small. Launch halo dominates. Effort too high. Can't rule out product quality issue.

---

## WHAT WE SHOULD DO INSTEAD

### OPTION 1: EXTEND TEST (Recommended)
**Change Apr 1-8 to Apr 1-14 (add 1 week)**
- Increase sample size to N=200+ (statistically valid)
- Include normalized traffic (after launch halo fades)
- Measure email list growth + Twitter follower growth (sustainability)
- Cost: Minimal (same channels, same effort)
- **Confidence: 7/10 (much better than Apr 1-8)**

### OPTION 2: PRE-LAUNCH VALIDATION
**Before launch (Mar 30-Apr 1): Test product quality with 10-20 beta customers**
- Gate: Does product convert at >50% rate?
- Cost: 2 hours of outreach
- Then run Apr 1-14 channel test (with validated product)
- **Confidence: 7/10 (product validated + channel tested)**

### OPTION 3: CONTINUOUS MONTH-LONG TEST
**Apr 1-30: Measure channels weekly, not as snapshot**
- Week 1: Traffic attribution
- Week 2: Audience growth trajectory
- Week 3-4: Sustainable revenue per channel
- Sample size: N=500+ (very robust)
- **Confidence: 8/10 (most reliable, but slowest)**
