## Three Risks Identified

### Risk 1: Sales Cycle (Medium Risk)
**Assumption:** 1 customer/month from Month 2
**Reality:** 8–12 week cycle = first customer Month 3–4

**Impact:** 3–4 month acquisition delay
**Mitigation:** Pre-warm 20 prospects in Month 1; shorten to 8 weeks
**If it fails:** Extend timeline to Month 7–8 for proof-of-concept

---

### Risk 2: Churn (Medium Risk)
**Assumption:** 0% (no customer loss)
**Reality:** 3–5% monthly churn typical for early-stage services

**Impact:** By Month 6, loses 0.5–1 customer to churn
**Mitigation:** Build QA systems, monthly check-ins, satisfaction guarantee
**If it fails:** Each lost customer = $1,200/month revenue, compounds

---

### Risk 3: Writer Burnout (High Risk)
**Assumption:** 1 writer scales from 2 to 5 customers
**Reality:** Beyond 6 articles/month, quality degrades; burnout by Month 5

**Impact:** Must hire backup writer by Month 4 (adds $1,500–$2,000/month cost)
**Mitigation:** Scout writers Month 1; hire backup Month 4
**If it fails:** Quality crashes → customer churn cascade → revenue loss

**Severity:** This is the limiting factor. Everything else can be managed; writer burnout is the knife's edge.
