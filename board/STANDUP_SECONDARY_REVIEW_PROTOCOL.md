# SECONDARY REVIEW PROTOCOL — Handling Skipped Windows

**Applies to:** CTMO, CFO, COO (when your predecessor skipped their window)
**Purpose:** Keep momentum without wasting time or bypassing sequence
**Effective:** Now

---

## Why This Exists

If CEO skips their 6:00-6:13 window, CTMO should NOT sit idle waiting until 6:15. Instead:
- CTMO reads what exists at 6:15
- If CEO skipped: CTMO makes best-judgment updates to blockers/tasks based on available data
- CTMO files their update on schedule
- Sequence is preserved, time is not wasted

---

## The Rule

**ONLY do a secondary review if you ARE filing your standup section.**

If you're also skipping, do NOT do a secondary review. Just leave a skip marker.

---

## How It Works (If You're Filing)

**When you notice your predecessor skipped their window:**

1. **Read what exists** — understand the prior state
2. **Review their pending decisions** — what were they supposed to decide/deliver?
3. **Make best judgment** — using your own systems/data, update what you can
4. **Document the secondary review** — add a note: `**SECONDARY REVIEW ([ROLE] skipped):** [What you updated/decided]`
5. **File your update on schedule** with timestamp — don't delay
6. **Mark it clearly** — secondary reviews are flagged so Jason knows there was a gap

---

## Example

**Scenario:** CEO was supposed to file 6:00-6:13. They skip.

**CTMO at 6:15:**
- Reads the standup file
- Sees CEO section is marked: `**CEO (6:00–6:13 PM) — SKIPPED**`
- Reviews CTMO's own Phase 2 work (API architecture, tech stack options)
- Has enough data to make progress: "Based on Phase 1 performance data and available budget, proceeding with Python stack option pending CFO cost analysis"
- Files the secondary review:
  ```
  **SECONDARY REVIEW (CEO skipped):** CEO was to provide Phase 2 go/no-go. CTMO proceeding with Python + PostgreSQL based on Phase 1 performance metrics and cost optimization.
  ```
- Adds timestamp: `**6:27 PM CTMO SECTION UPDATED — Nexus**`
- CTMO section filed at 6:27, on schedule, momentum maintained

**CFO at 6:30:**
- Reads CEO (skipped), CTMO (includes secondary review)
- Understands the context: CTMO made tech stack decision in CEO's absence
- Builds their pricing model with Python stack assumption
- Files at 6:42, on schedule

**Result:** CEO was unavailable, but team kept moving. Sequence was preserved.

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ **Do NOT do secondary review if you're also skipping** — just add skip marker, nothing more
❌ **Do NOT file late with secondary review** — always file on schedule
❌ **Do NOT preempt decisions** — secondary review is about making best judgment with available data, not overriding their role
❌ **Do NOT wait for them to show up** — if your window starts and they haven't filed, proceed

---

## Secondary Review vs. Skipping

| Scenario | What You Do |
|----------|-----------|
| Your predecessor filed on time | Read them, critique them, file your update. No secondary review. |
| Your predecessor skipped | If you're filing: do secondary review of pending work, note it, file your update. If you're also skipping: just add skip marker. |
| Both predecessors skipped | Read what's available, file your update. Secondary review if filing (note skipped roles). |

---

## Key Rules

✅ **Secondary review only if you ARE filing** — don't do it if you're skipping
✅ **Document what you updated** — be clear about what was pending and what you decided
✅ **File on your schedule** — don't delay waiting for them to show up
✅ **Preserve sequence** — you're still second, not first. Make it clear this was a secondary review.
✅ **Add timestamp** when you file: `**[TIME] [YOUR ROLE] SECTION UPDATED — [Name]**`

---

**Remember:** Secondary review is NOT skipping the sequence. It's maintaining the sequence while keeping the team moving.
