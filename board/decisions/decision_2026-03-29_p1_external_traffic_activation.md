**Date:** 2026-03-29 9:05 PM

---

## DECISION SUMMARY

**We are activating P1 Gumroad digital products with a multi-channel external traffic strategy.**

**Activation priority order (sequenced):**
1. **Channel 1 (Week 1, Primary):** Email pre-launch sequence to founder/indie hacker audience
2. **Channel 2 (Week 1, Parallel):** Twitter seeding and daily content (founder visibility)
3. **Channel 3 (Week 2, Secondary):** Medium cross-posting (SEO + credibility)
4. **Channel 4 (Week 3, Experimental):** Reddit communities (low-cost, high-variance)
5. **Channel 5 (Week 2, Optional):** ProductHunt launch (if product quality validated)

---

## SUCCESS GATES & PIVOT TRIGGERS

**Week 1 (Apr 1-5) success gate:** ≥50 external visitors to P1 landing page
- If achieved: Proceed to Week 2 (all channels), optimize top-performing channel
- If missed: Emergency meeting Apr 5 to diagnose (email list size? Twitter reach? Product messaging?)

**Week 2 (Apr 6-12) success gate:** ≥10 visits/day from external channels, ≥1 sale
- If achieved: Proceed to Month 2 (scale top performer, maintain others)
- If missed: Switch to paid traffic (Google/Facebook ads) or pivot messaging

**Week 4 (Apr 20-30) success gate:** ≥500 cumulative external visitors, ≥$100 revenue
- If achieved: Lock winning channel (likely email), prepare Month 2 scaling
- If missed: Enter Contingency Scenario 1 (see CEO contingency playbook)

---

## RATIONALE

**Why multi-channel?** Risk mitigation — if email list is small, Twitter can carry load (and vice versa). No single-channel dependency.

**Why sequential?** Focus effort and capital. Week 1 = email + Twitter only (parallel, low cost). Week 2 = add Medium/Reddit (additive, still low cost). Week 3+ = test paid ads only if organic stalling.

**Why prioritize email?** Data shows 40-60% revenue from founder email lists (highest conversion rate, lowest CAC).
